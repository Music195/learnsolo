Chain Rule for Derivatives
Suppose $y = f(u)$ is differentiable as a function of $u$,
and $u = g(x)$ is differentiable as a function of $x$.
Then the composite function $y = f(g(x))$ is differentiable as a function of $x$, and:
$\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$
(Chain Rule)
$\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = \left\{ f(g(x)) \right\}'$,   $\displaystyle \frac{dy}{du} = f'(u) = $ $f'(g(x))$,   $\displaystyle \frac{du}{dx} = $ $g'(x)$
Therefore, the chain rule can also be written as:
$\left\{ f(g(x)) \right\}'$ $= $ $f'(g(x))$ $g'(x)$
Inverse Function Rule
$\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{\color{#e65100}{\frac{dx}{dy}}}$
