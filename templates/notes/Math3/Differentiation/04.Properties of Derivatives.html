Properties of Derivatives
If both $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ are differentiable, then:
  1. $\displaystyle \big\{ k f(x) \big\}' = $ $k f'(x)$    (where $k$ is a constant)
  2. $\displaystyle \big\{ f(x) + g(x) \big\}' = $ $f'(x) + g'(x)$
  3. $\displaystyle \big\{ f(x) - g(x) \big\}' = $ $f'(x) - g'(x)$
  4. $\displaystyle \big\{ f(x) g(x) \big\}' = $ $f'(x)g(x) + f(x)g'(x)$
These properties allow you to differentiate linear combinations and products of functions easily.
