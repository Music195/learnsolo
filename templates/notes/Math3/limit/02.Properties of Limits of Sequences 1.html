Properties of Limits of Sequences 1
Suppose the sequences $\{a_n\}$ and $\{b_n\}$ both converge, with
$\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \alpha$,   $\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} b_n = \beta$.
1 $\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} k a_n$ = $k\alpha$ where $k$ is a constant
2 $\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} (a_n + b_n)$ = $\alpha + \beta$
$\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} (a_n - b_n)$ = $\alpha - \beta$
3 $\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n b_n$ = $\alpha\beta$
4 $\displaystyle \lim_{n \to \infty} \dfrac{a_n}{b_n}$ = $\dfrac{\alpha}{\beta}$ provided $\beta \neq 0$
