Euclidean Algorithm

Core Concept: Euclidean Algorithm

The Euclidean Algorithm is an efficient method for finding the greatest common divisor (GCD) of two integers. It works on the principle that the GCD of two numbers doesn't change if you replace the larger number with the remainder of dividing the larger by the smaller.

Euclidean Algorithm Steps

To find GCD(a, b) where a > b:

  1. Divide a by b to get quotient q and remainder r: a = bq + r
  2. If r = 0, then GCD(a, b) = b
  3. If r ≠ 0, then GCD(a, b) = GCD(b, r)
  4. Repeat the process with b and r until remainder is 0

The algorithm terminates because the remainders decrease with each step and eventually reach zero.

Detailed Examples

Example 1: GCD(48, 18)

48 ÷ 18 = 2 with remainder 12
18 ÷ 12 = 1 with remainder 6
12 ÷ 6 = 2 with remainder 0
GCD(48, 18) = 6

Example 2: GCD(100, 75)

100 ÷ 75 = 1 with remainder 25
75 ÷ 25 = 3 with remainder 0
GCD(100, 75) = 25

Example 3: GCD(17, 13) - Coprime Numbers

17 ÷ 13 = 1 with remainder 4
13 ÷ 4 = 3 with remainder 1
4 ÷ 1 = 4 with remainder 0
GCD(17, 13) = 1

Applications

  • Fraction Simplification: Reducing fractions to lowest terms
  • Cryptography: Essential for RSA algorithm and public-key cryptography
  • Computer Science: Used in algorithms for rational number arithmetic
  • Number Theory: Foundation for solving linear Diophantine equations
  • Engineering: Used in gear ratio calculations and signal processing

Find the GCD of 84 and 36 using the Euclidean Algorithm.

Determine if 15 and 28 are coprime (GCD = 1).

28 ÷ 15 = 1 with remainder 13
15 ÷ 13 = 1 with remainder 2
13 ÷ 2 = 6 with remainder 1
2 ÷ 1 = 2 with remainder 0
GCD(15, 28) = 1

Yes, 15 and 28 are coprime.

Key Takeaways

  • The Euclidean Algorithm is efficient and terminates in a finite number of steps
  • GCD(a, b) = GCD(b, r) where r is the remainder of a ÷ b
  • The algorithm works for any two positive integers
  • If the final remainder is 1, the numbers are coprime
  • The algorithm is the basis for many important mathematical and computational applications
