Congruence Rules

Core Concept: Congruence Relations

Two integers a and b are congruent modulo m if they have the same remainder when divided by m. This is written as:

$a \equiv b \pmod{m}$

This means that m divides a - b, or equivalently: $a - b = km$ for some integer k.

Basic Properties of Congruence

If $a \equiv c \pmod{m}$ and $b \equiv d \pmod{m}$, then:

  1. 1 $a + b \equiv c + d \pmod{m}$
  2. 2 $a - b \equiv c - d \pmod{m}$
  3. 3 $ab \equiv cd \pmod{m}$
  4. 4 $a^k \equiv c^k \pmod{m}$

Detailed Examples

Example 1: Basic Congruence

Check if 17 ≡ 5 mod 6:

17 ÷ 6 = 2 with remainder 5
5 ÷ 6 = 0 with remainder 5
Since both have remainder 5: 17 ≡ 5 mod 6 ✓

Example 2: Using Congruence Properties

If 7 ≡ 1 mod 3 and 4 ≡ 1 mod 3, find 7 × 4 mod 3:

7 ≡ 1 mod 3
4 ≡ 1 mod 3
7 × 4 ≡ 1 × 1 ≡ 1 mod 3
Check: 28 ÷ 3 = 9 with remainder 1 ✓

Example 3: Powers and Congruence

If 2 ≡ 5 mod 3, find 2³ mod 3:

2 ≡ 5 mod 3
2³ ≡ 5³ mod 3
8 ≡ 125 mod 3
8 ÷ 3 = 2 with remainder 2
125 ÷ 3 = 41 with remainder 2
So 2³ ≡ 2 mod 3 ✓

Applications

  • Cryptography: Foundation of RSA and other public-key cryptosystems
  • Computer Science: Hash functions and data structures
  • Number Theory: Solving linear congruences and systems of congruences
  • Calendar Calculations: Determining days of the week and leap years
  • Digital Signal Processing: Fast Fourier transforms and cyclic codes

Practice Problems

1

Determine if 23 ≡ 8 mod 5.

23 ÷ 5 = 4 with remainder 3
8 ÷ 5 = 1 with remainder 3
Since both have remainder 3: 23 ≡ 8 mod 5 ✓
2

If 3 ≡ 7 mod 4 and 2 ≡ 6 mod 4, find 3 × 2 mod 4.

3 ≡ 7 mod 4
2 ≡ 6 mod 4
3 × 2 ≡ 7 × 6 mod 4
42 ÷ 4 = 10 with remainder 2
So 3 × 2 ≡ 2 mod 4
3

Find x such that x ≡ 3 mod 7 and x ≡ 4 mod 5.

This is a system of linear congruences. Using the Chinese Remainder Theorem:

Find x = 5k + 4 where k = 0, 1, 2, ...
5k + 4 ≡ 3 mod 7
5k ≡ -1 mod 7
5k ≡ 6 mod 7
Multiply both sides by 3: 15k ≡ 18 mod 7
k ≡ 4 mod 7
x = 5(4) + 4 = 24
Check: 24 ÷ 7 = 3 with remainder 3 ✓
24 ÷ 5 = 4 with remainder 4 ✓
4

If 5 ≡ 2 mod 3, find 5² mod 3.

5 ≡ 2 mod 3
5² ≡ 2² mod 3
25 ≡ 4 mod 3
4 ÷ 3 = 1 with remainder 1
So 5² ≡ 1 mod 3

Key Takeaways

  • Congruence is an equivalence relation that preserves addition, subtraction, multiplication, and exponentiation
  • $a \equiv b \pmod{m}$ means m divides (a - b)
  • Congruence classes partition the integers into m distinct residue classes
  • The properties allow us to perform arithmetic operations on representatives of congruence classes
  • Congruence is fundamental to modular arithmetic and many cryptographic systems
