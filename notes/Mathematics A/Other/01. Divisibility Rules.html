Divisibility Rules

üìö Core Concepts

Divisibility rules are shortcuts to determine whether one integer is divisible by another without performing the actual division. These rules are based on the properties of our base-10 number system and modular arithmetic.

A number a is divisible by b (written as b|a) if there exists an integer k such that a = b √ó k.

Divisibility rules help us quickly check this condition for common divisors.

üî¢ Basic Divisibility Rules (Powers of 2 and 5)

Divisible by 2

Last digit is even (0, 2, 4, 6, 8)
Even numbers end with even digits
128: 8 is even ‚Üí 128 √∑ 2 = 64

Divisible by 4

Last two digits form a number divisible by 4
Check if the number formed by the last two digits is divisible by 4
516: 16 √∑ 4 = 4 ‚Üí 516 √∑ 4 = 129

Divisible by 8

Last three digits form a number divisible by 8
Check if the number formed by the last three digits is divisible by 8
1016: 016 = 16, 16 √∑ 8 = 2 ‚Üí 1016 √∑ 8 = 127

Divisible by 5

Last digit is 0 or 5
Numbers ending in 5 or 0 are divisible by 5
175: ends with 5 ‚Üí 175 √∑ 5 = 35

Divisible by 10

Last digit is 0
Multiples of 10 end with 0
420: ends with 0 ‚Üí 420 √∑ 10 = 42

üî¢ Sum-Based Rules (Divisors of 9)

Divisible by 3

Sum of digits is divisible by 3
Digital root property: 3 divides the sum of digits
123: 1+2+3=6, 6√∑3=2 ‚Üí 123√∑3=41

Divisible by 9

Sum of digits is divisible by 9
Digital root property: 9 divides the sum of digits
729: 7+2+9=18, 18√∑9=2 ‚Üí 729√∑9=81

üí° Examples

Example 1: Is 1,248 divisible by 8?

Check last three digits: 248. Is 248 √∑ 8 = 31? Yes, so 1,248 is divisible by 8.
Example 2: Is 2,547 divisible by 9?

Sum of digits: 2+5+4+7=18. 18√∑9=2, so yes, 2,547 is divisible by 9.

Last two digits: 24. 24√∑4=6, so yes, 15,624 is divisible by 4.

üéØ Key Takeaways

  • Divisibility rules are shortcuts based on base-10 properties
  • Power-of-2 rules check last digits (2:1, 4:2, 8:3)
  • Power-of-5 rules check last digit (5: 0 or 5, 10: 0)
  • Sum-of-digits rules work for 3 and 9
  • Rules can be combined for complex divisibility tests
  • Essential for mental math and number theory
  • Used in computer algorithms and error-checking
