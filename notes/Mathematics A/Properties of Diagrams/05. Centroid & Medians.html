Centroid & Medians

Geometry • Triangle Properties

Centroid Theorem

In any triangle, the three medians intersect at a single point called the centroid. The centroid divides each median in a 2:1 ratio, with the longer segment being from the vertex to the centroid.

If $G$ is the centroid of $\triangle ABC$, then:
$AG:GM = BG:GN = CG:GP = 2:1$
where $M$, $N$, $P$ are midpoints of the opposite sides.

Understanding the Centroid

The centroid is one of the most important points in a triangle. A median is a line segment from a vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side. The centroid is the intersection point of all three medians.

Key Properties:

  • The centroid is the "center of mass" or balance point of the triangle
  • It always lies inside the triangle
  • It divides each median in a 2:1 ratio (vertex to centroid : centroid to midpoint)
  • The centroid is the point where the triangle would balance perfectly

Proof: Centroid Divides Medians in 2:1 Ratio

Step 1: Coordinate Geometry Approach

Place $\triangle ABC$ on coordinate plane with $A(x_1,y_1)$, $B(x_2,y_2)$, $C(x_3,y_3)$.

Midpoint of $BC$: $M\left(\frac{x_2+x_3}{2}, \frac{y_2+y_3}{2}\right)$

Centroid $G$ coordinates: $G\left(\frac{x_1+x_2+x_3}{3}, \frac{y_1+y_2+y_3}{3}\right)$

Step 2: Section Formula

The centroid divides the median from $A$ to $M$ in the ratio $AM:MG$.

Using section formula: $G = \left(\frac{2\cdot M_x + 1\cdot A_x}{2+1}, \frac{2\cdot M_y + 1\cdot A_y}{2+1}\right)$

$G = \left(\frac{2\cdot\frac{x_2+x_3}{2} + x_1}{3}, \frac{2\cdot\frac{y_2+y_3}{2} + y_1}{3}\right) = \left(\frac{x_1+x_2+x_3}{3}, \frac{y_1+y_2+y_3}{3}\right)$

Step 3: Vector Approach

Consider vectors: $\overrightarrow{AG} = \frac{2}{3}\overrightarrow{AM}$

Since $M$ is midpoint of $BC$, $\overrightarrow{AM} = \overrightarrow{AB} + \frac{1}{2}\overrightarrow{AC}$

Therefore, $\overrightarrow{AG} = \frac{2}{3}(\overrightarrow{AB} + \frac{1}{2}\overrightarrow{AC}) = \frac{2}{3}\overrightarrow{AB} + \frac{1}{3}\overrightarrow{AC}$

Step 4: Area Ratio Property

The centroid divides each median such that the area ratios are consistent.

Since $AG:GM = 2:1$, the area of $\triangle AGC = \frac{1}{3}$ area of $\triangle ABC$.

Example 1: Finding Centroid Coordinates

Find the centroid of $\triangle ABC$ with vertices $A(1,2)$, $B(4,6)$, and $C(7,3)$.

Solution:

Formula: Centroid $G\left(\frac{x_1+x_2+x_3}{3}, \frac{y_1+y_2+y_3}{3}\right)$

$G\left(\frac{1+4+7}{3}, \frac{2+6+3}{3}\right) = G\left(\frac{12}{3}, \frac{11}{3}\right) = G(4, \frac{11}{3})$

Verification with medians:

  • Midpoint of $BC$: $M\left(\frac{4+7}{2}, \frac{6+3}{2}\right) = M(5.5, 4.5)$
  • Median from $A$ to $M$: parametric equations
  • $x = 1 + 4.5t$, $y = 2 + 2.5t$
  • Centroid divides in 2:1 ratio: $t = \frac{2}{2+1} = \frac{2}{3}$
  • $x = 1 + 4.5\cdot\frac{2}{3} = 1 + 3 = 4$
  • $y = 2 + 2.5\cdot\frac{2}{3} = 2 + \frac{5}{3} = \frac{11}{3}$

Example 2: Centroid and Area Division

The centroid of $\triangle ABC$ is at $G(3,4)$. If the area of $\triangle ABC$ is 24, find the area of $\triangle AGC$.

Solution:

The centroid divides each median in a 2:1 ratio.

Since $AG:GM = 2:1$, the area of $\triangle AGC = \frac{1}{3}$ the area of $\triangle ABC$.

Area of $\triangle AGC = \frac{1}{3} \times 24 = 8$

Note: This is true for any triangle because the centroid creates similar triangles with area ratios following the linear ratios squared.

Applications of Centroid

Physics and Engineering

The centroid represents the center of mass of a triangular lamina. This is crucial in physics for calculating moments of inertia and in engineering for determining balance points of triangular structures.

Computer Graphics

In computer graphics and CAD software, centroids are used for object manipulation, collision detection, and mesh generation algorithms.

Surveying and Navigation

Land surveyors use centroids to divide triangular plots of land and determine optimal locations for utilities or construction within triangular regions.

Practice Problems

1. Find the centroid of $\triangle ABC$ with vertices $A(2,3)$, $B(5,7)$, and $C(8,4)$.

$G\left(\frac{2+5+8}{3}, \frac{3+7+4}{3}\right) = G\left(\frac{15}{3}, \frac{14}{3}\right) = G(5, \frac{14}{3})$

2. In $\triangle ABC$, the centroid is at $G(4,5)$ and vertex $A$ is at $(1,2)$. Find the midpoint of side $BC$.

The centroid divides the median from $A$ to midpoint $M$ of $BC$ in 2:1 ratio.

Since $AG:GM = 2:1$, and $G$ divides $AM$ in this ratio, we can find $M$.

Using section formula: $M = \frac{3G - 2A}{1} = 3(4,5) - 2(1,2) = (12-2, 15-4) = (10,11)$

Wait, that's not right. Let me recalculate:

If $G$ divides $AM$ in ratio $AG:GM = 2:1$, then $G = \frac{1\cdot M + 2\cdot A}{1+2} = \frac{M + 2A}{3}$

So $3G = M + 2A \Rightarrow M = 3G - 2A = 3(4,5) - 2(1,2) = (12,15) - (2,4) = (10,11)$

3. The area of $\triangle ABC$ is 36. If the centroid divides the median from $A$ in 2:1 ratio, find the area of $\triangle AGC$.

The centroid divides each median in 2:1 ratio, so the area of $\triangle AGC = \frac{1}{3}$ the area of $\triangle ABC$.

Area of $\triangle AGC = \frac{1}{3} \times 36 = 12$

4. In $\triangle ABC$, $D$, $E$, $F$ are midpoints of sides $BC$, $AC$, $AB$ respectively. Prove that the medians intersect at a point that divides each median in 2:1 ratio.

Let $G$ be the intersection point of medians $AD$ and $BE$.

Consider vectors: $\overrightarrow{AG} = \frac{2}{3}\overrightarrow{AD}$

Since $D$ is midpoint of $BC$, $\overrightarrow{AD} = \overrightarrow{AB} + \frac{1}{2}\overrightarrow{AC}$

Thus $\overrightarrow{AG} = \frac{2}{3}(\overrightarrow{AB} + \frac{1}{2}\overrightarrow{AC}) = \frac{2}{3}\overrightarrow{AB} + \frac{1}{3}\overrightarrow{AC}$

This shows $G$ is the centroid. The 2:1 ratio follows from the section formula.

5. Find the centroid of an equilateral triangle with side length 6 units and one vertex at the origin.

Place equilateral triangle with $A(0,0)$, $B(6,0)$, $C(3, 3\sqrt{3})$.

Centroid: $G\left(\frac{0+6+3}{3}, \frac{0+0+3\sqrt{3}}{3}\right) = G\left(\frac{9}{3}, \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{3}\right) = G(3, \sqrt{3})$

In an equilateral triangle, the centroid coincides with the orthocenter and circumcenter.

Key Takeaways

  • The centroid is the intersection point of the three medians of a triangle.
  • A median connects a vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side.
  • The centroid divides each median in a 2:1 ratio (vertex to centroid : centroid to midpoint).
  • The centroid is the center of mass or balance point of the triangle.
  • The centroid coordinates can be found using the formula: $G\left(\frac{x_1+x_2+x_3}{3}, \frac{y_1+y_2+y_3}{3}\right)$
  • The centroid always lies inside the triangle.
  • The centroid creates six smaller triangles of equal area.
  • In an equilateral triangle, the centroid coincides with the orthocenter and circumcenter.
