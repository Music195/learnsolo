üìè Theorem: Equal Tangent Lengths

If two tangents are drawn from a point outside a circle to the circle, then:

  • Lengths are Equal: The lengths of the two tangents are equal
  • Right Angles: Each tangent is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact
  • Equal Segments: The line from the center to the external point bisects the angle formed by the tangents

This fundamental property is essential for solving many circle geometry problems involving tangents.
