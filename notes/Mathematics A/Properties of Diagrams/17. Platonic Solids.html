Mathematical Properties
For any Platonic solid: $V - E + F = 2$ (Euler's Formula)
1. Each face has the same number of edges ($p$) and vertices ($p$)
2. Each vertex has the same number of faces meeting ($q$)
3. Total edges: $E = \frac{F \cdot p}{2} = \frac{V \cdot q}{2}$
4. Euler's formula: $V - E + F = 2$
5. Substituting: $V - \frac{V \cdot q}{2} + F = 2$
6. Only solutions where $p ‚â• 3$, $q ‚â• 3$, and $\frac{1}{p} + \frac{1}{q} < \frac{1}{2}$ are possible

Detailed Examples
Tetrahedron Analysis
\[ \begin{aligned} F &= 4 \quad (p=3) \\ V &= 4 \quad (q=3) \\ E &= \frac{4 \times 3}{2} = 6 \\ V - E + F &= 4 - 6 + 4 = 2 ‚úì \end{aligned} \]

Each face is an equilateral triangle. Three faces meet at each vertex.

Cube Analysis
\[ \begin{aligned} F &= 6 \quad (p=4) \\ V &= 8 \quad (q=3) \\ E &= \frac{6 \times 4}{2} = 12 \\ V - E + F &= 8 - 12 + 6 = 2 ‚úì \end{aligned} \]

Each face is a square. Three faces meet at each vertex.

Dodecahedron Analysis
\[ \begin{aligned} F &= 12 \quad (p=5) \\ V &= 20 \quad (q=3) \\ E &= \frac{12 \times 5}{2} = 30 \\ V - E + F &= 20 - 30 + 12 = 2 ‚úì \end{aligned} \]

Each face is a regular pentagon. Three faces meet at each vertex.

Applications of Platonic Solids
Crystallography: Platonic solids appear in crystal structures and mineral formations.
Architecture: Used in building design and geodesic domes (truncated icosahedron).
Game Design: Dice are based on Platonic solids (d4=tetrahedron, d6=cube, d8=octahedron, d12=dodecahedron, d20=icosahedron).
Molecular Chemistry: Some molecules form Platonic solid structures (e.g., methane has tetrahedral geometry).
Art and Design: Used in sculpture, jewelry, and computer graphics for their aesthetic properties.

Problem 4: Impossible Platonic Solid

Why can't there be a Platonic solid with triangular faces where 7 triangles meet at each vertex?

For triangular faces (p=3), q=7: $\frac{1}{p} + \frac{1}{q} = \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{7} = \frac{10}{21} \approx 0.476$

This is greater than $\frac{1}{2} = 0.5$, so it violates the inequality $\frac{1}{p} + \frac{1}{q} < \frac{1}{2}$

The angle at each vertex would be too large to tile the sphere.

Key Takeaways
‚úì There are exactly five Platonic solids: tetrahedron, cube, octahedron, dodecahedron, and icosahedron
‚úì All Platonic solids satisfy Euler's formula: V - E + F = 2
‚úì Each Platonic solid is characterized by two numbers: (p, q) where p is the number of sides per face and q is the number of faces meeting at each vertex
‚úì The five solids are the only regular polyhedra that can exist in 3D space
