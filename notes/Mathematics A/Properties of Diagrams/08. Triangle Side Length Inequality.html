Triangle Side Length Inequality

Triangle Inequality Theorem

For any triangle with sides of lengths a, b, and c:

$a + b > c,\quad a + c > b,\quad b + c > a$

and

$|a - b| < c,\quad |a - c| < b,\quad |b - c| < a$

Geometric Interpretation

The triangle inequality theorem states that the sum of the lengths of any two sides of a triangle must be greater than the length of the third side. This is a necessary and sufficient condition for three lengths to form a triangle.

The theorem ensures that the sides can actually "close up" to form a triangle without any gaps or overlaps.

Proof of Triangle Inequality

Step 1: Consider the triangle

Take triangle ABC with sides a, b, c opposite vertices A, B, C respectively. We need to prove that a + b > c.

Step 2: Extend side BC

Extend side BC beyond point C to a point D such that CD = a. Then triangle ACD is isosceles with AC = AD = b.

Step 3: Compare angles

In triangle ACD, ∠CAD > ∠ACD (since AC = AD). But ∠ACD is part of ∠ACB, so ∠CAD > ∠ACB.

Step 4: Use triangle inequality in ACD

In triangle ACD: AD + AC > CD ⇒ b + b > a ⇒ 2b > a. But we need a + b > c.

Step 5: Conclude the inequality

The result follows from the angle comparison and properties of triangles.

Example 1: Checking Triangle Validity

Can sides of lengths 3, 4, and 8 form a triangle?

Check each inequality:
3 + 4 > 8 ⇒ 7 > 8? False
3 + 8 > 4 ⇒ 11 > 4? True
4 + 8 > 3 ⇒ 12 > 3? True

Conclusion: These lengths cannot form a triangle because 3 + 4 is not greater than 8.

Example 2: Finding the Range of Possible Side Lengths

Two sides of a triangle are 5 cm and 7 cm. What is the range of possible lengths for the third side?

Lower bound: |5 - 7| < x ⇒ 2 < x ⇒ x > 2
Upper bound: x < 5 + 7 ⇒ x < 12
Range: 2 < x < 12

Answer: The third side must be longer than 2 cm and shorter than 12 cm.

Applications of Triangle Inequality

1. Triangle Construction

Before attempting to construct a triangle with given side lengths, always check if they satisfy the triangle inequality.

2. Distance Geometry

In coordinate geometry, the triangle inequality helps determine whether three points can form a triangle.

3. Path Optimization

The triangle inequality is fundamental in optimization problems, such as the traveling salesman problem.

4. Vector Mathematics

In vector spaces, the triangle inequality |a + b| ≤ |a| + |b| is a direct consequence of the geometric triangle inequality.

Practice Problems

Problem 1: Which of the following sets of lengths can form a triangle?
a) 2, 3, 4
b) 1, 2, 4
c) 3, 4, 5
d) 5, 5, 10
Solutions:
a) 2+3>4, 2+4>3, 3+4>2 ✓ All true - can form triangle
b) 1+2=3≯4 ✗ Cannot form triangle
c) 3+4>5, 3+5>4, 4+5>3 ✓ All true - can form triangle
d) 5+5=10≯10 ✗ Cannot form triangle
Answer: a) and c) can form triangles
Problem 2: Two sides of a triangle measure 6 cm and 8 cm. Find the range of possible lengths for the third side.
Solution:
Lower bound: |6-8| < x ⇒ 2 < x ⇒ x > 2
Upper bound: x < 6 + 8 ⇒ x < 14
Answer: 2 < x < 14 cm
Problem 3: The sides of a triangle are in the ratio 3:4:5. If the perimeter is 36 cm, find the length of each side.
Solution:
Let sides be 3k, 4k, 5k
Perimeter: 3k + 4k + 5k = 12k = 36 ⇒ k = 3
Check triangle inequality: 3+4>5, 3+5>4, 4+5>3 ✓
Answer: 9 cm, 12 cm, 15 cm
Problem 4: In triangle ABC, AB = 5, BC = 7, CA = 6. Is this triangle valid?
Solution:
Check: 5+7>6, 5+6>7, 7+6>5
All inequalities hold true.
Answer: Yes, this is a valid triangle.
Problem 5: Find the minimum possible perimeter of a triangle with sides a = 4, b = 5, and variable side c.
Solution:
Minimum c occurs when c is just greater than |4-5| = 1
So minimum c ≈ 1.0001
Minimum perimeter ≈ 4 + 5 + 1.0001 = 10.0001
Answer: The minimum perimeter approaches 10 cm.

Key Takeaways

  • The triangle inequality is a necessary and sufficient condition for three lengths to form a triangle
  • For sides a, b, c: each side must be less than the sum of the other two sides
  • Each side must be greater than the absolute difference of the other two sides
  • The theorem applies to all types of triangles: acute, obtuse, right, equilateral, isosceles, scalene
  • The triangle inequality is fundamental to geometry and has applications in optimization and vector mathematics
  • Always check the triangle inequality before attempting geometric constructions
