Circle Inscribed Angle Theorem

Inscribed Angle Theorem

The measure of an inscribed angle is equal to half the measure of the central angle that subtends the same arc.

$\measuredangle \text{inscribed} = \dfrac{1}{2} \measuredangle \text{central}$

Geometric Interpretation

An inscribed angle is formed by two chords that meet on the circumference of a circle. The inscribed angle theorem states that no matter where you place an inscribed angle that subtends the same arc, its measure will always be exactly half of the central angle that subtends the same arc.

This theorem is fundamental to circle geometry and has numerous applications in trigonometry and geometric constructions.

Proof of Inscribed Angle Theorem

Step 1: Draw the inscribed angle and central angle

Draw circle O with inscribed angle ∠APB and central angle ∠AOB subtending the same arc AB.

Step 2: Draw radius from center to point P

Draw radius OP. This divides the inscribed angle into two smaller angles.

Step 3: Identify isosceles triangles

Triangles OAP and OBP are isosceles with OA = OP = OB = radius. Therefore, base angles ∠OAP = ∠OPA and ∠OBP = ∠OPB.

Step 4: Use angle sum at point P

At point P: ∠APB = ∠APO + ∠BPO = (1/2)∠AOB + (1/2)∠AOB = (1/2)∠AOB

Step 5: Conclusion

Therefore, the inscribed angle ∠APB equals half the central angle ∠AOB.

Example 1: Finding Inscribed Angle

In circle O, central angle ∠AOB measures 120°. Find the measure of inscribed angle ∠APB that subtends the same arc AB.

Given: Central angle = 120°
Inscribed Angle Theorem: Inscribed angle = ½ × central angle
Calculation: Inscribed angle = ½ × 120° = 60°

Answer: The inscribed angle measures 60°.

Example 2: Finding Central Angle

In circle O, inscribed angle ∠ABC measures 35°. Find the measure of the central angle that subtends the same arc.

Given: Inscribed angle = 35°
Inscribed Angle Theorem: Central angle = 2 × inscribed angle
Calculation: Central angle = 2 × 35° = 70°

Answer: The central angle measures 70°.

Practice Problems

Problem 1: In circle O, central angle ∠AOB = 100°. Find the measure of inscribed angle ∠APB.
Solution:
Inscribed angle = ½ × central angle
Inscribed angle = ½ × 100° = 50°
Answer: 50°
Problem 2: Inscribed angle ∠ABC measures 42°. Find the central angle subtending the same arc.
Solution:
Central angle = 2 × inscribed angle
Central angle = 2 × 42° = 84°
Answer: 84°
Problem 3: Two inscribed angles subtend the same arc. If one measures 65°, what is the other?
Solution:
Both inscribed angles subtending the same arc are equal.
Answer: 65°
Problem 4: In circle O, ∠AOB = 180° (semicircle). Find inscribed angle ∠APB.
Solution:
Inscribed angle = ½ × 180° = 90°
Answer: 90° (right angle)
Problem 5: Central angle is 3 times the inscribed angle subtending the same arc. Find both angles.
Solution:
Let inscribed angle = x, then central angle = 2x
Given: 2x = 3x ⇒ x = 0° (degenerate case)
This is impossible for a proper arc.
Answer: Impossible

Key Takeaways

  • The inscribed angle is always half the central angle subtending the same arc
  • All inscribed angles subtending the same arc are equal
  • An inscribed angle subtending a semicircle is always 90°
  • The theorem applies to any circle and any position of the inscribed angle
  • The theorem is fundamental to circle geometry and trigonometry
  • The relationship holds regardless of the circle's size or position
