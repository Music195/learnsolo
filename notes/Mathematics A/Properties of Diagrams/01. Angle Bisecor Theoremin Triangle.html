01. Angle Bisector Theorem in Triangle

Theorem Statement

Angle Bisector Theorem

In a triangle, the angle bisector divides the opposite side in the ratio of the other two sides.

If AD is the angle bisector of ∠A in △ABC, then:
\(\frac{BD}{DC} = \frac{AB}{AC}\)

Visual Understanding

The angle bisector creates two smaller triangles that are similar to each other. This similarity leads to the proportional relationship between the segments of the opposite side.

Proof of the Theorem

Given: △ABC with AD as angle bisector of ∠A
Draw: A perpendicular line from D to AB, meeting at E
Observe: ∠BAE = ∠CAD (both equal to half of ∠A)
∠AED = ∠AEB = 90° (by construction)
∴ △ABE ≅ △ACE (by AAS congruence)
∴ AE = AE (common side) and ∠BAE = ∠CAE
∴ △ADE ≅ △ADE (by SAS similarity)
∴ \(\frac{BD}{DC} = \frac{AB}{AC}\)

Detailed Examples

Example 1: Basic Application

In △ABC, AB = 8 cm, AC = 6 cm, and BC = 10 cm. If AD is the angle bisector of ∠A, find BD and DC.

By Angle Bisector Theorem: \(\frac{BD}{DC} = \frac{AB}{AC} = \frac{8}{6} = \frac{4}{3}\)
Let BD = 4k, DC = 3k, then BC = BD + DC = 7k = 10
k = 10/7 ≈ 1.4286
BD = 4 × (10/7) = 40/7 ≈ 5.71 cm
DC = 3 × (10/7) = 30/7 ≈ 4.29 cm

Result: BD ≈ 5.71 cm, DC ≈ 4.29 cm

Example 2: Finding Side Lengths

In △PQR, PQ = 9 cm, PR = 12 cm, and QR = 15 cm. If PS is the angle bisector of ∠P, find QS and SR.

\(\frac{QS}{SR} = \frac{PQ}{PR} = \frac{9}{12} = \frac{3}{4}\)
Let QS = 3k, SR = 4k, then QR = QS + SR = 7k = 15
k = 15/7 ≈ 2.1429
QS = 3 × (15/7) = 45/7 ≈ 6.43 cm
SR = 4 × (15/7) = 60/7 ≈ 8.57 cm

Result: QS ≈ 6.43 cm, SR ≈ 8.57 cm

Example 3: Coordinate Geometry

Points A(0,0), B(8,0), C(3,6). Find the coordinates of D where AD is the angle bisector of ∠A.

AB = distance from A to B = 8 units
AC = distance from A to C = √(3² + 6²) = √45 = 3√5 ≈ 6.71 units
Ratio BD/DC = AB/AC = 8/(3√5) = 8√5/15
Let BD = 8√5k, DC = 15k, then BC = 8√5k + 15k = 15 + 8√5 ≈ 15 + 17.89 = 32.89
D divides BC in ratio BD:DC = 8√5:15
D = ((15×8 + 8√5×3)/(15 + 8√5), (15×0 + 8√5×6)/(15 + 8√5))
D ≈ (4.85, 3.41)

Result: D ≈ (4.85, 3.41)

Applications

Triangle Construction

The angle bisector theorem helps in constructing triangles when given certain measurements and angle bisector information.

Coordinate Geometry

Used to find coordinates of points on triangle sides when angle bisectors are involved.

Engineering and Design

Applied in structural engineering for determining load distribution and stress points in triangular frameworks.

Navigation and Surveying

Used in land surveying to determine distances and positions using angular measurements.

Practice Problems

1

In △ABC, AB = 5 cm, AC = 4 cm, and BC = 7 cm. If AD is the angle bisector of ∠A, find BD and DC.

BD/DC = AB/AC = 5/4
Let BD = 5k, DC = 4k, then BC = 9k = 7
k = 7/9
BD = 5 × 7/9 = 35/9 ≈ 3.89 cm
DC = 4 × 7/9 = 28/9 ≈ 3.11 cm

Answer: BD ≈ 3.89 cm, DC ≈ 3.11 cm

2

In △PQR, ∠P = 60°, PQ = 8 cm, and PR = 6 cm. If PS is the angle bisector, find QS and SR.

The problem gives angle and two sides, so we need to find QR first.

QS/SR = PQ/PR = 8/6 = 4/3
Let QS = 4k, SR = 3k
Need to find QR using law of cosines
QR² = PQ² + PR² - 2×PQ×PR×cos∠P
QR² = 64 + 36 - 2×8×6×cos60° = 100 - 96×0.5 = 100 - 48 = 52
QR = √52 ≈ 7.21 cm
QS + SR = 7k = 7.21, k ≈ 1.03
QS ≈ 4.12 cm, SR ≈ 3.09 cm

Answer: QS ≈ 4.12 cm, SR ≈ 3.09 cm

3

Points A(1,1), B(5,1), C(3,4). Find the coordinates of D where AD is the angle bisector of ∠A.

AB = distance from A to B = √((5-1)² + (1-1)²) = 4
AC = distance from A to C = √((3-1)² + (4-1)²) = √(4+9) = √13 ≈ 3.61
Ratio BD/DC = AB/AC = 4/√13 = 4√13/13
D divides BC in ratio 4√13:13
D = ((13×5 + 4√13×3)/(13 + 4√13), (13×1 + 4√13×4)/(13 + 4√13))
D ≈ (3.85, 2.15)

Answer: D ≈ (3.85, 2.15)

5

In △ABC, AB = 9 cm, AC = 12 cm, and ∠A = 60°. Find the length of the angle bisector AD.

Formula: AD = √[AB×AC×(1 - BC²/(AB+AC)²)]
First find BC using law of cosines
BC² = AB² + AC² - 2×AB×AC×cos∠A
BC² = 81 + 144 - 2×9×12×cos60° = 225 - 216×0.5 = 225 - 108 = 117
BC = √117 ≈ 10.82 cm
AD = √[9×12×(1 - 117/(9+12)²)] = √[108×(1 - 117/441)]
AD = √[108×(1 - 0.265)] = √[108×0.735] ≈ √79.38 ≈ 8.91 cm

Answer: AD ≈ 8.91 cm

Key Takeaways

  • Theorem: The angle bisector divides the opposite side in the ratio of the adjacent sides
  • Ratio: BD/DC = AB/AC
  • Proof: Based on similar triangles formed by the angle bisector
  • Applications: Used in triangle construction, coordinate geometry, and surveying
  • Formula for length: AD = √[AB×AC×(1 - BC²/(AB+AC)²)]
  • Important: The theorem applies to any triangle, regardless of whether it's acute, obtuse, or right-angled
