Circumcenter and Circumcircle of a Triangle

Circumcenter and Circumcircle

Geometry • Triangle Properties

Circumcenter Theorem

The three perpendicular bisectors of the sides of a triangle intersect at a single point called the circumcenter. This point is equidistant from all three vertices of the triangle.

If $O$ is the circumcenter of $\triangle ABC$, then:
$OA = OB = OC = R$
where $R$ is the circumradius.

Understanding the Circumcenter

The circumcenter is the center of the circle that passes through all three vertices of the triangle. This circle is called the circumcircle or circumscribed circle.

The circumcenter has different positions depending on the type of triangle:

  • Acute triangle: Circumcenter lies inside the triangle
  • Right triangle: Circumcenter lies on the hypotenuse (midpoint)
  • Obtuse triangle: Circumcenter lies outside the triangle

Proof: Existence of Circumcenter

Step 1: Perpendicular Bisectors

Consider $\triangle ABC$. Draw the perpendicular bisectors of sides $AB$, $BC$, and $CA$. Each perpendicular bisector passes through the midpoint of its side and is perpendicular to it.

Step 2: Intersection Point

The perpendicular bisector of $AB$ intersects the perpendicular bisector of $BC$ at point $O$. We need to show that $O$ also lies on the perpendicular bisector of $AC$.

Step 3: Distance Equality

Since $O$ lies on the perpendicular bisector of $AB$, $OA = OB$.
Since $O$ lies on the perpendicular bisector of $BC$, $OB = OC$.
Therefore, $OA = OB = OC$, so $O$ is equidistant from all three vertices.

Step 4: Circumcircle Construction

The circle with center $O$ and radius $OA$ (or $OB$ or $OC$) will pass through all three vertices $A$, $B$, and $C$, forming the circumcircle.

Example 1: Finding Circumcenter Coordinates

Find the circumcenter of $\triangle ABC$ with vertices $A(1,1)$, $B(4,5)$, and $C(7,2)$.

Solution:

Step 1: Find midpoints of the sides:

  • Midpoint of $AB$: $((1+4)/2, (1+5)/2) = (2.5, 3)$
  • Midpoint of $BC$: $((4+7)/2, (5+2)/2) = (5.5, 3.5)$
  • Midpoint of $AC$: $((1+7)/2, (1+2)/2) = (4, 1.5)$

Step 2: Find equations of perpendicular bisectors:

  • Perp. bisector of $AB$: slope of $AB$ is $(5-1)/(4-1) = 4/3$, so slope of perp. bisector is $-3/4$.
    Equation: $y - 3 = -\frac{3}{4}(x - 2.5) \Rightarrow y = -\frac{3}{4}x + \frac{15}{4} + 3 = -\frac{3}{4}x + \frac{27}{4}$
  • Perp. bisector of $BC$: slope of $BC$ is $(2-5)/(7-4) = (-3)/3 = -1$, so slope of perp. bisector is $1$.
    Equation: $y - 3.5 = 1(x - 5.5) \Rightarrow y = x - 5.5 + 3.5 = x - 2$

Step 3: Solve the system:
$-\frac{3}{4}x + \frac{27}{4} = x - 2$
$\frac{27}{4} + 2 = x + \frac{3}{4}x$
$\frac{35}{4} = \frac{7}{4}x$
$x = 5$
$y = 5 - 2 = 3$

Circumcenter: $O(5, 3)$

Circumradius: $R = \sqrt{(5-1)^2 + (3-1)^2} = \sqrt{16 + 4} = \sqrt{20} = 2\sqrt{5} \approx 4.47$

Example 2: Circumcenter in Right Triangle

Find the circumcenter of right triangle $ABC$ with right angle at $C(0,0)$, $A(3,0)$, and $B(0,4)$.

Solution:

Since it's a right triangle, the circumcenter lies at the midpoint of the hypotenuse $AB$.

Midpoint of $AB$: $((3+0)/2, (0+4)/2) = (1.5, 2)$

Circumcenter: $O(1.5, 2)$

Circumradius: $R = \sqrt{(1.5-0)^2 + (2-0)^2} = \sqrt{2.25 + 4} = \sqrt{6.25} = 2.5$

Applications of Circumcenter

Navigation and GPS

The circumcenter concept is used in trilateration, a method used by GPS systems to determine location by measuring distances to multiple satellites.

Circle Construction

The circumcircle is the smallest circle that can contain the triangle. This is useful in computer graphics for bounding boxes and collision detection.

Engineering Applications

In structural engineering, the circumcenter helps determine the center of mass and balance points for triangular frameworks.

Practice Problems

1. Find the circumcenter of $\triangle ABC$ with vertices $A(2,3)$, $B(5,7)$, and $C(8,4)$.

Midpoints:

  • $AB$: $(3.5, 5)$
  • $BC$: $(6.5, 5.5)$
  • $AC$: $(5, 3.5)$

Perp. bisector of AB: slope of AB is 2, so perp. slope is -1/2.
Equation: $y - 5 = -\frac{1}{2}(x - 3.5) \Rightarrow y = -\frac{1}{2}x + \frac{3.5}{2} + 5 = -\frac{1}{2}x + 6.75$

Perp. bisector of BC: slope of BC is 3/3=1, so perp. slope is -1.
Equation: $y - 5.5 = -1(x - 6.5) \Rightarrow y = -x + 6.5 + 5.5 = -x + 12$

Intersection: Solve: $6.75 - \frac{1}{2}x = 12 - x$
$\frac{1}{2}x = 5.25 \Rightarrow x = 10.5$
$y = 12 - 10.5 = 1.5$

Circumcenter: $O(10.5, 1.5)$

2. In $\triangle ABC$, the circumcenter is at $(3,4)$ and vertex $A$ is at $(1,2)$. Find the distance from circumcenter to vertex $B$.

The distance from circumcenter to any vertex is the circumradius $R$.
$R = \sqrt{(3-1)^2 + (4-2)^2} = \sqrt{4 + 4} = \sqrt{8} = 2\sqrt{2} \approx 2.828$

Therefore, the distance from circumcenter to vertex $B$ is $2\sqrt{2}$ units.

3. Determine the type of triangle where the circumcenter lies outside the triangle.

The circumcenter lies outside the triangle in an obtuse triangle (a triangle with one obtuse angle greater than 90°).

  • Acute triangle: circumcenter inside
  • Right triangle: circumcenter on hypotenuse
  • Obtuse triangle: circumcenter outside
4. Find the equation of the circumcircle of $\triangle ABC$ with vertices $A(0,0)$, $B(4,0)$, and $C(2,3)$.

Circumcenter: Intersection of perpendicular bisectors.

Midpoint of $AB$: $(2, 0)$
Midpoint of $AC$: $(1, 1.5)$

Perp. bisector of $AB$ (horizontal): $y = 0$
Perp. bisector of $AC$: slope of $AC$ is $3/2$, so perp. slope is $-2/3$.
Equation: $y - 1.5 = -\frac{2}{3}(x - 1) \Rightarrow y = -\frac{2}{3}x + \frac{2}{3} + 1.5 = -\frac{2}{3}x + \frac{7}{3}$

Intersection: $0 = -\frac{2}{3}x + \frac{7}{3} \Rightarrow x = \frac{7}{2} = 3.5$
Circumcenter: $O(3.5, 0)$

Radius: $R = \sqrt{(3.5-0)^2 + (0-0)^2} = 3.5$

Equation: $(x - 3.5)^2 + (y - 0)^2 = (3.5)^2$
$(x - 3.5)^2 + y^2 = 12.25$

5. In $\triangle ABC$, if the circumcenter coincides with the centroid, what can you conclude about the triangle?

If the circumcenter coincides with the centroid, the triangle must be equilateral. In an equilateral triangle, all four centers (circumcenter, centroid, orthocenter, and incenter) coincide at the same point.

Key Takeaways

  • The circumcenter is the intersection point of the three perpendicular bisectors of a triangle's sides.
  • The circumcenter is equidistant from all three vertices of the triangle.
  • The circumcircle passes through all three vertices with the circumcenter as its center.
  • The position of the circumcenter depends on the triangle type: inside (acute), on hypotenuse (right), or outside (obtuse).
  • The circumradius $R$ can be calculated using formulas: $R = \frac{abc}{4K}$ where $K$ is the area.
  • The circumcenter has important applications in navigation, computer graphics, and engineering.
  • In an equilateral triangle, the circumcenter coincides with the centroid and orthocenter.
