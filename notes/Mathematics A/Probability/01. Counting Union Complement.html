Counting Elements in Unions and Complements

Core Concept: Inclusion-Exclusion Principle

The Inclusion-Exclusion Principle is a fundamental counting technique used to calculate the size of the union of sets or the complement of a set. It accounts for elements that belong to multiple sets to avoid double-counting.

Union of Two Sets

$n(A \cup B) = n(A) + n(B) - n(A \cap B)$

Complement of a Set

$n(A^c) = n(U) - n(A)$

Where U is the universal set

These formulas are essential for counting problems in probability, combinatorics, and set theory.

Detailed Examples

Example 1: Union of Two Sets

In a class of 30 students, 18 play soccer, 12 play basketball, and 5 play both. How many students play at least one sport?

n(Soccer ∪ Basketball) = n(Soccer) + n(Basketball) - n(Soccer ∩ Basketball)
n(Soccer ∪ Basketball) = 18 + 12 - 5 = 25
25 students play at least one sport

Example 2: Complement

In a group of 50 people, 28 like chocolate ice cream. How many don't like chocolate ice cream?

n(Don't like chocolate) = n(Total) - n(Like chocolate)
n(Don't like chocolate) = 50 - 28 = 22
22 people don't like chocolate ice cream

Example 3: Multiple Applications

A survey of 100 students found that 60 like math, 45 like science, and 20 like both. How many like neither subject?

n(Math ∪ Science) = 60 + 45 - 20 = 85
n(Neither) = n(Total) - n(Math ∪ Science) = 100 - 85 = 15
15 students like neither math nor science

n(Math ∪ Physics) = n(Math) + n(Physics) - n(Math ∩ Physics)
n(Math ∪ Physics) = 25 + 20 - 10 = 35
35 students study at least one subject

Out of 100 people surveyed, 60 like pizza, 45 like burgers, and 25 like both. How many like neither?

n(Pizza ∪ Burgers) = 60 + 45 - 25 = 80
n(Neither) = 100 - 80 = 20
20 people like neither pizza nor burgers
3

In a group of 50 employees, 30 have health insurance, 20 have dental insurance, and 15 have both. How many have at least one type of insurance?

n(Health ∪ Dental) = 30 + 20 - 15 = 35
35 employees have at least one type of insurance
4

n(Fiction ∪ Hardcover) = 120 + 80 - 40 = 160
n(Neither) = 200 - 160 = 40
40 books are neither fiction nor hardcover

Key Takeaways

  • The Inclusion-Exclusion Principle prevents double-counting elements in multiple sets
  • Union size = sum of individual sizes - size of intersection
  • Complement size = universal set size - set size
  • These principles are fundamental to probability and combinatorics
  • Always account for overlapping elements when counting
