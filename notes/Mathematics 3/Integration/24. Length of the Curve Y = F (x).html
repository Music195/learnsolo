Length of the Curve y = f(x)

üìê Concepts

The arc length of the curve \( y = f(x) \) from \( x = a \) to \( x = b \) is the total distance traveled along the curve. The formula is derived by considering the curve as a parametric curve where \( x = t \), \( y = f(t) \):

\( L = \int_a^b \sqrt{1 + [f'(x)]^2} \, dx \)

This can also be written as:

\( L = \int_a^b \sqrt{1 + \left(\frac{dy}{dx}\right)^2} \, dx \)
Geometric Interpretation: The integrand \( \sqrt{1 + [f'(x)]^2} \) represents the length of an infinitesimal segment ds where \( ds = \sqrt{(dx)^2 + (dy)^2} = \sqrt{1 + (dy/dx)^2} \, dx \).

üîç Examples

Example 1: Straight Line

Find the arc length of \( y = x \) from x=0 to x=1.

\( f'(x) = 1 \)

\( L = \int_0^1 \sqrt{1 + 1^2} \, dx = \int_0^1 \sqrt{2} \, dx = \sqrt{2} \)

Example 2: Semicircle

Find the arc length of \( y = \sqrt{r^2 - x^2} \) (upper semicircle) from x=-r to x=r.

\( f'(x) = \frac{1}{2}(r^2 - x^2)^{-1/2} \cdot (-2x) = -\frac{x}{\sqrt{r^2 - x^2}} \)

\( L = \int_{-r}^r \sqrt{1 + \left(-\frac{x}{\sqrt{r^2 - x^2}}\right)^2} \, dx = \int_{-r}^r \sqrt{1 + \frac{x^2}{r^2 - x^2}} \, dx \)

\( = \int_{-r}^r \sqrt{\frac{r^2 - x^2 + x^2}{r^2 - x^2}} \, dx = \int_{-r}^r \sqrt{\frac{r^2}{r^2 - x^2}} \, dx = \int_{-r}^r \frac{r}{\sqrt{r^2 - x^2}} \, dx \)

\( = r [\arcsin(x/r)]_{-r}^r = r (\arcsin(1) - \arcsin(-1)) = r (\pi/2 - (-\pi/2)) = r\pi \)

Example 3: Exponential Curve

Find the arc length of \( y = e^x \) from x=0 to x=1.

\( f'(x) = e^x \)

\( L = \int_0^1 \sqrt{1 + (e^x)^2} \, dx = \int_0^1 \sqrt{1 + e^{2x}} \, dx \)

This integral cannot be evaluated in elementary functions, but can be approximated numerically.

üöÄ Applications

  • Physics: Calculating distances along curved paths, cable lengths, spring extensions
  • Engineering: Determining material usage for curved components, wire lengths, conveyor belt lengths
  • Computer Graphics: Computing curve lengths for animation, pathfinding, and rendering
  • Surveying: Measuring distances along irregular terrain or roads
  • Manufacturing: Calculating cutting lengths for curved materials, pipe bending
  • Architecture: Measuring curved building elements, arch lengths, decorative curves

‚úèÔ∏è Practice Problems

Problem 1: Find the arc length of the curve \( y = \frac{x^2}{2} \) from x=0 to x=2.

\( f'(x) = x \)

\( L = \int_0^2 \sqrt{1 + x^2} \, dx \)

Using the formula: \( \int \sqrt{x^2 + a^2} \, dx = \frac{x}{2} \sqrt{x^2 + a^2} + \frac{a^2}{2} \ln|x + \sqrt{x^2 + a^2}| + C \)

\( L = \left[ \frac{x}{2} \sqrt{x^2 + 1} + \frac{1}{2} \ln|x + \sqrt{x^2 + 1}| \right]_0^2 \)

\( = \left( \frac{2}{2} \sqrt{4 + 1} + \frac{1}{2} \ln|2 + \sqrt{5}| \right) - \left( 0 + \frac{1}{2} \ln|0 + 1| \right) \)

\( = \left( \sqrt{5} + \frac{1}{2} \ln(2 + \sqrt{5}) \right) - \frac{1}{2} \ln 1 = \sqrt{5} + \frac{1}{2} \ln(2 + \sqrt{5}) \)

\( \approx 2.236 + 0.5 \times 1.443 = 2.236 + 0.722 = 2.958 \)
Problem 2: Find the arc length of the curve \( y = \ln(\sec x) \) from x=0 to x=œÄ/4.

\( f'(x) = \frac{1}{\sec x} \cdot \sec x \tan x = \tan x \)

\( L = \int_0^{\pi/4} \sqrt{1 + \tan^2 x} \, dx = \int_0^{\pi/4} \sqrt{\sec^2 x} \, dx = \int_0^{\pi/4} \sec x \, dx \)

\( = [\ln|\sec x + \tan x|]_0^{\pi/4} = \ln|\sec(\pi/4) + \tan(\pi/4)| - \ln|\sec 0 + \tan 0| \)

\( = \ln|\sqrt{2} + 1| - \ln|1 + 0| = \ln(\sqrt{2} + 1) - \ln 1 = \ln(\sqrt{2} + 1) \)

\( \approx \ln(1.414 + 1) = \ln(2.414) \approx 0.881 \)
Problem 3: Find the arc length of the curve \( y = \frac{1}{4}x^2 - \frac{1}{2} \ln x \) from x=1 to x=2.

\( f'(x) = \frac{1}{2}x - \frac{1}{2x} = \frac{1}{2} \left(x - \frac{1}{x}\right) \)

\( L = \int_1^2 \sqrt{1 + \left[\frac{1}{2} \left(x - \frac{1}{x}\right)\right]^2} \, dx = \int_1^2 \sqrt{1 + \frac{1}{4} \left(x - \frac{1}{x}\right)^2} \, dx \)

\( = \int_1^2 \sqrt{1 + \frac{1}{4} \left(x^2 - 2 + \frac{1}{x^2}\right)} \, dx = \int_1^2 \sqrt{1 + \frac{1}{4} \left(x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2} - 2\right)} \, dx \)

\( = \int_1^2 \sqrt{1 + \frac{x^2}{4} + \frac{1}{4x^2} - \frac{1}{2}} \, dx = \int_1^2 \sqrt{\frac{1}{2} + \frac{x^2}{4} + \frac{1}{4x^2}} \, dx \)

\( = \int_1^2 \sqrt{\frac{2x^2 + x^2 + 1}{4x^2}} \, dx = \int_1^2 \sqrt{\frac{3x^2 + 1}{4x^2}} \, dx = \int_1^2 \frac{\sqrt{3x^2 + 1}}{2x} \, dx \)

This integral requires advanced techniques and is typically evaluated numerically.

üéØ Key Takeaways

  • Arc length formula: \( L = \int_a^b \sqrt{1 + [f'(x)]^2} \, dx \)
  • The integrand represents ds/dx, the rate of change of arc length with respect to x
  • For straight lines, the formula reduces to the distance formula
  • For circles, it gives the expected arc length
  • The result is always positive, representing total distance along the curve
  • Integration often requires trigonometric identities, substitution, or numerical methods
  • The formula assumes the function is differentiable on [a, b]
