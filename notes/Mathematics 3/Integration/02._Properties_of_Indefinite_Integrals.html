Properties of Indefinite Integrals

Basic Properties

Indefinite integrals follow several important properties that make integration easier. These properties are derived from the corresponding properties of derivatives.

Property 1: Constant Multiple Rule

\( \int k f(x) \, dx = k \int f(x) \, dx \) where k is a constant

A constant factor can be moved outside the integral sign.

Property 2: Sum Rule

\( \int [f(x) + g(x)] \, dx = \int f(x) \, dx + \int g(x) \, dx \)

The integral of a sum is the sum of the integrals.

Property 3: Difference Rule

\( \int [f(x) - g(x)] \, dx = \int f(x) \, dx - \int g(x) \, dx \)

The integral of a difference is the difference of the integrals.

Why These Properties Work

These properties follow directly from the corresponding differentiation rules:

  • Constant Multiple: \( \frac{d}{dx}[k F(x)] = k \frac{d}{dx}[F(x)] \)
  • Sum Rule: \( \frac{d}{dx}[F(x) + G(x)] = \frac{d}{dx}[F(x)] + \frac{d}{dx}[G(x)] \)
  • Difference Rule: \( \frac{d}{dx}[F(x) - G(x)] = \frac{d}{dx}[F(x)] - \frac{d}{dx}[G(x)] \)

Since integration is the inverse of differentiation, these properties hold for integrals as well.

These properties help break down complex integrals into simpler parts.

Examples

Example 1: Constant Multiple Rule

Find \( \int 5x^3 \, dx \)

Using the constant multiple rule:

\( \int 5x^3 \, dx = 5 \int x^3 \, dx = 5 \cdot \frac{x^4}{4} + C = \frac{5}{4} x^4 + C \)

Example 2: Sum Rule

Find \( \int (x^2 + 3x + 1) \, dx \)

Using the sum rule:

\( \int (x^2 + 3x + 1) \, dx = \int x^2 \, dx + \int 3x \, dx + \int 1 \, dx \)

\( = \frac{x^3}{3} + 3 \cdot \frac{x^2}{2} + x + C = \frac{x^3}{3} + \frac{3}{2} x^2 + x + C \)

Example 3: Difference Rule

Find \( \int (x^3 - 2x^2 + 5) \, dx \)

Using the difference rule:

\( \int (x^3 - 2x^2 + 5) \, dx = \int x^3 \, dx - \int 2x^2 \, dx + \int 5 \, dx \)

\( = \frac{x^4}{4} - 2 \cdot \frac{x^3}{3} + 5x + C = \frac{x^4}{4} - \frac{2}{3} x^3 + 5x + C \)

Example 4: Combining Properties

Find \( \int 4(x^2 + 2x - 1) \, dx \)

First use the constant multiple rule, then the sum rule:

\( \int 4(x^2 + 2x - 1) \, dx = 4 \int (x^2 + 2x - 1) \, dx \)

\( = 4 \left( \int x^2 \, dx + \int 2x \, dx - \int 1 \, dx \right) \)

\( = 4 \left( \frac{x^3}{3} + 2 \cdot \frac{x^2}{2} - x + C \right) = 4 \left( \frac{x^3}{3} + x^2 - x + C \right) \)

\( = \frac{4}{3} x^3 + 4x^2 - 4x + C \)

Applications

  • Physics: Breaking down complex force functions into simpler components
  • Economics: Integrating cost and revenue functions with multiple terms
  • Engineering: Analyzing systems with multiple contributing factors
  • Chemistry: Modeling reaction rates with multiple concentration terms
  • Biology: Population models with birth and death rate components

Practice Problems

1. Find \( \int 6x^2 \, dx \)

Solution:

Using the constant multiple rule: \( \int 6x^2 \, dx = 6 \int x^2 \, dx = 6 \cdot \frac{x^3}{3} + C = 2x^3 + C \)

2. Find \( \int (x^3 + 4x) \, dx \)

Solution:

Using the sum rule: \( \int (x^3 + 4x) \, dx = \int x^3 \, dx + \int 4x \, dx = \frac{x^4}{4} + 4 \cdot \frac{x^2}{2} + C = \frac{x^4}{4} + 2x^2 + C \)

3. Find \( \int (2x^2 - 3x + 1) \, dx \)

Solution:

Using sum and difference rules: \( \int (2x^2 - 3x + 1) \, dx = \int 2x^2 \, dx - \int 3x \, dx + \int 1 \, dx \)

\( = 2 \cdot \frac{x^3}{3} - 3 \cdot \frac{x^2}{2} + x + C = \frac{2}{3} x^3 - \frac{3}{2} x^2 + x + C \)

4. Find \( \int 3(x^2 + 2x - 1) \, dx \)

Solution:

Combine constant multiple and sum rules: \( \int 3(x^2 + 2x - 1) \, dx = 3 \int (x^2 + 2x - 1) \, dx \)

\( = 3 \left( \int x^2 \, dx + \int 2x \, dx - \int 1 \, dx \right) = 3 \left( \frac{x^3}{3} + 2 \cdot \frac{x^2}{2} - x + C \right) \)

\( = 3 \left( \frac{x^3}{3} + x^2 - x + C \right) = x^3 + 3x^2 - 3x + C \)

5. Find \( \int \frac{2x^3 - x^2 + 3x - 1}{x} \, dx \)

Solution:

Rewrite as: \( \int \frac{2x^3 - x^2 + 3x - 1}{x} \, dx = \int (2x^2 - x + 3 - \frac{1}{x}) \, dx \)

\( = \int 2x^2 \, dx - \int x \, dx + \int 3 \, dx - \int \frac{1}{x} \, dx \)

\( = 2 \cdot \frac{x^3}{3} - \frac{x^2}{2} + 3x - \ln|x| + C = \frac{2}{3} x^3 - \frac{1}{2} x^2 + 3x - \ln|x| + C \)

Key Takeaways

  • Constant factors can be moved outside the integral: \( \int k f(x) \, dx = k \int f(x) \, dx \)
  • Integrals of sums are sums of integrals: \( \int [f(x) ± g(x)] \, dx = \int f(x) \, dx ± \int g(x) \, dx \)
  • These properties make complex integrals easier to solve
  • Always apply these properties before using specific integration rules
  • The constant of integration C is carried through all operations
