Distance Traveled on a Number Line

üìê Concepts

When a particle moves along a number line with velocity \( v(t) \) at time \( t \), the distance traveled between times \( t_1 \) and \( t_2 \) is found by integrating the absolute value of the velocity function:

\( s = \int_{t_1}^{t_2} |v(t)| \, dt \)

This formula accounts for the fact that distance is always positive, regardless of the direction of motion. The absolute value ensures that both forward and backward motion contribute positively to the total distance traveled.

Key Insight: Distance traveled ‚â† Displacement. While displacement considers direction and can be negative, distance traveled is always positive and represents the total path length covered.

üîç Examples

Example 1: Constant Velocity

A car moves at a constant speed of 60 km/h for 2 hours. Find the distance traveled.

Solution: Since velocity is constant and positive, \( v(t) = 60 \) km/h

\( s = \int_{0}^{2} |60| \, dt = \int_{0}^{2} 60 \, dt = 60 \times 2 = 120 \) km

Example 2: Piecewise Velocity

A particle has velocity \( v(t) = \begin{cases} 2t & 0 \leq t \leq 2 \\ -t + 4 & 2 < t \leq 4 \end{cases} \). Find distance from t=0 to t=4.

\( s = \int_{0}^{2} |2t| \, dt + \int_{2}^{4} |-t + 4| \, dt = \int_{0}^{2} 2t \, dt + \int_{2}^{4} (t - 4) \, dt \)

\( = \left[t^2\right]_{0}^{2} + \left[\frac{1}{2}t^2 - 4t\right]_{2}^{4} = (4 - 0) + \left(8 - 16 - \frac{1}{2}(4) + 8\right) = 4 + (8 - 16 - 2 + 8) = 4 + (-2) = 2 \)

üéØ Key Takeaways

  • Distance traveled is always positive and equals \( \int |v(t)| dt \)
  • Displacement considers direction and equals \( \int v(t) dt \)
  • The absolute value accounts for changes in direction of motion
  • For velocity functions that never change sign, distance = |displacement|
  • Integration of absolute value requires careful analysis of where the function is positive/negative
