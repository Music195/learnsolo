Definite Integrals of Even and Odd Functions

Definite Integrals of Even and Odd Functions

Even and odd functions have special symmetry properties that make their definite integrals over symmetric intervals particularly simple to evaluate. These properties can significantly simplify integration problems and provide important insights into function behavior.

Why These Properties Matter:

These theorems allow us to evaluate integrals over symmetric intervals without performing the full integration, and they help us understand the geometric meaning of symmetry in integration.

Even and Odd Functions

Definitions

Even Function: A function \( f(x) \) is even if \( f(-x) = f(x) \) for all x in its domain.

Odd Function: A function \( f(x) \) is odd if \( f(-x) = -f(x) \) for all x in its domain.

Examples

  • Even functions: \( f(x) = x^2 \), \( f(x) = \cos x \), \( f(x) = |x| \)
  • Odd functions: \( f(x) = x^3 \), \( f(x) = \sin x \), \( f(x) = x \)
  • Neither: \( f(x) = x^2 + x \), \( f(x) = e^x \)

Integration Theorems

Theorem 1: Even Functions

Property: If \( f(x) \) is even, then:

\( \int_{-a}^{a} f(x) \, dx = 2 \int_{0}^{a} f(x) \, dx \)

Geometric Interpretation:

The area under the curve from -a to a is twice the area from 0 to a, because the function is symmetric with respect to the y-axis.

Theorem 2: Odd Functions

Property: If \( f(x) \) is odd, then:

\( \int_{-a}^{a} f(x) \, dx = 0 \)

Geometric Interpretation:

The positive and negative areas cancel each other out due to the function's antisymmetry.

Proofs

Proof of Even Function Theorem

Let \( f(x) \) be even. Then \( f(-x) = f(x) \).

\( \int_{-a}^{a} f(x) \, dx = \int_{-a}^{0} f(x) \, dx + \int_{0}^{a} f(x) \, dx \)

Substitute \( u = -x \) in the first integral: \( \int_{-a}^{0} f(x) \, dx = \int_{a}^{0} f(-u) (-du) = \int_{a}^{0} f(u) (-du) = \int_{0}^{a} f(u) \, du \)

Therefore: \( \int_{-a}^{a} f(x) \, dx = \int_{0}^{a} f(u) \, du + \int_{0}^{a} f(x) \, dx = 2 \int_{0}^{a} f(x) \, dx \)

Proof of Odd Function Theorem

Let \( f(x) \) be odd. Then \( f(-x) = -f(x) \).

\( \int_{-a}^{a} f(x) \, dx = \int_{-a}^{0} f(x) \, dx + \int_{0}^{a} f(x) \, dx \)

Substitute \( u = -x \) in the first integral: \( \int_{-a}^{0} f(x) \, dx = \int_{a}^{0} f(-u) (-du) = \int_{a}^{0} [-f(u)] (-du) = \int_{a}^{0} -f(u) (-du) = \int_{0}^{a} f(u) \, du \)

Therefore: \( \int_{-a}^{a} f(x) \, dx = \int_{0}^{a} f(u) \, du + \int_{0}^{a} f(x) \, dx = \int_{0}^{a} f(x) \, dx - \int_{0}^{a} f(x) \, dx = 0 \)

Examples

Example 1: Even Function

Evaluate \( \int_{-2}^{2} (x^2 + 1) \, dx \)

Solution:

First, check if the function is even: \( f(-x) = (-x)^2 + 1 = x^2 + 1 = f(x) \), so it's even.

\( \int_{-2}^{2} (x^2 + 1) \, dx = 2 \int_{0}^{2} (x^2 + 1) \, dx = 2 \left[ \frac{x^3}{3} + x \right]_0^2 = 2 \left( \frac{8}{3} + 2 \right) = 2 \left( \frac{8}{3} + \frac{6}{3} \right) = 2 \cdot \frac{14}{3} = \frac{28}{3} \)

Example 2: Odd Function

Evaluate \( \int_{-3}^{3} x^3 \, dx \)

Solution:

First, check if the function is odd: \( f(-x) = (-x)^3 = -x^3 = -f(x) \), so it's odd.

\( \int_{-3}^{3} x^3 \, dx = 0 \)

Example 3: Neither Even nor Odd

Evaluate \( \int_{-1}^{1} (x^2 + x) \, dx \)

Solution:

Check: \( f(-x) = (-x)^2 + (-x) = x^2 - x \), which is neither f(x) nor -f(x), so neither even nor odd.

Compute directly: \( \int_{-1}^{1} (x^2 + x) \, dx = \left[ \frac{x^3}{3} + \frac{x^2}{2} \right]_{-1}^{1} = \left( \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{2} \right) - \left( -\frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{2} \right) = \frac{5}{6} - \left( -\frac{1}{6} \right) = \frac{5}{6} + \frac{1}{6} = 1 \)

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Evaluate \( \int_{-4}^{4} \cos x \, dx \)

Solution:

cos(x) is even because cos(-x) = cos(x).

\( \int_{-4}^{4} \cos x \, dx = 2 \int_{0}^{4} \cos x \, dx = 2 [\sin x]_0^4 = 2 (\sin 4 - \sin 0) = 2 \sin 4 \)

Problem 2

Evaluate \( \int_{-5}^{5} x^4 \, dx \)

Solution:

x⁴ is even because (-x)⁴ = x⁴.

\( \int_{-5}^{5} x^4 \, dx = 2 \int_{0}^{5} x^4 \, dx = 2 \left[ \frac{x^5}{5} \right]_0^5 = 2 \cdot \frac{5^5}{5} = 2 \cdot \frac{3125}{5} = 2 \cdot 625 = 1250 \)

Problem 3

Evaluate \( \int_{-2}^{2} \sin x \, dx \)

Solution:

sin(x) is odd because sin(-x) = -sin(x).

\( \int_{-2}^{2} \sin x \, dx = 0 \)

Problem 4

Evaluate \( \int_{-3}^{3} |x| \, dx \)

Solution:

|x| is even because |-x| = |x|.

\( \int_{-3}^{3} |x| \, dx = 2 \int_{0}^{3} |x| \, dx = 2 \int_{0}^{3} x \, dx = 2 \left[ \frac{x^2}{2} \right]_0^3 = 2 \cdot \frac{9}{2} = 9 \)

Key Takeaways

  • Even functions: \( f(-x) = f(x) \), integral from -a to a = 2∫ from 0 to a
  • Odd functions: \( f(-x) = -f(x) \), integral from -a to a = 0
  • These properties only apply when the interval is symmetric about zero
  • Even functions are symmetric with respect to the y-axis
  • Odd functions are antisymmetric (rotated 180° about origin)
  • Always check if a function is even/odd before applying these theorems
  • These properties can greatly simplify definite integration
  • Geometric interpretation: even functions have equal areas on both sides, odd functions have canceling areas
