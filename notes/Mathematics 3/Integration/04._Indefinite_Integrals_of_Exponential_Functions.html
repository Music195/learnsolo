Indefinite Integrals of Exponential Functions

Basic Exponential Integrals

The most fundamental exponential integrals involve the natural exponential function and general exponential functions:

Basic Rules:
  • \( \int e^x \, dx = e^x + C \)
  • \( \int a^x \, dx = \frac{a^x}{\ln a} + C \) for \( a > 0, a \neq 1 \)

Why These Rules Work

These integrals are derived directly from the derivatives of exponential functions:

  • \( \frac{d}{dx}[e^x] = e^x \), so \( \int e^x \, dx = e^x + C \)
  • \( \frac{d}{dx}[a^x] = a^x \ln a \), so \( \int a^x \, dx = \frac{a^x}{\ln a} + C \)

The natural exponential function e^x is its own derivative and antiderivative.

Advanced Exponential Integrals

More complex exponential integrals often require substitution or other techniques:

Using Substitution:
  • \( \int e^{kx} \, dx = \frac{1}{k} e^{kx} + C \)
  • \( \int a^{kx} \, dx = \frac{a^{kx}}{k \ln a} + C \)
  • \( \int e^{ax + b} \, dx = \frac{1}{a} e^{ax + b} + C \)

Visual Guide

Exponential functions grow rapidly and their integrals show this growth pattern.

Examples

Example 1: Basic Natural Exponential

Find \( \int e^x \, dx \)

Direct integration: \( \int e^x \, dx = e^x + C \)

Check: \( \frac{d}{dx}[e^x + C] = e^x \)

Example 2: General Exponential

Find \( \int 2^x \, dx \)

Using the general formula: \( \int 2^x \, dx = \frac{2^x}{\ln 2} + C \)

Check: \( \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{2^x}{\ln 2} + C\right] = \frac{2^x \ln 2}{\ln 2} = 2^x \)

Example 3: Exponential with Coefficient

Find \( \int 3e^{2x} \, dx \)

Factor out the constant: \( \int 3e^{2x} \, dx = 3 \int e^{2x} \, dx \)

Let u = 2x, then du = 2 dx, so dx = du/2

\( = 3 \int e^u \cdot \frac{du}{2} = 3 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \int e^u \, du = \frac{3}{2} e^u + C = \frac{3}{2} e^{2x} + C \)

Example 4: Exponential with Linear Term

Find \( \int e^{3x + 1} \, dx \)

Let u = 3x + 1, then du = 3 dx, so dx = du/3

\( \int e^{3x + 1} \, dx = \int e^u \cdot \frac{du}{3} = \frac{1}{3} \int e^u \, du = \frac{1}{3} e^u + C = \frac{1}{3} e^{3x + 1} + C \)

Example 5: General Base with Coefficient

Find \( \int 5^x \, dx \)

Using the general formula: \( \int 5^x \, dx = \frac{5^x}{\ln 5} + C \)

Check: \( \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{5^x}{\ln 5} + C\right] = \frac{5^x \ln 5}{\ln 5} = 5^x \)

Integration Techniques for Exponential Functions

  • Direct Integration: For basic functions like e^x, a^x
  • Substitution: For e^{kx}, a^{kx}, e^{ax+b}
  • Constant Multiple Rule: Factor out constants before integrating
  • Combining with Other Rules: Use with sum/difference rules for complex expressions

Applications

  • Growth and Decay: Population growth, radioactive decay, compound interest
  • Physics: Temperature problems, electrical circuits, wave functions
  • Biology: Bacterial growth, drug concentration in blood
  • Finance: Investment growth, loan calculations
  • Chemistry: Reaction rates, pH calculations

Practice Problems

1. Find \( \int e^{4x} \, dx \)

Solution:

Let u = 4x, then du = 4 dx, so dx = du/4

\( \int e^{4x} \, dx = \int e^u \cdot \frac{du}{4} = \frac{1}{4} \int e^u \, du = \frac{1}{4} e^u + C = \frac{1}{4} e^{4x} + C \)

2. Find \( \int 3^x \, dx \)

Solution:

Using the general exponential formula: \( \int 3^x \, dx = \frac{3^x}{\ln 3} + C \)

Check: \( \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{3^x}{\ln 3} + C\right] = \frac{3^x \ln 3}{\ln 3} = 3^x \)

3. Find \( \int 2e^{x/2} \, dx \)

Solution:

Factor out the constant: \( \int 2e^{x/2} \, dx = 2 \int e^{x/2} \, dx \)

Let u = x/2, then du = (1/2) dx, so dx = 2 du

\( = 2 \int e^u \cdot 2 \, du = 4 \int e^u \, du = 4 e^u + C = 4 e^{x/2} + C \)

4. Find \( \int e^{2x + 3} \, dx \)

Solution:

Let u = 2x + 3, then du = 2 dx, so dx = du/2

\( \int e^{2x + 3} \, dx = \int e^u \cdot \frac{du}{2} = \frac{1}{2} \int e^u \, du = \frac{1}{2} e^u + C = \frac{1}{2} e^{2x + 3} + C \)

5. Find \( \int 4 \cdot 7^x \, dx \)

Solution:

Factor out the constant: \( \int 4 \cdot 7^x \, dx = 4 \int 7^x \, dx = 4 \cdot \frac{7^x}{\ln 7} + C = \frac{4}{\ln 7} \cdot 7^x + C \)

Check: \( \frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{4}{\ln 7} \cdot 7^x + C\right] = \frac{4}{\ln 7} \cdot 7^x \ln 7 = 4 \cdot 7^x \)

6. Find \( \int e^x + 2^x \, dx \)

Solution:

Use the sum rule: \( \int (e^x + 2^x) \, dx = \int e^x \, dx + \int 2^x \, dx \)

\( = e^x + \frac{2^x}{\ln 2} + C \)

Check: \( \frac{d}{dx}\left[e^x + \frac{2^x}{\ln 2} + C\right] = e^x + \frac{2^x \ln 2}{\ln 2} = e^x + 2^x \)

Key Takeaways

  • \( \int e^x \, dx = e^x + C \), the natural exponential is its own antiderivative
  • \( \int a^x \, dx = \frac{a^x}{\ln a} + C \) for general exponential functions
  • Use substitution for expressions like e^{kx} or e^{ax+b}
  • Factor out constants before applying the exponential rules
  • Combine with sum/difference rules for complex expressions
  • Always verify answers by differentiation
