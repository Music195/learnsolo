Properties of Limits of Sequences 1

üìê Concepts

If sequences \( \{a_n\} \) and \( \{b_n\} \) both converge to limits Œ± and Œ≤ respectively, then the following properties hold for their limits:

# Property Limit Conditions
1 \( \lim_{n \to \infty} k a_n \) = \( k\alpha \) k is a constant
2 \( \lim_{n \to \infty} (a_n + b_n) \) = \( \alpha + \beta \)
\( \lim_{n \to \infty} (a_n - b_n) \) = \( \alpha - \beta \)
3 \( \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n b_n \) = \( \alpha\beta \)
4 \( \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{a_n}{b_n} \) = \( \frac{\alpha}{\beta} \) Œ≤ ‚â† 0
Important Note: These properties only apply when both sequences converge. If either sequence diverges, these rules do not necessarily hold.

üîç Examples

Example 1: Constant Multiple

If \( \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 3 \), find \( \lim_{n \to \infty} 5a_n \).

\( \lim_{n \to \infty} 5a_n = 5 \cdot \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 5 \cdot 3 = 15 \)

Example 2: Sum and Difference

If \( \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 2 \) and \( \lim_{n \to \infty} b_n = 4 \), find \( \lim_{n \to \infty} (a_n + b_n) \) and \( \lim_{n \to \infty} (a_n - b_n) \).

\( \lim_{n \to \infty} (a_n + b_n) = 2 + 4 = 6 \)

\( \lim_{n \to \infty} (a_n - b_n) = 2 - 4 = -2 \)

Example 3: Product

If \( \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = \frac{1}{2} \) and \( \lim_{n \to \infty} b_n = 3 \), find \( \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n b_n \).

\( \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n b_n = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 3 = \frac{3}{2} \)

Example 4: Quotient

If \( \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 5 \) and \( \lim_{n \to \infty} b_n = 2 \), find \( \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{a_n}{b_n} \).

\( \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{a_n}{b_n} = \frac{5}{2} = 2.5 \)

‚úèÔ∏è Practice Problems

Problem 1: Given \( \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 3 \) and \( \lim_{n \to \infty} b_n = -2 \), find \( \lim_{n \to \infty} (2a_n + 3b_n) \).

\( \lim_{n \to \infty} (2a_n + 3b_n) = 2 \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n + 3 \lim_{n \to \infty} b_n = 2(3) + 3(-2) = 6 - 6 = 0 \)
Problem 2: Given \( \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 4 \) and \( \lim_{n \to \infty} b_n = 5 \), find \( \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{a_n^2}{b_n} \).

\( \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{a_n^2}{b_n} = \frac{\left(\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n\right)^2}{\lim_{n \to \infty} b_n} = \frac{4^2}{5} = \frac{16}{5} = 3.2 \)
Problem 3: Given \( \lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = 1 \) and \( \lim_{n \to \infty} b_n = 1 \), find \( \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{a_n + b_n}{a_n - b_n} \).

\( \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{a_n + b_n}{a_n - b_n} = \frac{\lim_{n \to \infty} (a_n + b_n)}{\lim_{n \to \infty} (a_n - b_n)} = \frac{1 + 1}{1 - 1} = \frac{2}{0} \)

This limit does not exist because the denominator approaches 0. The properties don't apply when the limit of the denominator is 0.

üéØ Key Takeaways

  • Constant multiple: \( \lim k a_n = k \lim a_n \)
  • Sum/Difference: \( \lim (a_n ¬± b_n) = \lim a_n ¬± \lim b_n \)
  • Product: \( \lim a_n b_n = (\lim a_n)(\lim b_n) \)
  • Quotient: \( \lim \frac{a_n}{b_n} = \frac{\lim a_n}{\lim b_n} \) (if \( \lim b_n ‚â† 0 \))
  • These properties only work when both sequences converge
  • Be careful with quotients when the denominator limit is zero
  • These properties extend to more complex expressions through repeated application
