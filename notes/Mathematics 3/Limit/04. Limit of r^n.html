üí° Examples

Example 1: Find $\lim_{n \to \infty} \left(\dfrac{3}{4}\right)^n$

Since $\left|\dfrac{3}{4}\right| = \dfrac{3}{4} < 1$, the limit is 0.
Example 2: Find $\lim_{n \to \infty} 2^n$

Since $2 > 1$, the sequence diverges to +‚àû.
Example 3: Find $\lim_{n \to \infty} (-0.8)^n$

Since $|-0.8| = 0.8 < 1$, the limit is 0 (despite the negative base).

üî¨ Applications

  • Compound Interest: When calculating compound interest over many periods, the formula involves terms like $(1 + r/n)^{nt}$ which behave according to these rules.
  • Population Growth: Exponential population models use sequences like $r^n$ where r > 1 indicates growth.
  • Radioactive Decay: The remaining amount of radioactive material follows $r^n$ where |r| < 1.
  • Geometric Series: The convergence of geometric series depends on whether |r| < 1.
  • Probability: In Markov chains and probability theory, transition probabilities often involve exponential terms.
