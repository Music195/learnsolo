Convergence and Divergence of Geometric Series

üìö Core Concepts

A geometric series is an infinite sum of terms where each term is obtained by multiplying the previous term by a constant ratio $r$. The general form is:

$a + ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + \cdots + ar^{n-1} + \cdots = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} ar^n$

where $a$ is the first term and $r$ is the common ratio.
The Common Ratio $r$: This is the factor by which each term is multiplied to get the next term. For example, in the series $3 + 6 + 12 + 24 + \cdots$, the common ratio is $r = 2$.

üìä Convergence Conditions

Condition Behavior Sum of Series
$|r| < 1$ Converges $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} ar^n = \dfrac{a}{1-r}$
$|r| \geq 1$ Diverges No finite sum
$a = 0$ Always Converges $0$
Key Insight: The convergence of a geometric series depends only on the absolute value of the common ratio |r|. If |r| ‚â• 1, the series diverges. If |r| < 1, it converges to a/(1-r).

üí° Examples

Example 1: Does $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} 3 \cdot \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^n$ converge?

Here $a = 3$, $r = \dfrac{1}{2}$. Since $|r| = \dfrac{1}{2} < 1$, the series converges to $\dfrac{3}{1 - \dfrac{1}{2}} = 6$.
Example 2: Does $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} 2 \cdot (-1.5)^n$ converge?

Here $a = 2$, $r = -1.5$. Since $|r| = 1.5 > 1$, the series diverges.
Example 3: Find the sum of $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{3^n}$.

This is a geometric series with $a = \dfrac{1}{3}$, $r = \dfrac{1}{3}$. Sum = $\dfrac{\dfrac{1}{3}}{1 - \dfrac{1}{3}} = \dfrac{1}{2}$.

üî¨ Applications

  • Finance: Present value calculations for annuities and perpetuities
  • Physics: Modeling repeated reflections in optics and acoustics
  • Computer Science: Analysis of algorithms and data structures
  • Probability: Expected values in infinite state Markov chains
  • Engineering: Signal processing and control systems analysis

üìù Practice Problems

Problem 1: Determine whether $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} 4 \cdot \left(\dfrac{2}{3}\right)^n$ converges or diverges. If it converges, find the sum.

Since $|2/3| = 2/3 < 1$, the series converges to $\dfrac{4}{1 - 2/3} = \dfrac{4}{1/3} = 12$.
Problem 2: Does $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \dfrac{1}{2^n}$ converge? Find the sum if it does.

Yes, it converges. Here a = 1, r = 1/2. Sum = $\dfrac{1}{1 - 1/2} = 2$.
Problem 3: Determine the convergence of $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} 5 \cdot (-2)^n$.

Since |-2| = 2 > 1, the series diverges.
Problem 4: Find the sum of $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \dfrac{3}{4^n}$.

This is a geometric series with a = 3/4, r = 1/4. Sum = $\dfrac{3/4}{1 - 1/4} = \dfrac{3/4}{3/4} = 1$.

üéØ Key Takeaways

  • Geometric series converge if and only if |r| < 1
  • The sum of a convergent geometric series is a/(1-r)
  • If |r| ‚â• 1, the series diverges (terms don't approach zero)
  • The ratio test for series convergence is based on geometric series
  • Geometric series have numerous applications in mathematics and science
  • Partial sums can help visualize the convergence behavior
