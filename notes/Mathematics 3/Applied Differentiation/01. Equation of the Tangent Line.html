Equation of the Tangent Line

Mathematical Foundations

The tangent line to a curve at a point is a straight line that touches the curve at exactly one point and has the same slope as the curve at that point. This concept is fundamental to differential calculus and has numerous applications in physics, engineering, and geometry.

Tangent Line Definition

A tangent line touches a curve at exactly one point

Slope equals the derivative at that point

Represents instantaneous rate of change

Fundamental to differential calculus

Point-Slope Form

$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$

$m = f'(a)$ is the slope

$(x_1, y_1) = (a, f(a))$ is the point

Most useful form for tangent lines

Slope-Intercept Form

$y = mx + b$

$m = f'(a)$, $b = f(a) - f'(a)a$

Easier for graphing

Requires calculating y-intercept

Normal Line

Perpendicular to tangent line

Slope is negative reciprocal

$m_{normal} = -\frac{1}{f'(a)}$

Important in optimization problems

Worked Examples

Example 1: Quadratic Function

Find the equation of the tangent line to $f(x) = x^2$ at x = 2.

Step 1: Find f(2) = (2)² = 4. Point is (2, 4)

Step 2: Find f'(x) = 2x. Slope f'(2) = 2×2 = 4

Step 3: Tangent line: y - 4 = 4(x - 2)

Step 4: Simplify: y = 4x - 8 + 4 = 4x - 4

Normal line: Slope = -1/4, so y - 4 = (-1/4)(x - 2)

Example 2: Trigonometric Function

Find the tangent line to $f(x) = \sin(x)$ at x = π/4.

Step 1: f(π/4) = sin(π/4) = √2/2 ≈ 0.707. Point is (π/4, √2/2)

Step 2: f'(x) = cos(x). Slope f'(π/4) = cos(π/4) = √2/2 ≈ 0.707

Step 3: Tangent line: y - √2/2 = (√2/2)(x - π/4)

Step 4: This is the equation in point-slope form

Example 3: Exponential Function

Find the tangent line to $f(x) = e^x$ at x = 0.

Step 1: f(0) = e⁰ = 1. Point is (0, 1)

Step 2: f'(x) = e^x. Slope f'(0) = e⁰ = 1

Step 3: Tangent line: y - 1 = 1(x - 0) = x

Step 4: y = x + 1

Note: At x = 0, the tangent line to e^x is y = x + 1

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Find the equation of the tangent line to $f(x) = x^3 - 2x + 1$ at x = 1.

Step 1: f(1) = (1)³ - 2(1) + 1 = 1 - 2 + 1 = 0. Point: (1, 0)

Step 2: f'(x) = 3x² - 2. Slope f'(1) = 3(1)² - 2 = 1

Step 3: Tangent line: y - 0 = 1(x - 1) ⇒ y = x - 1

Normal line: Slope = -1, so y - 0 = -1(x - 1) ⇒ y = -x + 1

Problem 2

Find the tangent line to $f(x) = \sqrt{x}$ at x = 4.

Step 1: f(4) = √4 = 2. Point: (4, 2)

Step 2: f'(x) = 1/(2√x). Slope f'(4) = 1/(2√4) = 1/(2×2) = 1/4

Step 3: Tangent line: y - 2 = (1/4)(x - 4)

Step 4: y = (1/4)x - 1 + 2 = (1/4)x + 1

Problem 3

Find both tangent and normal lines to $f(x) = x^2 + 3x - 1$ at x = -1.

Step 1: f(-1) = (-1)² + 3(-1) - 1 = 1 - 3 - 1 = -3. Point: (-1, -3)

Step 2: f'(x) = 2x + 3. Slope f'(-1) = 2(-1) + 3 = 1

Step 3: Tangent line: y - (-3) = 1(x - (-1)) ⇒ y + 3 = x + 1 ⇒ y = x - 2

Step 4: Normal line slope = -1/1 = -1. y + 3 = -1(x + 1) ⇒ y = -x - 4

Problem 4

Find the tangent line to $f(x) = \ln(x)$ at x = 1.

Step 1: f(1) = ln(1) = 0. Point: (1, 0)

Step 2: f'(x) = 1/x. Slope f'(1) = 1/1 = 1

Step 3: Tangent line: y - 0 = 1(x - 1) ⇒ y = x - 1

Note: The tangent line to ln(x) at x = 1 is y = x - 1

Key Takeaways

  • Tangent line formula: $y - f(a) = f'(a)(x - a)$
  • Slope equals derivative: The slope of the tangent line is $f'(a)$
  • Point of tangency: The line passes through $(a, f(a))$
  • Normal line: Perpendicular to tangent, slope is $-1/f'(a)$
  • Geometric interpretation: Instantaneous rate of change
  • Applications: Physics (velocity), optimization, curve sketching
  • Horizontal tangent: When $f'(a) = 0$, tangent is horizontal
  • Vertical tangent: When $f'(a)$ is undefined, tangent is vertical
1 $y - f(a) = f'(a)(x - a)$ (Point-Slope Form)
2 $y = f'(a)x + [f(a) - f'(a)a]$ (Slope-Intercept Form)
3 $y - f(a) = -\frac{1}{f'(a)}(x - a)$ (Normal Line)
