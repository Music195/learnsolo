Properties of Derivatives
Fundamental Rules for Differentiation:
If f(x) and g(x) are differentiable functions, and k is a constant:
1. Basic Properties
Constant Multiple Rule:
$\dfrac{d}{dx}[k \cdot f(x)] = k \cdot f'(x)$
Example: $\dfrac{d}{dx}(5x^2) = 5 \cdot 2x = 10x$
Sum Rule:
$\dfrac{d}{dx}[f(x) + g(x)] = f'(x) + g'(x)$
Example: $\dfrac{d}{dx}(x^2 + x) = 2x + 1$
Difference Rule:
$\dfrac{d}{dx}[f(x) - g(x)] = f'(x) - g'(x)$
Example: $\dfrac{d}{dx}(x^3 - x) = 3x^2 - 1$
2. Product Rule
$\dfrac{d}{dx}[f(x) \cdot g(x)] = f'(x)g(x) + f(x)g'(x)$

Alternative Form: $(uv)' = u'v + uv'$

Example: $\dfrac{d}{dx}(x^2 \cdot \sin x) = 2x \cdot \sin x + x^2 \cdot \cos x$
3. Quotient Rule
$\dfrac{d}{dx}\left[\dfrac{f(x)}{g(x)}\right] = \dfrac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2}$

Alternative Form: $\left(\dfrac{u}{v}\right)' = \dfrac{u'v - uv'}{v^2}$

Example: $\dfrac{d}{dx}\left(\dfrac{x^2}{x+1}\right) = \dfrac{2x(x+1) - x^2(1)}{(x+1)^2} = \dfrac{x^2 + 2x}{(x+1)^2}$
4. Chain Rule
$\dfrac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)$

Alternative Forms:
$\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \dfrac{dy}{du} \cdot \dfrac{du}{dx}$
$(f \circ g)'(x) = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)$

Example: $\dfrac{d}{dx}(\sin(x^2)) = \cos(x^2) \cdot 2x$
5. Visual Understanding

Product Rule: The derivative of a product is NOT the product of the derivatives

6. Common Patterns
  • Power Rule: $\dfrac{d}{dx}(x^n) = nx^{n-1}$
  • Exponential: $\dfrac{d}{dx}(e^x) = e^x$
  • Trigonometric: $\dfrac{d}{dx}(\sin x) = \cos x$, $\dfrac{d}{dx}(\cos x) = -\sin x$
  • Logarithmic: $\dfrac{d}{dx}(\ln x) = \dfrac{1}{x}$
  • Constants: $\dfrac{d}{dx}(c) = 0$
7. Step-by-Step Examples
Example 1 - Product Rule:
Find $\dfrac{d}{dx}(x^3 \sin x)$
Solution: $(x^3)' \sin x + x^3 (\sin x)' = 3x^2 \sin x + x^3 \cos x$
Example 2 - Quotient Rule:
Find $\dfrac{d}{dx}\left(\dfrac{x^2 + 1}{x - 1}\right)$
Solution: $\dfrac{(2x)(x-1) - (x^2 + 1)(1)}{(x-1)^2} = \dfrac{2x^2 - 2x - x^2 - 1}{(x-1)^2} = \dfrac{x^2 - 2x - 1}{(x-1)^2}$
Example 3 - Chain Rule:
Find $\dfrac{d}{dx}((x^2 + 3x + 1)^5)$
Solution: $5(x^2 + 3x + 1)^4 \cdot (2x + 3)$
8. Applications
Physics: Related rates, velocity/acceleration of composite motions
Economics: Marginal revenue = price × (1 + elasticity)
Biology: Population growth models with multiple factors
9. Practice Problems
Problem 1: Differentiate $f(x) = x^2 \cos x$ using the product rule.
Problem 2: Find $\dfrac{d}{dx}\left(\dfrac{x+1}{x^2-1}\right)$ using the quotient rule.
Problem 3: Differentiate $g(x) = \sin(x^3 + 2x)$ using the chain rule.
10. Key Takeaways
  • Derivative of a sum is sum of derivatives
  • Derivative of a product is NOT product of derivatives
  • Quotient rule has subtraction in numerator
  • Chain rule: derivative of composite is product of derivatives
  • Always check if functions are differentiable first
  • Practice combining multiple rules for complex functions
