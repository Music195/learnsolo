Definition of Derivative
Definition of the Derivative
For a function $f(x)$, the derivative at $x = a$ is defined as:
$$f'(a) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(a+h) - f(a)}{h} = \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x) - f(a)}{x - a}$$
1. Geometric Interpretation
Tangent and Secant Lines:
  • The derivative $f'(a)$ is the slope of the tangent line to $y = f(x)$ at $x = a$
  • The difference quotient $\frac{f(a+h) - f(a)}{h}$ is the slope of the secant line between $(a, f(a))$ and $(a+h, f(a+h))$
  • As $h \to 0$, the secant line approaches the tangent line

2. Conceptual Meaning
Physical Interpretation:
  • In physics, the derivative represents instantaneous rate of change (e.g., velocity is the derivative of position)
  • In economics, it represents marginal change (e.g., marginal cost)
  • In geometry, it gives the slope of the curve at a point
3. Notation
  • $f'(x)$: Lagrange notation
  • $\dfrac{df}{dx}$: Leibniz notation
  • $D_x f(x)$: Euler notation
4. Examples
Example 1: Find the derivative of $f(x) = x^2$ at $x = 3$ using the definition.
$$f'(3) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{(3+h)^2 - 9}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{9 + 6h + h^2 - 9}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{6h + h^2}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} (6 + h) = 6$$
Example 2: Find the derivative of $f(x) = \sin x$ at $x = 0$ using the definition.
$$f'(0) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\sin(h) - 0}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\sin h}{h} = 1$$
Example 3: Find the derivative of $f(x) = e^x$ at $x = 0$ using the definition.
$$f'(0) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{e^{0+h} - e^0}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{e^h - 1}{h} = 1$$
5. Differentiability and Continuity
  • If $f$ is differentiable at $a$, then $f$ is continuous at $a$
  • Not all continuous functions are differentiable (e.g., $|x|$ at $x=0$)
6. Applications
Physics: Instantaneous velocity, acceleration
Economics: Marginal cost, marginal revenue
Biology: Growth rates
7. Practice Problems
Problem 1: Use the definition to find the derivative of $f(x) = x^3$ at $x = 2$.

8. Key Takeaways
  • The derivative is the limit of the difference quotient
  • It represents the slope of the tangent line
  • Physical meaning: instantaneous rate of change
  • If differentiable, then continuous
  • Not all continuous functions are differentiable
