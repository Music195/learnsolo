Core Concept: Sum of Geometric Sequence

The sum of a geometric sequence combines all terms from the first term to the nth term. The formula depends on whether the common ratio is 1 or not, as this affects the algebraic manipulation.

Sum Formula (r ‚â† 1)

$S_n = \frac{a(1 - r^n)}{1 - r} = \frac{a(r^n - 1)}{r - 1}$

Where:
$S_n$ = sum of first n terms
$a$ = first term
$r$ = common ratio
$n$ = number of terms

Sum Formula (r = 1)

$S_n = na$

When r = 1, the sequence becomes constant: a, a, a, ...
Sum = n √ó a (arithmetic series)

Derivation of the Sum Formula

Multiplication Method

Let S = a + ar + ar¬≤ + ar¬≥ + ... + ar^(n-1)

Multiply both sides by r: rS = ar + ar¬≤ + ar¬≥ + ... + ar^n

Subtract: S - rS = a - ar^n

S(1 - r) = a(1 - r^n)

$S_n = \frac{a(1 - r^n)}{1 - r}$
The key insight is multiplying the sum by r and subtracting. This eliminates all middle terms, leaving only the first and last terms multiplied by appropriate factors.

Applications of Geometric Series

Real-World Applications
  • Compound Interest: Money growing at constant percentage rates
  • Population Growth: Populations increasing by constant factors
  • Radioactive Decay: Quantities decreasing by constant fractions
  • Bouncing Ball: Height decreasing by constant ratio each bounce
  • Loan Payments: Present value calculations with constant rates
