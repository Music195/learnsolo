General Term of a Geometric Sequence

Core Concept: Geometric Sequence

A geometric sequence is a sequence where each term after the first is obtained by multiplying the preceding term by a constant ratio. This common ratio is the key characteristic that distinguishes geometric sequences from arithmetic sequences.

General Term Formula

$a_n = ar^{n-1}$

Where:
$a_n$ = nth term
$a$ = first term
$r$ = common ratio
$n$ = term position

The nth term is obtained by multiplying the first term by the common ratio raised to the power of (n-1). This creates exponential growth or decay depending on whether |r| > 1 or |r| < 1.

Understanding the Common Ratio

Common Ratio Definition

The common ratio r is found by dividing any term by its preceding term:

$r = \frac{a_2}{a_1} = \frac{a_3}{a_2} = \frac{a_4}{a_3} = \dots$

For the sequence a, ar, ar², ar³, ...

Special Cases

When r = 1: All terms equal to a (constant sequence)

When r = -1: Alternating sequence: a, -a, a, -a, ...

When r = 0: Sequence becomes: a, 0, 0, 0, ... (after first term)

Worked Examples

1

Find the 8th term of the geometric sequence: 3, 6, 12, 24, ...

a = 3, r = 6÷3 = 2, n = 8
a₈ = a × r^(n-1)
a₈ = 3 × 2^(8-1)
a₈ = 3 × 2⁷
a₈ = 3 × 128 = 384
2

In a geometric sequence, the 3rd term is 24 and the 6th term is 192. Find the first term and common ratio.

a₃ = a × r² = 24
a₆ = a × r⁵ = 192
Divide second equation by first:
(a × r⁵) ÷ (a × r²) = 192 ÷ 24
r³ = 8 ⇒ r = 2
From a₃: a × 2² = 24
a × 4 = 24 ⇒ a = 6
3

Find which term of the sequence 2, 6, 18, 54, ... is equal to 4374.

a = 2, r = 6÷2 = 3
aₙ = 2 × 3^(n-1) = 4374
3^(n-1) = 4374 ÷ 2 = 2187
3^(n-1) = 3⁷ = 2187
n-1 = 7 ⇒ n = 8

Geometric vs Arithmetic Sequences

Arithmetic Sequence

Add constant difference

a, a+d, a+2d, a+3d, ...

Linear growth

Geometric Sequence

Multiply by constant ratio

a, ar, ar², ar³, ...

Exponential growth/decay

Key Takeaways

  • The general term formula $a_n = ar^{n-1}$ uses multiplication by powers of r
  • The common ratio r determines growth rate: |r| > 1 grows, |r| < 1 decays
  • Geometric sequences model exponential phenomena (population, interest, etc.)
  • The first term is when n=1: $a_1 = a$
  • Each term is r times the previous term
