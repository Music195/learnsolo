Centroid & Medians (重心・中線)

Triangle Geometry
In any triangle, the three medians (lines from each vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side) always intersect at a single point.

This intersection point is called the centroid.

The centroid divides each median in a 2 : 1 ratio, counting from the vertex.

Properties:
  • The centroid is the "center of gravity" of the triangle.
  • It always lies inside the triangle.
The centroid G is the intersection of the three medians.
Each median is divided in a 2 : 1 ratio by the centroid.
💡 Fact: The centroid G is the balance point of the triangle and is always inside the triangle.
