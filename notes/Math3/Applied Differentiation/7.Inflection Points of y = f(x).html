Inflection Points of \( y = f(x) \)

Application of Differentiation
Suppose \( f(x) \) has a second derivative \( f''(x) \).
  1. If \( f''(a) = 0 \) and the sign of \( f''(x) \) changes as \( x \) passes through \( a \), then the point \( (a, f(a)) \) is an inflection point of the curve \( y = f(x) \).
  2. If \( (a, f(a)) \) is an inflection point of \( y = f(x) \), then \( f''(a) = 0 \).
Note: The converse of 2 does not always hold.
That is, even if \( f''(a) = 0 \), the point \( (a, f(a)) \) is not necessarily an inflection point of \( y = f(x) \).
