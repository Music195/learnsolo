Equation of the Tangent Line

Application of Differentiation
For a curve \( y = f(x) \), the equation of the tangent line at the point \( (a, f(a)) \) is:
\( y - f(a) = f'(a)\,(x - a) \)
If the function \( y = f(x) \) is differentiable at \( x = a \), the derivative \( f'(a) \) gives the slope of the tangent line to the curve at the point \( (a, f(a)) \).
