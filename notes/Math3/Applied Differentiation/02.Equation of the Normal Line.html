Equation of the Normal Line

Application of Differentiation
For a curve \ y = f(x) \, the equation of the normal line at the point \( (a, f(a)) \) (when \( f'(a) \neq 0 \)) is:
\( y - f(a) = -\dfrac{1}{f'(a)}\,(x - a) \)
The normal line at \( (a, f(a)) \) is perpendicular to the tangent line at that point. The product of their slopes is \(-1\).
Therefore, the slope of the normal line is \( -\dfrac{1}{f'(a)} \).
