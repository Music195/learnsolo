Properties of Limits of Functions

Suppose that \[ \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = \alpha, \quad \lim_{x \to a} g(x) = \beta \] where \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \) are finite values.
1.  \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} k f(x) = k\alpha \) (where \( k \) is a constant)
2.  \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} \{ f(x) + g(x) \} = \alpha + \beta \)
   \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} \{ f(x) - g(x) \} = \alpha - \beta \)
3.  \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} f(x)g(x) = \alpha\beta \)
4.  \( \displaystyle \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \frac{\alpha}{\beta} \) provided \( \beta \neq 0 \)
Note:
These properties also hold for limits as \( x \to \infty \) or \( x \to -\infty \), and are analogous to the properties for sequences.
Additional Properties:

5.  If \( f(x) \leq g(x) \) always holds near \( x = a \), then \( \alpha \leq \beta \).
6.  If \( f(x) \leq h(x) \leq g(x) \) always holds near \( x = a \), and \( \lim_{x \to a} f(x) = \lim_{x \to a} g(x) = \alpha \), then \[ \lim_{x \to a} h(x) = \alpha \]
Note:
Property 6 is known as the Squeeze Theorem.
For properties 5 and 6, "near \( x = a \)" can be interpreted as "for all sufficiently large \( x \)" when considering limits as \( x \to \infty \) or \( x \to -\infty \). The same results hold in those cases.
