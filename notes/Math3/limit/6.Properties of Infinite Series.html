Properties of Infinite Series

Suppose the infinite series \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n \) and \( \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n \) both converge,
and let \[ \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n = S, \quad \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n = T. \]
1.  \( \displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} k a_n = kS \) (where \( k \) is a constant)
2.  \( \displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (a_n + b_n) = S + T \)   ,   \( \displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (a_n - b_n) = S - T \)
