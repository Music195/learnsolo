5. Convergence and Divergence of Geometric Series

The convergence or divergence of the infinite geometric series
$a + ar + ar^2 + \cdots + ar^{n-1} + \cdots$ is as follows:
$a \neq 0$ $|r| < 1$ → converges, sum is $\displaystyle \frac{a}{1-r}$
$|r| \geq 1$ → diverges
$a = 0$ Always converges, sum is $0$
The infinite geometric series $a + ar + ar^2 + \cdots + ar^{n-1} + \cdots$ converges
↔ $a = 0$ or $|r| < 1$
