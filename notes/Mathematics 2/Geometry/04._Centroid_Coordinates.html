Centroid Coordinates
The centroid is the point where the three medians of a triangle intersect. It divides each median in the ratio 2:1 and is located at the average of the three vertices' coordinates.
Centroid Formula: $$G\left(\frac{x_1 + x_2 + x_3}{3}, \frac{y_1 + y_2 + y_3}{3}\right)$$
$$\text{Where A(x₁,y₁), B(x₂,y₂), C(x₃,y₃) are vertices}$$
Median Property: $$\text{G divides each median in ratio 2:1}$$
$$\text{AG:GM = 2:1 (vertex to midpoint)}$$
Center of Mass: $$\text{Centroid is the balance point of triangle}$$
$$\text{All medians concur at G}$$
The centroid G is the intersection point of the three medians. It divides each median in the ratio 2:1, with the longer segment being from vertex to centroid. The centroid is also the center of mass of the triangle.

Mathematical Foundation

The centroid has several important mathematical properties:

  • Median Concurrency: The three medians of any triangle intersect at a single point called the centroid
  • Division Ratio: The centroid divides each median in the ratio 2:1, with the longer segment from vertex to centroid
  • Coordinate Formula: The centroid coordinates are the average of the three vertex coordinates
  • Center of Mass: The centroid is the balance point of the triangle
  • Area Properties: The centroid divides the triangle into six smaller triangles of equal area
  • Vector Properties: In vector geometry, the centroid is (A + B + C)/3
\begin{aligned} \text{Derivation:} \\ \text{Let G divide median from A to midpoint M of BC in ratio AG:GM = 2:1} \\ \text{Using section formula:} \\ G = \left( \frac{2 \cdot M_x + 1 \cdot A_x}{2 + 1}, \frac{2 \cdot M_y + 1 \cdot A_y}{2 + 1} \right) \\ \text{Where M is midpoint of BC:} \\ M = \left( \frac{B_x + C_x}{2}, \frac{B_y + C_y}{2} \right) \\ \text{Substituting gives centroid formula} \end{aligned}

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Find the coordinates of the centroid of a triangle with vertices A(3,4), B(7,6), and C(5,9).

Solution:

Using centroid formula:

x = (3 + 7 + 5)/3 = 15/3 = 5

y = (4 + 6 + 9)/3 = 19/3 ≈ 6.333

Result: (5, 19/3)

Problem 2

Find the centroid of triangle ABC where A(1,1), B(5,2), C(3,7).

Solution:

Centroid coordinates:

x = (1 + 5 + 3)/3 = 9/3 = 3

y = (1 + 2 + 7)/3 = 10/3 ≈ 3.333

Result: (3, 10/3)

Problem 3

The centroid of triangle ABC is at (4,5). If A(2,3) and B(6,7), find coordinates of C.

Solution:

Let C be (x,y). Centroid G(4,5):

(2 + 6 + x)/3 = 4 ⇒ 8 + x = 12 ⇒ x = 4

(3 + 7 + y)/3 = 5 ⇒ 10 + y = 15 ⇒ y = 5

Result: C(4, 5)

Key Takeaways
  • Centroid is the intersection point of the three medians of a triangle
  • Centroid divides each median in the ratio 2:1, with longer segment from vertex to centroid
  • Centroid coordinates are the average of the three vertex coordinates
  • Centroid is the center of mass and balance point of the triangle
  • Formula works independently for x and y coordinates
  • Centroid exists for every triangle regardless of its shape or position
  • Used to find centers of triangles in coordinate geometry problems
  • Centroid divides triangle into six smaller triangles of equal area
