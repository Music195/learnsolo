Equation of a Circle
A circle is the set of all points in a plane that are equidistant from a fixed point called the center. The distance from the center to any point on the circle is called the radius.
General Form: $$(x - a)^2 + (y - b)^2 = r^2$$
Center: (a, b)
Radius: r
Origin Center: $$x^2 + y^2 = r^2$$
Center: (0, 0)
Special case
Expanded Form: $$x^2 + y^2 + Dx + Ey + F = 0$$
General equation
The circle equation represents all points (x, y) that are exactly r units away from the center point (a, b). This is the distance formula squared.

Mathematical Foundation

The circle equation is derived from the distance formula:

  • Distance Formula: Distance between two points (x₁,y₁) and (x₂,y₂) is √[(x₂-x₁)² + (y₂-y₁)²]
  • Circle Definition: All points (x,y) at fixed distance r from center (a,b)
  • Equation Derivation: √[(x-a)² + (y-b)²] = r, then square both sides
  • Completing Square: Convert general form x² + y² + Dx + Ey + F = 0 to standard form
  • Center Formula: a = -D/2, b = -E/2, r² = a² + b² - F
  • Geometric Properties: Circle is symmetric, equidistant points, constant curvature
\begin{aligned} \text{Derivation:} \\ \text{Distance from (x,y) to (a,b) equals r:} \\ \sqrt{(x - a)^2 + (y - b)^2} = r \\ \text{Square both sides:} \\ (x - a)^2 + (y - b)^2 = r^2 \\ \\ \text{Completing the square:} \\ x^2 + y^2 + Dx + Ey + F = 0 \\ (x + D/2)^2 + (y + E/2)^2 = (D/2)^2 + (E/2)^2 - F \end{aligned}

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Write the equation of a circle with center (3, -2) and radius 5 in standard form.

Solution:

Standard form: (x - a)² + (y - b)² = r²

Substitute a = 3, b = -2, r = 5

Result: (x - 3)² + (y + 2)² = 25

Problem 2

Convert x² + y² - 4x + 6y - 3 = 0 to standard form.

Solution:

Complete square for x: (x - 2)² - 4

Complete square for y: (y + 3)² - 9

Equation: (x - 2)² + (y + 3)² - 4 - 9 - 3 = 0

(x - 2)² + (y + 3)² = 16

Result: (x - 2)² + (y + 3)² = 16

Problem 3

Find the center and radius of (x + 1)² + (y - 4)² = 49.

Solution:

Center: (-1, 4)

Radius: √49 = 7

Result: Center (-1, 4), radius 7

Key Takeaways
  • Circle equation: (x - a)² + (y - b)² = r² represents all points at distance r from center (a,b)
  • Origin-centered circle: x² + y² = r² when center is at (0,0)
  • General form: x² + y² + Dx + Ey + F = 0 can be converted to standard form by completing square
  • Center coordinates: a = -D/2, b = -E/2 from general form
  • Radius formula: r = √(a² + b² - F) or r = √(r²) from standard form
  • Completing square: Group x and y terms, add/subtract constants to form perfect squares
  • Essential for analytic geometry, computer graphics, and geometric modeling
  • Circle properties: symmetric, constant radius, infinite points at fixed distance
