Circle and Regions
Circle inequalities divide the coordinate plane into distinct regions. The circle boundary separates points that satisfy the inequality from those that don't, with different shading patterns indicating the solution region.
Interior Region: $x^2 + y^2 < r^2$
Points inside circle, boundary excluded
Exterior Region: $x^2 + y^2 > r^2$
Points outside circle, boundary excluded
Inclusive Region: $x^2 + y^2 \leq r^2$
Points inside including boundary
Circle inequalities create disk regions (interior) and exterior regions. The inequality symbol determines whether the boundary circle is included in the solution set.
Types of Circle Inequalities

Interior Region

Points inside the circle (excluded)
$$x^2 + y^2 < r^2$$
Region: All points inside the circle
Boundary: Dashed circle (not included)
Area: πr² (disk area)
Open region: Boundary points excluded

Exterior Region

Points outside the circle (excluded)
$$x^2 + y^2 > r^2$$
Region: All points outside the circle
Boundary: Dashed circle (not included)
Area: Infinite (unbounded region)
Open region: Boundary points excluded

Inclusive Region

Points inside including the circle
$$x^2 + y^2 \leq r^2$$
Region: All points inside and on the circle
Boundary: Solid circle (included)
Area: πr² (closed disk)
Closed region: Boundary points included

Mathematical Foundation

Circle inequalities represent regions in the coordinate plane bounded by circles:

  • Standard form: (x - h)² + (y - k)² ≶ r² where (h,k) is center and r is radius
  • General form: x² + y² + Dx + Ey + F ≶ 0, complete the square to find center and radius
  • Interior region: x² + y² < r² represents open disk (boundary excluded)
  • Exterior region: x² + y² > r² represents points outside circle (boundary excluded)
  • Closed disk: x² + y² ≤ r² includes boundary points
  • Distance interpretation: √(x² + y²) is distance from origin to point (x,y)
  • Strict vs inclusive: < and > exclude boundary (open regions), ≤ and ≥ include boundary (closed regions)
  • Area formulas: Interior disk has area πr², exterior region is unbounded
  • Boundary behavior: Points on circle satisfy equality x² + y² = r²
  • Applications: Used in optimization, collision detection, and geometric constraints
\begin{aligned} \text{Circle inequalities:} \\ (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 &< r^2 \implies \text{Interior (open disk)} \\ (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 &> r^2 \implies \text{Exterior (unbounded)} \\ (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 &\leq r^2 \implies \text{Closed disk} \\ (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 &\geq r^2 \implies \text{Closed exterior} \\ \\ \text{Distance formula:} \\ d &= \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} \quad (\text{for center at origin}) \\ d &< r \implies \text{Interior point} \\ d &= r \implies \text{Boundary point} \\ d &> r \implies \text{Exterior point} \end{aligned}

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Graph the inequality x² + y² ≤ 4. Describe the solution region and calculate its area.

Solution:

Circle: center (0,0), radius r = 2

Inequality: x² + y² ≤ 4 (closed disk)

Region: All points inside and on the circle x² + y² = 4

Area: πr² = π(2)² = 4π

Result: Closed disk with area 4π square units

Problem 2

Graph (x+1)² + (y-1)² > 9 using the test point method.

Solution:

Circle: center (-1,1), radius r = 3

Inequality: (x+1)² + (y-1)² > 9 (exterior region)

Test point (0,0): (0+1)² + (0-1)² = 1 + 1 = 2 < 9? False

Since test point doesn't satisfy inequality, shade exterior region

Result: Region outside circle (x+1)² + (y-1)² = 9, excluding boundary

Problem 3

Determine the region defined by x² + y² ≥ 25.

Solution:

Circle: center (0,0), radius r = 5

Inequality: x² + y² ≥ 25 (closed exterior region)

Test point (0,0): 0 + 0 = 0 ≥ 25? False

Since test point doesn't satisfy inequality, shade exterior region

Result: Region outside and on circle x² + y² = 25

Key Takeaways
  • Circle inequalities divide the plane into interior (disk) and exterior regions
  • Strict inequalities (<, >) use dashed boundaries and exclude the circle
  • Inclusive inequalities (≤, ≥) use solid boundaries and include the circle
  • Interior regions (x² + y² < r²) are bounded disks with area πr²
  • Exterior regions (x² + y² > r²) are unbounded and extend infinitely
  • Test point method: substitute any point not on boundary to determine shading
  • Distance from origin √(x² + y²) determines whether point satisfies inequality
  • Essential for understanding regions, optimization, and geometric constraints
