Parallel and Perpendicular Lines
Parallel lines never intersect and maintain constant distance, while perpendicular lines intersect at right angles. These relationships are fundamental to coordinate geometry.
Parallel Lines: $$m_1 = m_2$$
Same slope, never intersect
Perpendicular Lines: $$m_1 \cdot m_2 = -1$$
Product of slopes = -1
Perpendicular Slope: $$m_2 = -\frac{1}{m_1}$$
Negative reciprocal
Parallel lines have identical slopes and never intersect. Perpendicular lines have slopes that are negative reciprocals of each other and intersect at 90° angles.

Mathematical Foundation

The relationships between parallel and perpendicular lines are based on slope properties:

  • Parallel Lines: Lines with identical slopes maintain constant separation and never intersect
  • Perpendicular Lines: Lines whose slopes multiply to -1 intersect at right angles
  • Slope Relationship: If line 1 has slope m₁, perpendicular line has slope -1/m₁
  • Geometric Interpretation: Parallel lines are equidistant, perpendicular lines form 90° angles
  • Coordinate Geometry: Essential for determining line relationships and constructing perpendiculars
  • Vector Interpretation: Parallel lines have proportional direction vectors, perpendicular lines have dot product zero
\begin{aligned} \text{Parallel Lines:} \\ \text{Slope m₁ = m₂} \\ \text{Equation: y = m₁x + b₁, y = m₁x + b₂} \\ \text{Distance between lines: } \frac{|b₂ - b₁|}{\sqrt{1 + m₁²}} \\ \\ \text{Perpendicular Lines:} \\ \text{Slope m₂ = -1/m₁} \\ \text{Product: m₁ × m₂ = -1} \\ \text{Intersection angle: 90°} \end{aligned}

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Determine if the lines y = 3x + 2 and y = 3x - 5 are parallel, perpendicular, or neither.

Solution:

Both lines have slope m = 3

Since slopes are equal, the lines are parallel

Result: Parallel lines

Problem 2

Determine if the lines y = 2x + 1 and y = (-1/2)x + 3 are parallel, perpendicular, or neither.

Solution:

Line 1 slope: m₁ = 2

Line 2 slope: m₂ = -1/2

Product: 2 × (-1/2) = -1

Result: Perpendicular lines

Problem 3

Find the equation of the line perpendicular to y = -2x + 5 that passes through the point (3, 1).

Solution:

Original slope: m₁ = -2

Perpendicular slope: m₂ = -1/(-2) = 1/2

Using point (3,1): y - 1 = (1/2)(x - 3)

y - 1 = (1/2)x - 3/2

y = (1/2)x - 3/2 + 1 = (1/2)x - 1/2

Result: y = (1/2)x - 1/2

Key Takeaways
  • Parallel lines have equal slopes (m₁ = m₂) and never intersect
  • Perpendicular lines have slopes that multiply to -1 (m₁ × m₂ = -1)
  • The slope of a perpendicular line is the negative reciprocal (-1/m₁)
  • Parallel lines maintain constant distance and have the same direction
  • Perpendicular lines intersect at exactly 90° angles
  • Horizontal and vertical lines are always perpendicular
  • Essential for coordinate geometry, construction, and spatial relationships
  • Used in architecture, engineering, and computer graphics
