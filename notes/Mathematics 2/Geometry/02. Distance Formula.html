Distance Formula
The distance formula calculates the straight-line distance between two points in a coordinate plane. It is derived from the Pythagorean theorem and is fundamental to coordinate geometry.
Two Points: $$d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$$
$$\text{Distance between } A(x_1,y_1) \text{ and } B(x_2,y_2)$$
From Origin: $$d = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$$
$$\text{Distance from } (0,0) \text{ to } (x,y)$$
3D Distance: $$d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2 + (z_2 - z_1)^2}$$
$$\text{Distance in three dimensions}$$
The distance formula is derived from the Pythagorean theorem: the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of squares of the other two sides. In coordinates, this becomes the distance between two points.

Mathematical Foundation

The distance formula is a direct application of the Pythagorean theorem in coordinate geometry:

  • Pythagorean Theorem: In a right triangle, c² = a² + b² where c is the hypotenuse
  • Coordinate Application: The differences in x and y coordinates form the legs of a right triangle
  • Vector Interpretation: Distance between points is the magnitude of the vector difference
  • 3D Extension: Simply adds z-coordinate difference squared
  • Circle Definition: All points at fixed distance from center form a circle
  • Triangle Properties: Used to verify triangle inequalities and calculate side lengths
\begin{aligned} \text{Derivation:} \\ \text{Points A(x₁,y₁), B(x₂,y₂)} \\ \text{Differences: Δx = x₂ - x₁, Δy = y₂ - y₁} \\ \text{Distance: d = √(Δx² + Δy²)} \\ \text{This is the hypotenuse of right triangle with legs |Δx|, |Δy|} \end{aligned}

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Find the distance between points A(2, 3) and B(5, 7).

Solution:

Δx = 5 - 2 = 3

Δy = 7 - 3 = 4

d = √(3² + 4²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5

Result: 5 units

Problem 2

Find the distance from the origin to the point (6, 8).

Solution:

d = √(6² + 8²) = √(36 + 64) = √100 = 10

Result: 10 units

Problem 3

Find the distance between points P(-1, 2) and Q(3, -4).

Solution:

Δx = 3 - (-1) = 4

Δy = -4 - 2 = -6

d = √(4² + (-6)²) = √(16 + 36) = √52 = 2√13 ≈ 7.21

Result: 2√13 units (or approximately 7.21)

Key Takeaways
  • Distance formula: √((x₂-x₁)² + (y₂-y₁)²) gives straight-line distance between two points
  • Derived from Pythagorean theorem applied to coordinate differences
  • Distance from origin: √(x² + y²) is a special case
  • Always yields positive distance (absolute value)
  • Extends to 3D: √((x₂-x₁)² + (y₂-y₁)² + (z₂-z₁)²)
  • Used in circle equations, triangle properties, and geometric proofs
  • Essential for coordinate geometry and analytic geometry
  • Distance between points is the magnitude of the vector joining them
