Exponent Laws
Exponent laws are fundamental rules for manipulating expressions with exponents. These laws allow us to simplify complex exponential expressions and solve equations involving powers.
Product Law: $$a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}$$
Quotient Law: $$\frac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n}$$
Power Law: $$(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$$
Product to Power: $$(ab)^n = a^n b^n$$

Product Law

a^m × a^n = a^(m+n)
When multiplying powers with the same base, add the exponents.

Quotient Law

a^m ÷ a^n = a^(m-n)
When dividing powers with the same base, subtract the exponents.

Power Law

(a^m)^n = a^(mn)
When raising a power to another power, multiply the exponents.

Product to Power

(ab)^n = a^n × b^n
When raising a product to a power, distribute the exponent to each factor.

Mathematical Foundation

The exponent laws are derived from the fundamental definition of exponentiation and the properties of multiplication:

  • Product Law: Based on repeated multiplication: a^m × a^n = a × a × ... × a (m times) × a × a × ... × a (n times) = a^(m+n)
  • Quotient Law: Division of powers: a^m ÷ a^n = (a × a × ... × a)/(a × a × ... × a) = a^(m-n)
  • Power Law: Raising to a power: (a^m)^n = a^m × a^m × ... × a^m (n times) = a^(m×n)
  • Product to Power: Distributive property: (ab)^n = ab × ab × ... × ab (n times) = a^n × b^n

These laws extend to rational exponents and can be proven using logarithms for real exponents.

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Simplify the expression: 5^3 × 5^4 ÷ 5^2

Solution:

5^3 × 5^4 ÷ 5^2 = 5^(3+4-2) = 5^5 = 3125

Using the laws step by step:

5^3 × 5^4 = 5^(3+4) = 5^7

5^7 ÷ 5^2 = 5^(7-2) = 5^5 = 3125

Problem 2

Simplify: (2^2)^3 × 2^4

Solution:

(2^2)^3 × 2^4 = 2^(2×3) × 2^4 = 2^6 × 2^4 = 2^(6+4) = 2^10 = 1024

Step by step:

(2^2)^3 = 2^(2×3) = 2^6

2^6 × 2^4 = 2^(6+4) = 2^10 = 1024

Problem 3

Simplify: (3x^2y)^3 × (2xy^2)^2

Solution:

(3x^2y)^3 × (2xy^2)^2 = 3^3 × (x^2)^3 × y^3 × 2^2 × x^2 × (y^2)^2

= 27 × x^(2×3) × y^3 × 4 × x^2 × y^(2×2)

= 108 × x^(6+2) × y^(3+4)

= 108x^8y^7

Key Takeaways
  • Product Law: a^m × a^n = a^(m+n) - add exponents for same base
  • Quotient Law: a^m ÷ a^n = a^(m-n) - subtract exponents for same base
  • Power Law: (a^m)^n = a^(mn) - multiply exponents when raising to power
  • Product to Power: (ab)^n = a^n × b^n - distribute exponent to each factor
  • Laws apply to all real exponents (integers, rationals, irrationals)
  • Base cannot be zero (except when exponent is positive)
  • Order of operations: apply laws in sequence for complex expressions
