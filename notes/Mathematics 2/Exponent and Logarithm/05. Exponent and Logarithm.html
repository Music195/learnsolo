Exponent and Logarithm Relationship
Logarithms are the inverse operations of exponentiation. Understanding this fundamental relationship is crucial for solving exponential equations and real-world applications involving growth and decay.
Fundamental Relationship: $$M = a^p \iff \log_a M = p$$
Exponential Form: $$a^{\log_a M} = M$$
Logarithmic Form: $$\log_a (a^p) = p$$

Mathematical Foundation

The relationship between exponents and logarithms is fundamental to mathematics and has numerous applications:

  • Definition: A logarithm is the exponent to which a base must be raised to produce a given number
  • Inverse Operations: Exponentiation and logarithms are inverse operations of each other
  • Domain and Range: For log_a(M), a > 0, a ≠ 1, and M > 0
  • One-to-One Correspondence: Each exponential equation has a unique logarithmic equivalent

The fundamental relationship can be extended to more complex expressions:

\begin{cases} a^{\log_a x} = x & (x > 0) \\ \log_a (a^x) = x & (a > 0, a \neq 1) \end{cases}

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Convert 7² = 49 to logarithmic form.

Solution:

Using the relationship: M = a^p ⇔ log_a M = p

Here: M = 49, a = 7, p = 2

Therefore: log₇(49) = 2

Verification: 7^(log₇(49)) = 49 ✓

Problem 2

Convert log₁₀(1000) = 3 to exponential form.

Solution:

Using the relationship: log_a M = p ⇔ M = a^p

Here: a = 10, M = 1000, p = 3

Therefore: 10³ = 1000

Verification: log₁₀(10³) = 3 ✓

Problem 3

Simplify 2^{log₂(x)} where x > 0.

Solution:

Using the relationship: a^{log_a M} = M

Here: a = 2, M = x

Therefore: 2^{log₂(x)} = x

Explanation: The exponentiation and logarithm operations cancel each other out, leaving just x.

Key Takeaways
  • Fundamental relationship: M = a^p ⇔ log_a M = p
  • Logarithms are exponents: log_a M = p means a^p = M
  • Inverse operations: a^{log_a x} = x and log_a(a^x) = x
  • Domain restrictions: a > 0, a ≠ 1, M > 0 for log_a M
  • Conversion is bidirectional: exponential ↔ logarithmic forms
  • Applications: solving exponential equations, pH calculations, earthquake magnitudes
  • Verification: always check that both forms represent the same mathematical relationship
  • Complex expressions: apply relationship to simplify nested exponential-logarithmic expressions
