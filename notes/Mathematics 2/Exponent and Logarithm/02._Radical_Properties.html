Properties of Radicals
Radical properties allow us to manipulate and simplify expressions containing roots. These properties are essential for algebraic operations involving radicals and connect closely with exponent laws.
Product Property: $$\sqrt[n]{a} \cdot \sqrt[n]{b} = \sqrt[n]{ab}$$
Quotient Property: $$\sqrt[n]{\frac{a}{b}} = \frac{\sqrt[n]{a}}{\sqrt[n]{b}}$$
Power Property: $$(\sqrt[n]{a})^m = \sqrt[n]{a^m}$$
Radical of Radical: $$\sqrt[m]{\sqrt[n]{a}} = \sqrt[mn]{a}$$

Product Property

√[n]{a} × √[n]{b} = √[n]{ab}
Multiply numbers under the same radical by multiplying the radicands.

Quotient Property

√[n]{a/b} = √[n]{a} ÷ √[n]{b}
Divide numbers under the same radical by dividing the radicands.

Power Property

(√[n]{a})^m = √[n]{a^m}
Raising a radical to a power is equivalent to raising the radicand to that power.

Radical of Radical

√[m]{√[n]{a}} = √[mn]{a}
Taking a radical of a radical combines the indices by multiplication.

Mathematical Foundation

The properties of radicals are derived from the exponent laws, since radicals can be expressed as fractional exponents:

$$\sqrt[n]{a} = a^{1/n} \quad \text{and} \quad \sqrt[m]{\sqrt[n]{a}} = (a^{1/n})^{1/m} = a^{1/(mn)} = \sqrt[mn]{a}$$

Using this exponential form, the properties become:

  • Product: √[n]{a} × √[n]{b} = a^(1/n) × b^(1/n) = (ab)^(1/n) = √[n]{ab}
  • Quotient: √[n]{a/b} = (a/b)^(1/n) = a^(1/n) × b^(-1/n) = a^(1/n) ÷ b^(1/n) = √[n]{a} ÷ √[n]{b}
  • Power: (√[n]{a})^m = (a^(1/n))^m = a^(m/n) = √[n]{a^m}

These properties hold for positive real numbers and can be extended to complex numbers with appropriate branch cuts.

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Simplify the expression: √18 × √2

Solution:

√18 × √2 = √(18 × 2) = √36 = 6

Alternatively: √18 = √(9×2) = 3√2, so 3√2 × √2 = 3×2 = 6

Problem 2

Simplify: (√3)^4

Solution:

(√3)^4 = √3^4 = √81 = 9

Using exponent form: (3^(1/2))^4 = 3^(4/2) = 3^2 = 9

Problem 3

Simplify: √[4]{√8}

Solution:

√[4]{√8} = √[4]{8^(1/2)} = √[4]{8} = 8^(1/4) = (2^3)^(1/4) = 2^(3/4)

Alternatively: √[4]{√8} = √[4×2]{8} = √[8]{8} = 8^(1/8)

8^(1/8) = (2^3)^(1/8) = 2^(3/8)

Key Takeaways
  • Product Property: √[n]{a} × √[n]{b} = √[n]{ab} - multiply radicands under same index
  • Quotient Property: √[n]{a/b} = √[n]{a} ÷ √[n]{b} - divide radicands under same index
  • Power Property: (√[n]{a})^m = √[n]{a^m} - power outside equals power inside
  • Radical of Radical: √[m]{√[n]{a}} = √[mn]{a} - multiply indices
  • All properties require positive radicands for real numbers
  • Properties connect radicals to fractional exponents
  • Use properties to simplify complex radical expressions
