Derivative of Power and Constant Functions
The power rule is one of the most fundamental rules in differential calculus, allowing us to find derivatives of power functions efficiently.
Power Rule: $$\frac{d}{dx}[x^n] = nx^{n-1}$$
Constant Rule: $$\frac{d}{dx}[c] = 0$$
Special Cases: $$\frac{d}{dx}[x] = 1,\quad \frac{d}{dx}[x^0] = 0$$

Power Rule

$$\frac{d}{dx}[x^n] = nx^{n-1}$$
For any real number n, the derivative of x raised to the power n is n times x raised to the power n-1.

Examples:
$$\frac{d}{dx}[x^3] = 3x^2$$
$$\frac{d}{dx}[x^5] = 5x^4$$

Constant Rule

$$\frac{d}{dx}[c] = 0$$
The derivative of any constant function is zero. Constants don't change, so their rate of change is zero.

Examples:
$$\frac{d}{dx}[5] = 0$$
$$\frac{d}{dx}[\pi] = 0$$

Special Cases

$$\frac{d}{dx}[x] = 1,\quad \frac{d}{dx}[x^0] = 0$$
When n = 1: derivative of x is 1
When n = 0: derivative of constant 1 is 0
These follow directly from the power rule.

Mathematical Foundation

The power rule can be derived from the definition of the derivative:

$$f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}$$

For f(x) = xⁿ:

$$\begin{align} f'(x) &= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{(x+h)^n - x^n}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{1}{h} \left[ x^n + nx^{n-1}h + \binom{n}{2}x^{n-2}h^2 + \cdots + h^n - x^n \right] \\ &= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{1}{h} \left[ nx^{n-1}h + \binom{n}{2}x^{n-2}h^2 + \cdots + h^n \right] \\ &= \lim_{h \to 0} \left[ nx^{n-1} + \binom{n}{2}x^{n-2}h + \cdots + h^{n-1} \right] \\ &= nx^{n-1} \end{align}$$

The constant rule follows from the fact that a constant function has zero slope everywhere.

Applications
  • Rate of Change: Finding instantaneous rates of change for power functions
  • Tangent Lines: Determining slopes of tangent lines to curves
  • Optimization: Finding maximum/minimum values of functions
  • Physics: Velocity and acceleration calculations
  • Economics: Marginal cost and revenue functions
Practice Problems

Problem 1

Find the derivative of f(x) = x³ + 5.

Solution:

f(x) = x³ + 5

f'(x) = 3x² + 0 = 3x²

The derivative of the constant 5 is 0.

Problem 2

Find f'(2) for f(x) = x⁴.

Solution:

f(x) = x⁴

f'(x) = 4x³

f'(2) = 4×(2)³ = 4×8 = 32

Problem 3

Find the derivative of g(x) = 7x² - 3x + 2.

Solution:

g(x) = 7x² - 3x + 2

g'(x) = 14x - 3 + 0 = 14x - 3

Apply power rule to each term: derivative of 7x² is 14x, derivative of -3x is -3, derivative of constant 2 is 0.

Key Takeaways
  • Power rule: d/dx[xⁿ] = nxⁿ⁻¹ for any real number n
  • Constant rule: d/dx[c] = 0 for any constant c
  • Special case: d/dx[x] = 1
  • Power rule applies to polynomials, fractional powers, and negative powers
  • Derivative represents instantaneous rate of change
  • Essential foundation for all other differentiation rules
