Arc Length and Sector Area
Arc length and sector area are fundamental concepts in trigonometry. When working with angles in radians, we can calculate the length of an arc and the area of a sector using simple formulas that relate the angle to the circle's geometry.
Arc Length:
l = rθ
(θ in radians)
Sector Area:
S = (1/2)r²θ
(θ in radians)
Alternative:
S = (1/2)lr
(using arc length)
These formulas work only when the central angle θ is measured in radians. For degrees, convert to radians first using θ_radians = θ_degrees × (π/180).

Arc Length and Sector Area Formulas

Arc Length Formula

$$l = r\theta$$
Where:
l = arc length
r = radius
θ = central angle (in radians)
Example: r = 5, θ = π/2 → l = 5 × π/2 = 2.5π

Sector Area Formula

$$S = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$$
Where:
S = sector area
r = radius
θ = central angle (in radians)
Example: r = 4, θ = π/3 → S = (1/2)×16×π/3 = 8π/3

Alternative Sector Area

$$S = \frac{1}{2}lr$$
Where:
S = sector area
l = arc length
r = radius
Example: l = 10, r = 5 → S = (1/2)×10×5 = 25

Mathematical Foundation

The arc length and sector area formulas are derived from the geometry of circles and the definition of radians:

  • Arc length derivation: Since one radian corresponds to an arc length equal to the radius, the total arc length for angle θ radians is l = rθ
  • Sector area derivation: A sector is like a "pizza slice" of the circle. The area can be found by considering the sector as a fraction of the whole circle
  • Whole circle relationship: Full circle has 2π radians and area πr², so sector with θ radians has area (θ/2π) × πr² = (θ/2)r²
  • Alternative formula: S = (1/2)lr comes from the fact that the sector area equals half the product of arc length and radius
  • Units: Arc length has the same units as radius, sector area has square units
  • Angle conversion: Always convert degrees to radians before using these formulas
  • Special cases: θ = 2π gives full circle, θ = π gives semicircle, θ = π/2 gives quarter circle
  • Real-world applications: Navigation, astronomy, engineering, computer graphics, surveying
  • Integration connection: These formulas are fundamental to integral calculus and polar coordinates
  • Approximations: For small angles, arc length ≈ chord length, sector area ≈ triangle area
\begin{aligned} \text{Arc length:} \quad l &= r\theta \quad (\theta \text{ in radians}) \\ \text{Sector area:} \quad S &= \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta \quad (\theta \text{ in radians}) \\ \text{Alternative:} \quad S &= \frac{1}{2}lr \\ \text{Full circle:} \quad \theta &= 2\pi, \quad l = 2\pi r, \quad S = \pi r^2 \\ \text{Conversion:} \quad \theta_{\text{rad}} &= \theta_{\text{deg}} \times \frac{\pi}{180} \end{aligned}

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Find the arc length of a circle with radius 12 cm and central angle 45°.

Solution:

First convert 45° to radians: 45 × (π/180) = π/4

Arc length l = rθ = 12 × π/4 = 3π cm

Result: Arc length = 3π cm ≈ 9.42 cm

Problem 2

Find the area of a sector with radius 10 m and central angle 2.5 radians.

Solution:

Sector area S = (1/2)r²θ = (1/2) × 100 × 2.5 = 125 m²

Result: Sector area = 125 m²

Problem 3

A sector has arc length 20 cm and radius 8 cm. Find the sector area.

Solution:

Sector area S = (1/2)lr = (1/2) × 20 × 8 = 80 cm²

Result: Sector area = 80 cm²

Key Takeaways
  • Arc length formula l = rθ works only when θ is in radians
  • Sector area formula S = (1/2)r²θ works only when θ is in radians
  • Alternative sector area formula S = (1/2)lr uses arc length and radius
  • Always convert degrees to radians before using these formulas
  • Full circle: θ = 2π radians, l = 2πr, S = πr²
  • Semicircle: θ = π radians, l = πr, S = (1/2)πr²
  • Quarter circle: θ = π/2 radians, l = (π/2)r, S = (1/4)πr²
  • Arc length has same units as radius, sector area has square units
  • These formulas are fundamental to trigonometry and calculus
  • Real-world applications include navigation, engineering, and computer graphics
