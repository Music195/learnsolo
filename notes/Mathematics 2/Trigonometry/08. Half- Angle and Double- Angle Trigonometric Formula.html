Half-Angle and Double-Angle Trigonometric Formulas

Mathematical Foundation

The half-angle and double-angle formulas are fundamental trigonometric identities that express trigonometric functions of half or double angles in terms of the original angle. These formulas are essential for simplifying complex trigonometric expressions and finding exact values of trigonometric functions.

Double-Angle Formulas:
  • $\sin 2\alpha = 2\sin\alpha\cos\alpha$
  • $\cos 2\alpha = \cos^2\alpha - \sin^2\alpha = 2\cos^2\alpha - 1 = 1 - 2\sin^2\alpha$
  • $\tan 2\alpha = \frac{2\tan\alpha}{1 - \tan^2\alpha}$
Half-Angle Formulas:
  • $\sin^2 \frac{\alpha}{2} = \frac{1 - \cos\alpha}{2}$
  • $\cos^2 \frac{\alpha}{2} = \frac{1 + \cos\alpha}{2}$
  • $\tan^2 \frac{\alpha}{2} = \frac{1 - \cos\alpha}{1 + \cos\alpha}$

Worked Example 1: Finding sin(60°) using Double-Angle Formula

Use the double-angle formula to find the exact value of sin(60°).

Solution:

60° = 2 × 30°, so:

sin(60°) = sin(2 × 30°) = 2sin(30°)cos(30°)

= 2 × (1/2) × (√3/2) = 2 × (1/2) × (√3/2) = √3/2

Worked Example 2: Finding cos(60°) using Double-Angle Formula

Use the double-angle formula to find the exact value of cos(60°).

Solution:

cos(60°) = cos(2 × 30°) = cos²(30°) - sin²(30°)

= (√3/2)² - (1/2)² = (3/4) - (1/4) = 1/2

Alternatively: cos(60°) = 2cos²(30°) - 1 = 2(√3/2)² - 1 = 2(3/4) - 1 = 3/2 - 1 = 1/2

Worked Example 3: Finding sin(15°) using Half-Angle Formula

Use the half-angle formula to find the exact value of sin(15°).

Solution:

sin²(15°) = sin²(30°/2) = (1 - cos(30°))/2

= (1 - √3/2)/2 = (2/2 - √3/2)/2 = ((2 - √3)/2)/2 = (2 - √3)/4

sin(15°) = √[(2 - √3)/4] = √(2 - √3)/2

Worked Example 4: Finding cos(15°) using Half-Angle Formula

Use the half-angle formula to find the exact value of cos(15°).

Solution:

cos²(15°) = cos²(30°/2) = (1 + cos(30°))/2

= (1 + √3/2)/2 = (2/2 + √3/2)/2 = ((2 + √3)/2)/2 = (2 + √3)/4

cos(15°) = √[(2 + √3)/4] = √(2 + √3)/2

Detailed Double-Angle Formulas

1. Double-Angle Sine Formula
Formula:
$$\sin 2\alpha = 2\sin\alpha\cos\alpha$$

Derivation: From the addition formula with β = α:

sin(α + α) = sin α cos α + cos α sin α = 2 sin α cos α

2. Double-Angle Cosine Formulas
Formulas:
$$\cos 2\alpha = \cos^2\alpha - \sin^2\alpha$$
$$\cos 2\alpha = 2\cos^2\alpha - 1$$
$$\cos 2\alpha = 1 - 2\sin^2\alpha$$

Derivation: From the addition formula with β = α:

cos(α + α) = cos α cos α - sin α sin α = cos²α - sin²α

The other forms are obtained using the identity cos²α = 1 - sin²α or sin²α = 1 - cos²α.

3. Double-Angle Tangent Formula
Formula:
$$\tan 2\alpha = \frac{2\tan\alpha}{1 - \tan^2\alpha}$$

Derivation: From the tangent addition formula with β = α:

tan(α + α) = (tan α + tan α)/(1 - tan α tan α) = (2 tan α)/(1 - tan²α)

Detailed Half-Angle Formulas

4. Half-Angle Sine Formula
Formula:
$$\sin^2 \frac{\alpha}{2} = \frac{1 - \cos\alpha}{2}$$

Derivation: From the double-angle cosine formula:

cos α = 1 - 2 sin²(α/2)

2 sin²(α/2) = 1 - cos α

sin²(α/2) = (1 - cos α)/2

5. Half-Angle Cosine Formula
Formula:
$$\cos^2 \frac{\alpha}{2} = \frac{1 + \cos\alpha}{2}$$

Derivation: From the double-angle cosine formula:

cos α = 2 cos²(α/2) - 1

2 cos²(α/2) = 1 + cos α

cos²(α/2) = (1 + cos α)/2

6. Half-Angle Tangent Formula
Formula:
$$\tan^2 \frac{\alpha}{2} = \frac{1 - \cos\alpha}{1 + \cos\alpha}$$

Derivation: From the half-angle sine and cosine formulas:

tan²(α/2) = sin²(α/2)/cos²(α/2) = [(1 - cos α)/2] / [(1 + cos α)/2] = (1 - cos α)/(1 + cos α)

Practice Problems

Problem 1: Use the double-angle formula to find sin(120°).
Solution:
120° = 2 × 60°
sin(120°) = sin(2 × 60°) = 2 sin(60°) cos(60°) = 2 × (√3/2) × (1/2) = √3/2
Problem 2: Use the double-angle formula to find cos(120°).
Solution:
cos(120°) = cos(2 × 60°) = cos²(60°) - sin²(60°) = (1/2)² - (√3/2)² = 1/4 - 3/4 = -1/2
Alternatively: cos(120°) = 2 cos²(60°) - 1 = 2 × (1/2)² - 1 = 2 × (1/4) - 1 = 1/2 - 1 = -1/2
Problem 3: Use the half-angle formula to find sin(22.5°).
Solution:
sin²(22.5°) = sin²(45°/2) = (1 - cos(45°))/2 = (1 - √2/2)/2 = ((2 - √2)/2)/2 = (2 - √2)/4
sin(22.5°) = √[(2 - √2)/4] = √(2 - √2)/2
Problem 4: Simplify cos(2x) + sin²(x) using double-angle formulas.
Solution:
cos(2x) + sin²(x) = (1 - 2 sin²(x)) + sin²(x) = 1 - sin²(x)
Problem 5: Express tan(2x) in terms of sin x and cos x.
Solution:
tan(2x) = (2 tan x)/(1 - tan² x) = (2 sin x/cos x)/(1 - sin² x/cos² x)
= (2 sin x/cos x)/((cos² x - sin² x)/cos² x) = (2 sin x/cos x) × (cos² x)/(cos² x - sin² x)
= (2 sin x cos x)/(cos² x - sin² x)

Key Takeaways

  • Double-Angle Sine: sin(2α) = 2 sin α cos α - relates the sine of double angle to product of sine and cosine of original angle.
  • Double-Angle Cosine: Three equivalent forms: cos(2α) = cos²α - sin²α = 2 cos²α - 1 = 1 - 2 sin²α - all derived from the addition formula.
  • Double-Angle Tangent: tan(2α) = (2 tan α)/(1 - tan² α) - useful for tangent identities and solving equations.
  • Half-Angle Formulas: Express squares of half-angle trig functions in terms of the original angle's cosine.
  • Derivation Relationship: Half-angle formulas are derived from double-angle formulas, showing the interconnected nature of these identities.
  • Applications: Essential for trigonometric equation solving, integration, and finding exact values of compound angles.
