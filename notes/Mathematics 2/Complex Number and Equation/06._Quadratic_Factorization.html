Quadratic Factorization
Every quadratic equation can be factored into linear factors. This factorization reveals the roots and provides insights into the equation's behavior.
General Form: $$ax^2 + bx + c = a(x - \alpha)(x - \beta)$$
Expanded Form: $$a(x^2 - (\alpha+\beta)x + \alpha\beta)$$
Root Relationships: $$\alpha + \beta = -\frac{b}{a}, \quad \alpha\beta = \frac{c}{a}$$

Standard Factorization

ax² + bx + c = a(x - α)(x - β)
The quadratic is factored into a constant times two linear factors, where α and β are the roots.

Monic Factorization

x² + px + q = (x - α)(x - β)
When a = 1, the factorization becomes simpler with the constant term removed from the linear factors.

Complex Factorization

(x - (a+bi))(x - (a-bi))
Complex roots always come in conjugate pairs, leading to factorizations with complex coefficients.

Mathematical Foundation

The factorization of a quadratic equation is based on the fundamental theorem of algebra and the relationship between roots and coefficients:

$$ax^2 + bx + c = a(x - \alpha)(x - \beta)$$

Expanding the right side:

$$\begin{align} a(x - \alpha)(x - \beta) &= a[x^2 - (\alpha + \beta)x + \alpha\beta] \\ &= ax^2 - a(\alpha + \beta)x + a\alpha\beta \end{align}$$

Comparing with ax² + bx + c:

$$\begin{cases} -a(\alpha + \beta) = b \implies \alpha + \beta = -\frac{b}{a} \\ a\alpha\beta = c \implies \alpha\beta = \frac{c}{a} \end{cases}$$

This shows that factorization and root-finding are essentially the same process.

Applications
  • Root Finding: Factorization reveals the roots of the equation
  • Graph Analysis: x-intercepts of the parabola are the roots
  • Polynomial Division: Used in long division of polynomials
  • Equation Solving: Setting factors to zero gives solutions
  • Function Decomposition: Breaking down complex functions into simpler parts
Practice Problems

Problem 1

Factorize the quadratic equation x² - 7x + 12 = 0.

Solution:

Find factors of 12 that sum to 7: 3 and 4

x² - 7x + 12 = (x - 3)(x - 4)

Roots: x = 3, x = 4

Problem 2

Factorize 3x² - 10x + 8 = 0.

Solution:

Find factors of 24 that sum to 10: 4 and 6

3x² - 10x + 8 = 3(x² - 10/3x + 8/3)

= 3[(x - 2)(x - 4/3)] = 3(x - 2)(x - 4/3)

Problem 3

Write the factorization of x² + 6x + 10 = 0.

Solution:

Discriminant: 36 - 40 = -4 < 0

Roots: -3 ± i

Factorization: (x + 3 - i)(x + 3 + i)

Key Takeaways
  • Every quadratic can be factored as a(x - α)(x - β) where α, β are roots
  • The constant 'a' is factored out, leaving monic quadratic in the parentheses
  • Complex roots come in conjugate pairs
  • Factorization and root-finding are equivalent processes
  • The factored form reveals x-intercepts of the quadratic graph
  • Useful for polynomial division and function decomposition
