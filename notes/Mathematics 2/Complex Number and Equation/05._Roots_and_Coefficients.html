Relationship Between Roots and Coefficients
For any quadratic equation ax² + bx + c = 0, there are fundamental relationships between its roots and coefficients that hold true regardless of whether the roots are real or complex.
Sum of Roots: $$α + β = -\frac{b}{a}$$
Product of Roots: $$α \cdot β = \frac{c}{a}$$
Quadratic Equation: $$x^2 - (α+β)x + (αβ) = 0$$

Sum of Roots

α + β = -b/a
The sum of the roots equals the negative of the coefficient of x divided by the coefficient of x².

For x² + px + q = 0:
α + β = -p

Product of Roots

α × β = c/a
The product of the roots equals the constant term divided by the coefficient of x².

For x² + px + q = 0:
α × β = q

Quadratic from Roots

x² - (sum)x + product = 0
If you know the roots, you can form the quadratic equation.

Roots α, β:
(x - α)(x - β) = 0

Mathematical Foundation

For a quadratic equation ax² + bx + c = 0 with roots α and β, we can write:

$$ax^2 + bx + c = a(x - \alpha)(x - \beta)$$

Expanding the right side:

$$a(x - \alpha)(x - \beta) = a[x^2 - (\alpha + \beta)x + \alpha\beta] = ax^2 - a(\alpha + \beta)x + a\alpha\beta$$

Comparing coefficients with ax² + bx + c:

$$\begin{cases} -a(\alpha + \beta) = b \implies \alpha + \beta = -\frac{b}{a} \\ a\alpha\beta = c \implies \alpha\beta = \frac{c}{a} \end{cases}$$

These relationships hold for both real and complex roots, making them extremely useful for solving problems involving quadratic equations.

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Form the quadratic equation whose roots are 4 and -1.

Solution:

Sum of roots: 4 + (-1) = 3

Product of roots: 4 × (-1) = -4

Equation: x² - (sum)x + product = 0

x² - 3x - 4 = 0

Problem 2

For the equation 2x² + 3x - 5 = 0, find the sum and product of the roots.

Solution:

Sum of roots = -b/a = -3/2 = -1.5

Product of roots = c/a = -5/2 = -2.5

Problem 3

If α and β are roots of x² + px + q = 0, find the equation whose roots are α + 1 and β + 1.

Solution:

New sum: (α + 1) + (β + 1) = α + β + 2 = -p + 2

New product: (α + 1)(β + 1) = αβ + (α + β) + 1 = q - p + 1

Equation: x² - (-p + 2)x + (q - p + 1) = 0

x² + (p - 2)x + (q - p + 1) = 0

Key Takeaways
  • Sum of roots α + β = -b/a (negative of x coefficient over x² coefficient)
  • Product of roots α × β = c/a (constant term over x² coefficient)
  • These relationships work for both real and complex roots
  • To form equation from roots: x² - (sum)x + product = 0
  • For monic equations (a=1): sum = -b, product = c
  • Useful for finding new equations with transformed roots
