Quadratic Equation from Roots
Given the roots of a quadratic equation, we can construct the equation using the relationship between roots and coefficients.
Standard Form: $$x^2 - (\alpha + \beta)x + \alpha\beta = 0$$
General Form: $$x^2 - sx + p = 0$$
From Roots: $$(x - \alpha)(x - \beta) = 0$$

From Root Sum & Product

x² - (sum)x + product = 0
If you know the sum (s) and product (p) of the roots, the equation is x² - sx + p = 0.

Sum: α + β = s
Product: α × β = p

From Individual Roots

(x - α)(x - β) = 0
If you know the individual roots α and β, expand (x - α)(x - β) to get the equation.

Expansion: x² - (α+β)x + αβ = 0

Complex Roots

(x - (a+bi))(x - (a-bi))
Complex roots come in conjugate pairs. The equation will have real coefficients.

Result: x² - 2ax + (a² + b²) = 0

Mathematical Foundation

The quadratic equation with roots α and β can be constructed by expanding the factored form:

$$(x - \alpha)(x - \beta) = x^2 - (\alpha + \beta)x + \alpha\beta = 0$$

This is derived from the fundamental relationship between roots and coefficients:

$$\begin{cases} \alpha + \beta = -\frac{b}{a} \\ \alpha\beta = \frac{c}{a} \end{cases}$$

For a monic quadratic (coefficient of x² is 1), the equation becomes:

$$x^2 + \frac{b}{a}x + \frac{c}{a} = x^2 - (\alpha + \beta)x + \alpha\beta = 0$$

This method works for both real and complex roots, making it universally applicable.

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Form the quadratic equation whose roots are 3 and -2.

Solution:

Sum of roots: 3 + (-2) = 1

Product of roots: 3 × (-2) = -6

Equation: x² - (sum)x + product = 0

x² - x - 6 = 0

Problem 2

Find the quadratic equation with roots 2 + √3 and 2 - √3.

Solution:

Sum: (2 + √3) + (2 - √3) = 4

Product: (2 + √3)(2 - √3) = 4 - 3 = 1

Equation: x² - 4x + 1 = 0

Problem 3

If α and β are roots of x² + px + q = 0, find the equation whose roots are α + 1 and β + 1.

Solution:

New sum: (α + 1) + (β + 1) = α + β + 2 = -p + 2

New product: (α + 1)(β + 1) = αβ + (α + β) + 1 = q - p + 1

Equation: x² - (-p + 2)x + (q - p + 1) = 0

x² + (p - 2)x + (q - p + 1) = 0

Key Takeaways
  • To form equation from roots α, β: x² - (α+β)x + αβ = 0
  • The factored form (x - α)(x - β) expands to the equation
  • Complex roots come in conjugate pairs for real coefficients
  • Sum and product uniquely determine the quadratic equation
  • This method works for both real and complex roots
  • Useful for constructing equations with desired solution sets
