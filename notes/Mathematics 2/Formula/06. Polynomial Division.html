Polynomial Division
Polynomial division is the process of dividing one polynomial by another polynomial of lower degree. The result consists of a quotient polynomial and a remainder polynomial, following the division algorithm for polynomials.
Division Algorithm: $$A = BQ + R$$
Where: $$\deg(R) < \deg(B) \text{ or } R = 0$$
When dividing polynomial A by polynomial B, we get quotient Q and remainder R. The remainder R is either zero or has degree less than the degree of B.

Mathematical Foundation

The polynomial division algorithm states that for any polynomials A and B (with B ≠ 0):

  • Division Result: A = BQ + R where Q is the quotient and R is the remainder
  • Remainder Condition: Either R = 0 or degree of R < degree of B
  • Long Division: Similar to numerical long division but with polynomials
  • Synthetic Division: Shortcut method when divisor is linear (degree 1)
  • Factor Theorem: If B is a factor of A, then R = 0
  • Root-Factor Relationship: If r is a root of A, then (x - r) is a factor

The process involves dividing the leading terms, multiplying, and subtracting repeatedly.

\begin{aligned} \text{Long Division Steps:} \\ 1.\ \text{Divide leading terms} \\ 2.\ \text{Multiply quotient term by divisor} \\ 3.\ \text{Subtract from current polynomial} \\ 4.\ \text{Repeat with remainder} \end{aligned}
Division Methods

Long Division

Traditional method
Steps:
• Divide leading terms
• Multiply by divisor
• Subtract from dividend
• Bring down next term
• Repeat until done

Synthetic Division

For linear divisors
Conditions:
• Divisor must be (x - r)
• Faster than long division
• Only works for degree 1 divisors
• Coefficients only

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Divide (x³ - 2x² + 3x - 1) by (x - 1) using long division.

Solution:

  • Divide x³ by x: get x²
  • Multiply x² × (x - 1) = x³ - x²
  • Subtract: (x³ - 2x² + 3x - 1) - (x³ - x²) = -x² + 3x - 1
  • Divide -x² by x: get -x
  • Multiply -x × (x - 1) = -x² + x
  • Subtract: (-x² + 3x - 1) - (-x² + x) = 2x - 1
  • Divide 2x by x: get 2
  • Multiply 2 × (x - 1) = 2x - 2
  • Subtract: (2x - 1) - (2x - 2) = 1
  • Result: Quotient = x² - x + 2, Remainder = 1

Problem 2

Use synthetic division to divide (3x³ + x² - 2) by (x - 1).

Solution:

Synthetic division with root r = 1:

1 | 3 1 0 -2
_____|____________
     | 3 4 4
     | 3 4 4 2
  • Coefficients: 3, 1, 0, -2
  • Bring down 3
  • 3 × 1 = 3, add to next: 1 + 3 = 4
  • 4 × 1 = 4, add to next: 0 + 4 = 4
  • 4 × 1 = 4, add to next: -2 + 4 = 2
  • Result: Quotient = 3x² + 4x + 4, Remainder = 2

Problem 3

Factor x³ - 6x² + 11x - 6 given that (x - 1) is a factor.

Solution:

Use synthetic division with r = 1:

1 | 1 -6 11 -6
_____|_____________
     | 1 -5 6
     | 1 -5 6 0
  • Quotient: x² - 5x + 6
  • Factor quotient: (x - 2)(x - 3)
  • Result: (x - 1)(x - 2)(x - 3)
Key Takeaways
  • Division algorithm: A = BQ + R where deg(R) < deg(B) or R = 0
  • Long division: Traditional method for any polynomials
  • Synthetic division: Shortcut for linear divisors (x - r)
  • Factor theorem: (x - r) is factor iff r is root
  • Remainder theorem: Remainder when divided by (x - r) is P(r)
  • Rational root theorem: Possible rational roots are factors of constant term over factors of leading coefficient
  • Complete factorization: Combine division with other factoring methods
  • Applications: Solving equations, simplifying rational expressions
