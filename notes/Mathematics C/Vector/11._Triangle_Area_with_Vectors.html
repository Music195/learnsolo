Triangle Area with Vectors

Vector-Based Triangle Area Formula

The area of a triangle can be calculated using vector operations, providing a powerful connection between geometry and linear algebra.

Triangle Area Formulas
For triangle with vertices at origin O, and points A, B:
\(S = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{|\vec{a}|^2 |\vec{b}|^2 - (\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b})^2}\)
\(S = \frac{1}{2} |a_1b_2 - a_2b_1|\)
Cross Product Formula
\(S = \frac{1}{2} |\vec{a} \times \vec{b}|\)
Using the magnitude of the cross product (for 2D vectors)

Mathematical Foundation

The vector formula for triangle area is derived from the cross product and trigonometric identities.

Derivation from Cross Product
\(|\vec{a} \times \vec{b}| = |\vec{a}| |\vec{b}| \sin\theta\)
\(S = \frac{1}{2} |\vec{a}| |\vec{b}| \sin\theta\)
Area equals half the product of two sides times sine of included angle
Component Form Derivation
\(|\vec{a} \times \vec{b}| = |a_1b_2 - a_2b_1|\)
\(S = \frac{1}{2} |a_1b_2 - a_2b_1|\)
Determinant form gives the area directly
Distance Formula Connection
\(S = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{|\vec{a}|^2 |\vec{b}|^2 - (\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b})^2}\)
Using law of cosines: c² = a² + b² - 2ab cosθ

Practice Problems

Test your understanding with these practice problems.

1
Find the area of triangle OAB where \(\vec{OA} = (4, 2)\) and \(\vec{OB} = (1, 5)\)
Using determinant formula: \(S = \frac{1}{2} |4×5 - 2×1| = \frac{1}{2} |20 - 2| = \frac{1}{2} × 18 = 9\)
Using distance formula: \(S = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{20×26 - (4×1 + 2×5)^2} = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{520 - 14^2} = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{520 - 196} = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{324} = \frac{1}{2} × 18 = 9\)
Area = 9 square units
2
Calculate area for vectors \(\vec{a} = (3, 7)\) and \(\vec{b} = (6, 2)\)
Cross product magnitude: \(|\vec{a} × \vec{b}| = |3×2 - 7×6| = |-36| = 36\)
Triangle area: \(S = \frac{1}{2} × 36 = 18\)
Area = 18 square units
3
Find area using distance formula for \(\vec{a} = (2, 4)\) and \(\vec{b} = (5, 1)\)
\(|\vec{a}|^2 = 4 + 16 = 20\), \(|\vec{b}|^2 = 25 + 1 = 26\)
\(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = 2×5 + 4×1 = 14\)
\(S = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{20×26 - 14^2} = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{520 - 196} = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{324} = \frac{1}{2} × 18 = 9\)
Area = 9 square units

Key Takeaways

  • Triangle area can be found using vector cross product magnitude
  • Determinant formula |a₁b₂ - a₂b₁| gives area directly
  • Distance formula approach uses Pythagorean theorem
  • All methods give equivalent results
  • Essential for computational geometry and physics
  • Works for any triangle positioned anywhere in the plane
