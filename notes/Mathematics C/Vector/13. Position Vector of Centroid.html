Position Vector of the Centroid of a Triangle
For a triangle \( \triangle \text{ABC} \) with vertices \( A(\vec{a}) \), \( B(\vec{b}) \), and \( C(\vec{c}) \), the position vector \( \vec{g} \) of its centroid G is:
$$ \vec{g} = \frac{\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c}}{3} $$

Mathematical Foundation

The centroid of a triangle is the point where the three medians intersect. A median is a line segment from a vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side.

The position vector of the centroid can be derived by considering that each median divides the triangle into two regions of equal area, and the centroid is the "center of mass" of the triangle when each vertex has equal weight.

$$ \vec{g} = \frac{\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c}}{3} $$

This formula shows that the centroid is simply the average of the position vectors of the three vertices.

Applications
  • Center of Mass: The centroid represents the balance point of the triangle
  • Geometric Center: Used in computer graphics for triangle rendering and collision detection
  • Engineering: Important in structural analysis and finite element methods
  • Physics: Used in calculating gravitational center of triangular plates
  • Navigation: Helps in determining central positions in triangular formations
Practice Problems

Problem 1

Find the position vector of the centroid of a triangle with vertices A(1,2), B(3,4), and C(5,2).

Solution:

Using the centroid formula: \(\vec{g} = \frac{\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c}}{3}\)

\(\vec{g} = \frac{(1,2) + (3,4) + (5,2)}{3} = \frac{(9,8)}{3} = (3, \frac{8}{3})\)

Position vector of centroid: \((3, \frac{8}{3})\)

Problem 2

Determine the centroid of triangle ABC where A(-2,1), B(4,3), and C(2,-1).

Solution:

\(\vec{g} = \frac{(-2,1) + (4,3) + (2,-1)}{3} = \frac{(4,3)}{3} = (\frac{4}{3}, 1)\)

Position vector of centroid: \((\frac{4}{3}, 1)\)

Problem 3

If the centroid of triangle with vertices A(x₁,y₁), B(x₂,y₂), C(x₃,y₃) is (2,3), find the sum of coordinates.

Solution:

Centroid coordinates: \((\frac{x_1+x_2+x_3}{3}, \frac{y_1+y_2+y_3}{3}) = (2,3)\)

Therefore: \(\frac{x_1+x_2+x_3}{3} = 2\) ⇒ x₁+x₂+x₃ = 6

And: \(\frac{y_1+y_2+y_3}{3} = 3\) ⇒ y₁+y₂+y₃ = 9

Sum of all coordinates: 6 + 9 = 15

Key Takeaways
  • The centroid is the average position of the three vertices
  • Formula: \(\vec{g} = \frac{\vec{a} + \vec{b} + \vec{c}}{3}\)
  • Each median divides the triangle into two equal areas
  • The centroid is the intersection point of all three medians
  • Coordinates: \((\frac{x_1+x_2+x_3}{3}, \frac{y_1+y_2+y_3}{3})\)
  • Used as center of mass and geometric center of triangles
