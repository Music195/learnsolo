Properties of Complex Conjugate

Core Concept: Complex Conjugate

The complex conjugate of a complex number is obtained by changing the sign of its imaginary part. This operation has deep geometric and algebraic significance in complex analysis.

The conjugate of a complex number \( z = a + bi \) is:

\overline{z} = a - bi
Key Properties:
  • The conjugate of a real number is itself
  • Conjugating twice returns the original number
  • Conjugate of conjugate: \(\overline{\overline{z}} = z\)
  • Essential for complex number division and roots

Fundamental Properties

Arithmetic Properties
1 \(\overline{z_1 + z_2} = \overline{z_1} + \overline{z_2}\)
2 \(\overline{z_1 - z_2} = \overline{z_1} - \overline{z_2}\)
3 \(\overline{z_1 \cdot z_2} = \overline{z_1} \cdot \overline{z_2}\)
4 \(\overline{\dfrac{z_1}{z_2}} = \dfrac{\overline{z_1}}{\overline{z_2}} \quad (z_2 \neq 0)\)
Additional Properties
  • Power property: \(\overline{z^n} = (\overline{z})^n\) for any natural number n
  • Modulus property: \(|\overline{z}| = |z|\)
  • Real/imaginary parts: \(\overline{z} = 2\Re(z) - z\)
  • Self-conjugate: \(\overline{z} = z\) iff z is real

Geometric Interpretation

Reflection over Real Axis: The complex conjugate corresponds to reflecting a point in the complex plane over the real axis. If z = a + bi is at position (a,b), then \(\overline{z} = a - bi\) is at position (a,-b).
Distance Preservation: The modulus (distance from origin) is preserved under conjugation: |z| = |\(\overline{z}\)|. This makes conjugation an isometry of the complex plane.

Worked Examples

1

Find the conjugate of z = 3 + 4i.

z = 3 + 4i
Change sign of imaginary part: 4i becomes -4i
\(\overline{z} = 3 - 4i\)
Geometrically: Point (3,4) reflects to (3,-4)
2

Compute \(\overline{(2 + 3i)(1 - i)}\).

First compute the product: (2 + 3i)(1 - i)
= 2×1 + 2×(-i) + 3i×1 + 3i×(-i)
= 2 - 2i + 3i - 3i² = 2 + i + 3 = 5 + i
Now conjugate: \(\overline{5 + i} = 5 - i\)
Using property: \(\overline{(2 + 3i)(1 - i)} = \overline{2 + 3i} \cdot \overline{1 - i} = (2 - 3i)(1 + i)\)
= 2×1 + 2×i - 3i×1 - 3i×i = 2 + 2i - 3i - 3i² = 2 - i + 3 = 5 - i
3

Simplify \(\dfrac{z}{\overline{z}}\) where z ≠ 0.

Let z = a + bi, then \(\overline{z} = a - bi\)
\(\dfrac{z}{\overline{z}} = \dfrac{a + bi}{a - bi}\)
Multiply numerator and denominator by conjugate of denominator
= \(\dfrac{(a + bi)(a + bi)}{(a - bi)(a + bi)} = \dfrac{(a + bi)^2}{a^2 - (bi)^2}\)
= \(\dfrac{a^2 + 2abi + (bi)^2}{a^2 + b^2} = \dfrac{a^2 + 2abi - b^2}{a^2 + b^2}\)
= \(\dfrac{(a^2 - b^2) + 2abi}{a^2 + b^2}\)
This is a complex number with modulus 1

Applications in Complex Analysis

Important Applications
  • Division: Convert complex division to multiplication
  • Roots: Finding roots of complex polynomials
  • Real parts: Extracting real coefficients from complex expressions
  • Quadratic formula: Solving complex quadratic equations
  • Signal processing: Complex conjugate in Fourier analysis

Key Takeaways

  • \(\overline{z} = a - bi\) for z = a + bi
  • Arithmetic operations preserve conjugation
  • Geometric interpretation: reflection over real axis
  • Modulus is preserved: |z| = |\(\overline{z}\)|
  • Essential for complex division and polynomial roots
  • Self-conjugate numbers are real
