Product and Quotient of Complex Numbers in Polar Form

Core Concept: Polar Operations

The polar form makes complex multiplication and division incredibly simple. Instead of dealing with trigonometric identities, we just multiply moduli and add/subtract arguments.

For complex numbers in polar form:

z₁ = r₁(cos θ₁ + i sin θ₁), \quad z₂ = r₂(cos θ₂ + i sin θ₂)
Product:
z₁z₂ = r₁r₂ [cos(θ₁ + θ₂) + i sin(θ₁ + θ₂)]
Quotient:
\frac{z₁}{z₂} = \frac{r₁}{r₂} [cos(θ₁ - θ₂) + i sin(θ₁ - θ₂)] \quad (z₂ ≠ 0)
Simple Rules:
  • Multiplication: Multiply moduli, add arguments
  • Division: Divide moduli, subtract arguments
  • Arguments: Always work with principal values (-π, π]
  • Moduli: Must be non-negative real numbers

Geometric Interpretation

Multiplication as Rotation and Scaling: Multiplying by z₂ scales the magnitude by |z₂| and rotates by arg(z₂). The product z₁z₂ represents z₁ scaled by r₂ and rotated by θ₂.
Division as Inverse Rotation and Scaling: Dividing by z₂ scales by 1/|z₂| and rotates by -arg(z₂). This is equivalent to multiplying by the conjugate of z₂ and scaling appropriately.
Visual Properties
  • Rotation: Multiplication rotates by the argument angle
  • Scaling: Multiplication scales by the modulus
  • Composition: Multiple operations compose naturally
  • Unit circle: Multiplication by unit complex numbers is pure rotation

Worked Examples

1

Compute (2∠30°)(3∠45°).

z₁ = 2∠30°, z₂ = 3∠45°
Product: r₁r₂ ∠ (θ₁ + θ₂) = 2 × 3 ∠ (30° + 45°)
= 6 ∠ 75°
In rectangular form: 6(cos 75° + i sin 75°)
≈ 6(0.259 + i 0.965) ≈ 1.55 + 5.79i
2

Compute (8∠120°)/(2∠30°).

z₁ = 8∠120°, z₂ = 2∠30°
Quotient: (r₁/r₂) ∠ (θ₁ - θ₂) = (8/2) ∠ (120° - 30°)
= 4 ∠ 90°
In rectangular form: 4(cos 90° + i sin 90°)
= 4(0 + i × 1) = 4i
3

Find the product of 5∠60° and 2∠(-30°).

z₁ = 5∠60°, z₂ = 2∠(-30°)
Product: r₁r₂ ∠ (θ₁ + θ₂) = 5 × 2 ∠ (60° + (-30°))
= 10 ∠ 30°
In rectangular form: 10(cos 30° + i sin 30°)
= 10(0.866 + i 0.5) ≈ 8.66 + 5i
Note: Negative angles represent clockwise rotation

Applications in Complex Analysis

Key Applications
  • De Moivre's theorem: Powers and roots of complex numbers
  • Fourier analysis: Signal processing and frequency domain
  • Fractals: Complex iteration and Julia sets
  • Quantum mechanics: Wave functions and operators
  • Electrical engineering: AC circuit analysis
  • Control systems: Transfer functions and poles/zeros

Key Takeaways

  • Product: (r₁∠θ₁)(r₂∠θ₂) = r₁r₂ ∠ (θ₁ + θ₂)
  • Quotient: (r₁∠θ₁)/(r₂∠θ₂) = (r₁/r₂) ∠ (θ₁ - θ₂)
  • Geometric: multiplication combines scaling and rotation
  • Arguments add for products, subtract for quotients
  • Moduli multiply for products, divide for quotients
  • Essential for complex powers, roots, and periodic functions
