n-th Roots of Unity

Core Concept: Roots of Unity

The n-th roots of unity are the solutions to the equation zⁿ = 1. These are fundamental objects in complex analysis and have important applications in algebra, geometry, and number theory.

The n-th roots of unity are given by:

$$ z_k = \cos\left(\frac{2k\pi}{n}\right) + i\sin\left(\frac{2k\pi}{n}\right) $$
for k = 0, 1, 2, ..., n-1
Key Properties:
  • Unit circle: All roots lie on the unit circle |z| = 1
  • Regular polygon: Vertices of regular n-gon inscribed in unit circle
  • Primitive root: ω = e^(2πi/n) generates all roots
  • Cyclotomic equation: Minimal polynomial divides xⁿ - 1
  • Multiplicative group: Form cyclic group of order n under multiplication
  • Sum formula: Sum of all n-th roots of unity is 0 for n > 1

Mathematical Properties

Fundamental Properties
1. All roots satisfy zⁿ = 1
2. They form a cyclic group under multiplication
3. Sum of all roots: 1 + ω + ω² + ... + ω^(n-1) = 0 for n > 1
4. Product of roots: (xⁿ - 1)/(x - 1) = x^(n-1) + x^(n-2) + ... + 1
5. Primitive roots: ω^k where gcd(k,n) = 1
Special Cases
  • n=1: Only root is 1
  • n=2: Roots are 1 and -1
  • n=3: Cube roots: 1, ω, ω² where ω = -1/2 + i√3/2
  • n=4: Fourth roots: 1, i, -1, -i
  • n=6: Sixth roots combine square and cube roots
  • Prime n: All roots except 1 are primitive

Worked Examples

1

Find the cube roots of unity and verify they sum to zero.

Solve z³ = 1
Roots: z₀ = cos(0°) + i sin(0°) = 1
z₁ = cos(120°) + i sin(120°) = -1/2 + i√3/2
z₂ = cos(240°) + i sin(240°) = -1/2 - i√3/2
Sum: 1 + (-1/2 + i√3/2) + (-1/2 - i√3/2) = 1 - 1/2 - 1/2 + 0i = 0
Notice: 1 + ω + ω² = 0 where ω = e^(2πi/3)
2

Show that the 6th roots of unity are 1, -1, i, -i, and two complex cube roots.

6th roots: cos(2kπ/6) + i sin(2kπ/6) for k=0 to 5
k=0: cos(0°) + i sin(0°) = 1
k=1: cos(60°) + i sin(60°) = 1/2 + i√3/2
k=2: cos(120°) + i sin(120°) = -1/2 + i√3/2
k=3: cos(180°) + i sin(180°) = -1
k=4: cos(240°) + i sin(240°) = -1/2 - i√3/2
k=5: cos(300°) + i sin(300°) = 1/2 - i√3/2
Notice k=0,3 are ±1; k=1,5 are 6th roots that are also square roots; k=2,4 are 6th roots that are also cube roots
3

Find the minimal polynomial for the 5th roots of unity.

5th roots: 1, ζ, ζ², ζ³, ζ⁴ where ζ = e^(2πi/5)
The cyclotomic polynomial Φ₅(x) = (x⁵ - 1)/(x - 1) = x⁴ + x³ + x² + x + 1
This is the minimal polynomial for ζ over ℚ
It generates the 5th cyclotomic field ℚ(ζ)
All other roots are powers of ζ

Applications and Importance

Key Applications
  • Algebraic number theory: Cyclotomic fields and Galois theory
  • Discrete mathematics: Fast Fourier transform algorithms
  • Computer science: Error-correcting codes and cryptography
  • Signal processing: Digital signal processing and filtering
  • Geometry: Construction of regular polygons
  • Physics: Quantum mechanics and symmetry groups

Key Takeaways

  • n-th roots of unity solve zⁿ = 1 and lie on the unit circle
  • They form vertices of regular n-gon inscribed in unit circle
  • Sum of all roots is 0 for n > 1
  • Primitive roots generate the full set under multiplication
  • Fundamental to cyclotomic polynomials and field extensions
  • Essential in FFT algorithms and many mathematical applications
