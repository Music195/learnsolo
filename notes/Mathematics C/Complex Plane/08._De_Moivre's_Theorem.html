De Moivre's Theorem

Core Concept: De Moivre's Theorem

De Moivre's theorem is one of the most important results in complex analysis. It provides a simple way to compute powers of complex numbers in polar form and is fundamental to finding roots of complex numbers.

For any real number θ and integer n:

$$ \left( \cos\theta + i\sin\theta \right)^n = \cos n\theta + i\sin n\theta $$
Key Properties:
  • Power rule: Raising to nth power multiplies the angle by n
  • Integer powers: Works for positive, negative, and zero powers
  • Trigonometric identities: Generates multiple angle formulas
  • Complex roots: Essential for finding nth roots of complex numbers
  • Euler's formula: Connects to e^(iθ) representation

Mathematical Foundation

Proof by Induction
Base case (n=1): (cos θ + i sin θ)¹ = cos θ + i sin θ ✓
Inductive step: Assume true for n, prove for n+1
(cos θ + i sin θ)^{n+1} = (cos θ + i sin θ)^n (cos θ + i sin θ)
= (cos nθ + i sin nθ)(cos θ + i sin θ)
= cos nθ cos θ - sin nθ sin θ + i(cos nθ sin θ + sin nθ cos θ)
= cos((n+1)θ) + i sin((n+1)θ)
Extensions
  • Negative powers: (cos θ + i sin θ)^{-n} = cos(-nθ) + i sin(-nθ) = cos nθ - i sin nθ
  • Rational powers: For non-integer powers, defines branches of complex logarithm
  • Euler form: [e^(iθ)]^n = e^(inθ) = cos nθ + i sin nθ
  • Real powers: Generalizes to complex exponents via analytic continuation

Worked Examples

1

Compute (cos 30° + i sin 30°)³.

Using De Moivre's theorem: (cos θ + i sin θ)ⁿ = cos nθ + i sin nθ
Here θ = 30°, n = 3
cos(3×30°) + i sin(3×30°) = cos 90° + i sin 90°
= 0 + i × 1 = i
Verification: (cos 30° + i sin 30°)³ = (√3/2 + i/2)³
But De Moivre gives the result directly!
2

Find cos 3θ in terms of cos θ.

cos 3θ = Re[(cos θ + i sin θ)³]
(cos θ + i sin θ)³ = cos 3θ + i sin 3θ
Expand: cos³θ + 3i cos²θ sin θ - 3 cos θ sin²θ - i sin³θ
Real part: cos³θ - 3 cos θ sin²θ
= cos³θ - 3 cos θ (1 - cos²θ)
= cos³θ - 3 cos θ + 3 cos³θ
= 4 cos³θ - 3 cos θ
Therefore: cos 3θ = 4 cos³θ - 3 cos θ
3

Find the three cube roots of 1.

Solve z³ = 1, so z = 1^(1/3)
1 = cos 0° + i sin 0°
Roots: cos(0°/3 + 360°k/3) + i sin(0°/3 + 360°k/3) for k=0,1,2
k=0: cos 0° + i sin 0° = 1
k=1: cos 120° + i sin 120° = -1/2 + i √3/2
k=2: cos 240° + i sin 240° = -1/2 - i √3/2
These are the three cube roots of unity

Applications and Importance

Fundamental Applications
  • Trigonometric identities: Multiple angle formulas (cos nθ, sin nθ)
  • Complex roots: Finding nth roots of any complex number
  • Polynomial equations: Solving equations with complex coefficients
  • Fourier analysis: Signal processing and frequency analysis
  • Fractal geometry: Complex dynamics and Julia sets
  • Quantum mechanics: Angular momentum and spin states

Key Takeaways

  • (cos θ + i sin θ)ⁿ = cos nθ + i sin nθ for integer n
  • Multiplies angles by n, preserves unit circle
  • Generates trigonometric identities for multiple angles
  • Essential for finding complex roots and solving equations
  • Foundation for complex analysis and many applications
  • Extends to negative and fractional powers
