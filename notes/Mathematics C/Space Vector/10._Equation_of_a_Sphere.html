Equation of a Sphere

Interactive Learning with 3D Visualizations

1. Sphere with Center (a, b, c) and Radius r

The equation of a sphere with center (a, b, c) and radius r is:

$$(x - a)^2 + (y - b)^2 + (z - c)^2 = r^2$$

This represents all points (x, y, z) that are at a distance r from the center (a, b, c).

2. Sphere with Center at the Origin

When the center is at the origin (0, 0, 0), the equation simplifies to:

$$x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = r^2$$

This is the equation of a sphere centered at the origin with radius r.

Practice Problems

Problem 1: Find the equation of the sphere with center (2, 3, 4) and radius 5.

Using the general formula: $(x - a)^2 + (y - b)^2 + (z - c)^2 = r^2$

Substitute a=2, b=3, c=4, r=5:

$(x - 2)^2 + (y - 3)^2 + (z - 4)^2 = 25$

Final answer: $(x - 2)^2 + (y - 3)^2 + (z - 4)^2 = 25$

Problem 2: Find the center and radius of the sphere $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 - 6x + 4y - 2z = 11$.

Complete the square for each variable:

$(x^2 - 6x) + (y^2 + 4y) + (z^2 - 2z) = 11$

$(x - 3)^2 - 9 + (y + 2)^2 - 4 + (z - 1)^2 - 1 = 11$

$(x - 3)^2 + (y + 2)^2 + (z - 1)^2 = 25$

Center: (3, -2, 1), Radius: 5

Problem 3: Find the equation of the sphere that passes through the origin and has center (1, 2, 3).

The origin (0,0,0) lies on the sphere, so the distance from center to origin equals radius.

Distance = $\sqrt{(1-0)^2 + (2-0)^2 + (3-0)^2} = \sqrt{1 + 4 + 9} = \sqrt{14}$

Equation: $(x - 1)^2 + (y - 2)^2 + (z - 3)^2 = 14$

Important Notes:
  • The equation represents all points (x, y, z) at a distance r from the center (a, b, c).
  • For the origin as center, the formula simplifies to $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = r^2$.
  • This is the standard form for the equation of a sphere in 3D space.
  • To expand the equation: $(x - a)^2 + (y - b)^2 + (z - c)^2 = x^2 + y^2 + z^2 - 2ax - 2by - 2cz + (a^2 + b^2 + c^2 - r^2)$

Key Takeaways

Standard Form: $(x - a)^2 + (y - b)^2 + (z - c)^2 = r^2$ represents a sphere centered at (a,b,c) with radius r.
Origin Center: When center is (0,0,0), equation simplifies to $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = r^2$.
Geometric Meaning: Set of all points equidistant from a fixed center point.
Completing Square: Used to convert general quadratic equation to standard sphere form.
Applications: Physics (atoms, planets), computer graphics, GPS, and 3D modeling.
Surface Area: $4\pi r^2$, Volume: $\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$ for a sphere of radius r.
