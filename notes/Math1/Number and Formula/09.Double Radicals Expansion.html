Double Radicals Expansion
Let $a > 0$, $b > 0$.
1 $ \sqrt{(a+b) + 2\sqrt{ab}} $ = $ \sqrt{a} + \sqrt{b} $
2 $ a > b $   then   $ \sqrt{(a+b) - 2\sqrt{ab}} $ = $ \sqrt{a} - \sqrt{b} $
💡 Tip: These forms are useful for simplifying expressions with double radicals.
Try to express the answers in the form $|\sqrt{a} \pm \sqrt{b}|$.
