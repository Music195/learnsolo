Negation of "And" and "Or"

Mathematical Foundations

The negation of compound statements follows specific rules. When negating "and" and "or" statements, the logical operators change according to De Morgan's laws. These rules are fundamental to logical reasoning and mathematical proofs.

Negation of "And"

$\neg(p \land q) \equiv \neg p \lor \neg q$

The negation of "p and q" is "not p or not q"

Example: ¬(A ∧ B) = ¬A ∨ ¬B

Logic: If it's not true that both are true, then at least one is false

Negation of "Or"

$\neg(p \lor q) \equiv \neg p \land \neg q$

The negation of "p or q" is "not p and not q"

Example: ¬(A ∨ B) = ¬A ∧ ¬B

Logic: If it's not true that at least one is true, then both are false

Word Form

¬"p and q" = "not p or not q"

¬"p or q" = "not p and not q"

Essential for negating complex statements

Foundation of logical reasoning

Set Theory Equivalent

¬(A ∩ B) = ¬A ∪ ¬B

¬(A ∪ B) = ¬A ∩ ¬B

Same laws apply to sets

Complement of intersection/union

Worked Examples

Example 1: Negation of "And"

Statement: "x > 2 and x < 8"

Let P: x > 2, Q: x < 8

Original: P ∧ Q

Negation: ¬(P ∧ Q) ≡ ¬P ∨ ¬Q

¬(x > 2 ∧ x < 8) ≡ ¬(x > 2) ∨ ¬(x < 8)

≡ x ≤ 2 ∨ x ≥ 8

The solution is x ≤ 2 or x ≥ 8

Example 2: Negation of "Or"

Statement: "x = 3 or x = 5"

Let P: x = 3, Q: x = 5

Original: P ∨ Q

Negation: ¬(P ∨ Q) ≡ ¬P ∧ ¬Q

¬(x = 3 ∨ x = 5) ≡ ¬(x = 3) ∧ ¬(x = 5)

≡ x ≠ 3 ∧ x ≠ 5

The solution is x ≠ 3 and x ≠ 5

Example 3: Complex Statement

Statement: "The number is even and greater than 10"

Let P: number is even, Q: number > 10

Negation: ¬(P ∧ Q) ≡ ¬P ∨ ¬Q

≡ "number is odd or number ≤ 10"

This shows how the "and" becomes "or" when negated

Example 4: Set Theory Application

Statement: x ∈ A ∩ B (x is in A and B)

Negation: x ∉ A ∩ B

Using De Morgan: x ∉ A ∩ B ≡ x ∉ A ∨ x ∉ B

≡ x ∈ Ā ∪ B̄

Same principle applies to set operations

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Negate the statement: "x ≥ 5 and x ≤ 15"

Let P: x ≥ 5, Q: x ≤ 15

¬(P ∧ Q) ≡ ¬P ∨ ¬Q

¬(x ≥ 5 ∧ x ≤ 15) ≡ ¬(x ≥ 5) ∨ ¬(x ≤ 15)

≡ x < 5 ∨ x > 15

The solution is x < 5 or x > 15

Problem 2

Negate the statement: "The triangle is equilateral or isosceles"

Let P: triangle is equilateral, Q: triangle is isosceles

¬(P ∨ Q) ≡ ¬P ∧ ¬Q

≡ "triangle is not equilateral and not isosceles"

≡ "triangle is scalene"

The triangle is scalene

Problem 3

Write the negation of: "x ∈ A ∪ B"

¬(x ∈ A ∪ B) ≡ x ∉ A ∪ B

Using De Morgan: x ∉ A ∪ B ≡ x ∉ A ∧ x ∉ B

≡ x ∈ Ā ∩ B̄

The element is in neither A nor B

Problem 4

Negate: "The student passed math and passed science"

Let P: passed math, Q: passed science

¬(P ∧ Q) ≡ ¬P ∨ ¬Q

≡ "failed math or failed science"

The student failed math or failed science (or both)

Key Takeaways

  • Negation of AND: ¬(P ∧ Q) ≡ ¬P ∨ ¬Q
  • Negation of OR: ¬(P ∨ Q) ≡ ¬P ∧ ¬Q
  • Word rule: "Not (A and B)" = "Not A or Not B"
  • Word rule: "Not (A or B)" = "Not A and Not B"
  • Set theory: Same rules apply with ∩, ∪, and complements
  • Truth tables: Verify equivalence with truth tables
  • Essential for: Logical reasoning, mathematical proofs, programming
  • Common mistake: Forgetting to switch AND ↔ OR when negating
$\overline{p \text{ and } q}$ $\Leftrightarrow$ $\overline{p}$ or $\overline{q}$
$\overline{p \text{ or } q}$ $\Leftrightarrow$ $\overline{p}$ and $\overline{q}$
