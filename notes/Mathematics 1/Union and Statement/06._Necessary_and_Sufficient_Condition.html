Mathematical Foundations

Necessary and sufficient conditions are fundamental concepts in logic and mathematics. They describe the relationship between two statements and help us understand when one condition guarantees another.

Sufficient Condition

$p \Rightarrow q$

If p is true, then q must be true

p guarantees q

Example: "Being a square" is sufficient for "being a rectangle"

Necessary Condition

$p \Rightarrow q$

q must be true for p to be true

p requires q

Example: "Being a rectangle" is necessary for "being a square"

Logical equivalence

Example: "If not a rectangle, then not a square"
