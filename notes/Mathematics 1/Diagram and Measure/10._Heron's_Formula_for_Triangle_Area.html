Heron's Formula for Triangle Area

Mathematical Foundation

Heron's formula, named after the ancient Greek mathematician Hero of Alexandria, provides a way to calculate the area of any triangle when all three side lengths are known. Unlike other area formulas that require height or angle information, Heron's formula depends only on the side lengths, making it extremely useful for solving geometry problems where only distances are given.

Heron's Formula

Area = √[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)]

Where:

  • s is the semiperimeter: s = (a + b + c)/2
  • a, b, c are the side lengths of the triangle

Alternative forms:

  • Area = (1/4)√[(a+b+c)(b+c-a)(c+a-b)(a+b-c)]
  • Area = (1/4)√[2(a²b² + b²c² + c²a²) - (a⁴ + b⁴ + c⁴)]

Derivation: From Base-Height Formula

Consider a triangle with sides a, b, c and height h from vertex A to base a:

  • Area = (1/2) × base × height = (1/2) × a × h
  • By Pythagorean theorem: b² = h² + (s-a)² and c² = h² + (s-b)²
  • Subtracting: b² - c² = (s-a)² - (s-b)² = (s-a - s + b)(s-a + s - b) = (b-a)(2s - a - b)
  • But 2s - a - b = c, so b² - c² = (b-a)c
  • From b² = h² + (s-a)²: h² = b² - (s-a)²
  • h² = [s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)] × 16 / a²
  • Area = (1/2)a × h = (1/2)a × √[16s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)/a²] = √[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)]

Geometric Interpretation

The formula can be understood geometrically by considering the area as composed of three smaller triangles formed by dropping perpendiculars from the vertices to the opposite sides. Each small triangle has area equal to the product of the semiperimeter differences and the height.

The key insight is that the height h satisfies: h² = [16s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)] / a²

This leads directly to the area formula when we substitute into the base-height formula.

Worked Example 1: Basic Calculation

Find the area of a triangle with sides 3 cm, 4 cm, 5 cm.

Solution:

Semiperimeter s = (3 + 4 + 5)/2 = 6 cm

Area = √[6(6-3)(6-4)(6-5)] = √[6×3×2×1] = √[36] = 6 cm²

Worked Example 2: Decimal Sides

Find the area of a triangle with sides 7.2 cm, 8.5 cm, 9.1 cm.

Solution:

s = (7.2 + 8.5 + 9.1)/2 = 12.4 cm

Area = √[12.4(12.4-7.2)(12.4-8.5)(12.4-9.1)] = √[12.4×5.2×3.9×3.3]

Area = √[12.4×5.2×3.9×3.3] ≈ √[838.416] ≈ 28.96 cm²

Worked Example 3: Large Triangle

Find the area of a triangle with sides 13 cm, 14 cm, 15 cm.

Solution:

s = (13 + 14 + 15)/2 = 21 cm

Area = √[21(21-13)(21-14)(21-15)] = √[21×8×7×6] = √[7056] ≈ 84 cm²

Practice Problems

Problem 1: Find the area of a triangle with sides 5 cm, 6 cm, 7 cm.
Solution:
s = (5 + 6 + 7)/2 = 9 cm
Area = √[9(9-5)(9-6)(9-7)] = √[9×4×3×2] = √[216] ≈ 14.7 cm²
Problem 2: Find the area of a triangle with sides 8 cm, 10 cm, 12 cm.
Solution:
s = (8 + 10 + 12)/2 = 15 cm
Area = √[15(15-8)(15-10)(15-12)] = √[15×7×5×3] = √[1575] ≈ 39.7 cm²
Problem 3: Find the area of a triangle with sides 9 cm, 12 cm, 15 cm.
Solution:
s = (9 + 12 + 15)/2 = 18 cm
Area = √[18(18-9)(18-12)(18-15)] = √[18×9×6×3] = √[2916] = 54 cm²

Key Takeaways

  • Formula: Area = √[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)] where s = (a+b+c)/2
  • Semiperimeter: s is half the perimeter of the triangle
  • Triangle Inequality: Must satisfy a+b>c, a+c>b, b+c>a
  • Units: Area has units of length squared
  • Equilateral: For equilateral triangle: Area = (√3/4) × side²
  • Right Triangle: Reduces to (1/2)ab when c² = a² + b²
  • Applications: Surveying, navigation, engineering
  • Historical: Discovered by Hero of Alexandria (c. 10-70 AD)
  • Alternative Forms: Multiple algebraic equivalents exist
  • Geometric Meaning: Relates area to side lengths only
