Trigonometric Ratios of 90°−θ

Mathematical Foundation

The cofunction identities relate the trigonometric functions of complementary angles. Two angles are complementary if their sum is 90°. These identities are fundamental in trigonometry and have important applications in solving triangles and simplifying trigonometric expressions.

Sine Cofunction

sin(90° - θ) = cos θ
The sine of an angle equals the cosine of its complement

Cosine Cofunction

cos(90° - θ) = sin θ
The cosine of an angle equals the sine of its complement

Tangent Cofunction

tan(90° - θ) = 1/tan θ
The tangent of an angle equals the cotangent of its complement

Memory Tip: "Co" in Cofunction

The "co" in cosine, cosecant, and cotangent indicates they're cofunctions of sine, secant, and tangent respectively:

  • cosine is the cofunction of sine
  • cosecant is the cofunction of secant
  • cotangent is the cofunction of tangent

Visual Proof: Triangle Method

Angle θ

sin θ = a/c
cos θ = b/c
tan θ = a/b

Angle (90° - θ)

sin(90°-θ) = b/c
cos(90°-θ) = a/c
tan(90°-θ) = b/a

Conclusion: sin(90°-θ) = cos θ, cos(90°-θ) = sin θ, tan(90°-θ) = 1/tan θ

Extended Cofunction Identities

Secant:
sec(90° - θ) = csc θ
Cosecant:
csc(90° - θ) = sec θ
Cotangent:
cot(90° - θ) = tan θ

Worked Example 1

Find sin 20° using cofunction identities.

Solution:

sin 20° = cos(90° - 20°) = cos 70°

This shows that sin 20° = cos 70°

Worked Example 2

If cos θ = 0.6, find sin(90° - θ).

Solution:

cos(90° - θ) = sin θ

So sin(90° - θ) = cos θ = 0.6

Worked Example 3

Simplify: sin(90° - x) + cos(90° - x)

Solution:

sin(90° - x) = cos x

cos(90° - x) = sin x

So: cos x + sin x

Practice Problems

Problem 1: Express cos 25° in terms of sine.
Solution:
cos 25° = sin(90° - 25°) = sin 65°
Problem 2: If tan θ = 2, find tan(90° - θ).
Solution:
tan(90° - θ) = 1/tan θ = 1/2 = 0.5
Problem 3: Find the value of sin 40° + cos 50°.
Solution:
cos 50° = cos(90° - 40°) = sin 40°
So: sin 40° + sin 40° = 2 sin 40°
Problem 4: Simplify: cos(90° - α) - sin(90° - α)
Solution:
cos(90° - α) = sin α
sin(90° - α) = cos α
So: sin α - cos α
Problem 5: If sin θ = 0.8, find cos(90° - θ) without using a calculator.
Solution:
cos(90° - θ) = sin θ = 0.8

Key Takeaways

  • Complementary Angles: Two angles that sum to 90°
  • Sine-Cosine: sin(90° - θ) = cos θ and cos(90° - θ) = sin θ
  • Tangent-Cotangent: tan(90° - θ) = cot θ = 1/tan θ
  • Memory: "Co" functions are cofunctions of their counterparts
  • Visual Proof: Same triangle, different angle labels
  • Applications: Solving triangles, simplifying expressions
  • Extended: sec(90° - θ) = csc θ, csc(90° - θ) = sec θ
  • Unit Circle: Cofunctions relate to coordinate symmetry
  • Calculus: Important in derivatives and integrals
  • Problem Solving: Convert between sine and cosine functions
