Triangle Incircle Area Formula

Mathematical Foundation

The incircle of a triangle is the largest circle that can be inscribed inside the triangle, touching all three sides. The area of a triangle can be expressed in terms of its incircle radius using a remarkably simple formula. This relationship connects the triangle's area to its perimeter through the incircle, providing a powerful tool for solving geometric problems involving inscribed circles.

Triangle Area via Incircle

Area = r × s

Where:

  • r is the incircle radius
  • s is the semiperimeter: s = (a + b + c)/2
  • a, b, c are the side lengths

Alternative form: Area = (1/2) × r × (a + b + c)

Key Property: Tangent Segments

From any point on the incircle, the tangent segments to the sides are equal:

  • Tangent from A: s - a
  • Tangent from B: s - b
  • Tangent from C: s - c

This property is crucial for understanding the incircle's position and radius.

Derivation: Area Division Method

The triangle can be divided into three smaller triangles by drawing lines from each vertex to the tangent points:

  • Each small triangle has height r (incircle radius)
  • The bases of these small triangles are: s-a, s-b, s-c
  • Area of △ABO = (1/2) × (s-a) × r
  • Area of △BCO = (1/2) × (s-b) × r
  • Area of △CAO = (1/2) × (s-c) × r

Total Area:

Area = (1/2)r(s-a) + (1/2)r(s-b) + (1/2)r(s-c)

Area = (1/2)r[(s-a) + (s-b) + (s-c)]

Area = (1/2)r[s - a - b - c + 2s] Wait, let's recalculate...

Actually: (s-a) + (s-b) + (s-c) = 3s - (a+b+c)

But s = (a+b+c)/2, so 3s - (a+b+c) = 3s - 2s = s

Therefore: Area = (1/2)r × s ✓

Incircle Properties

  • Radius Formula: r = Area / s
  • Alternative Radius: r = √[(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)/s]
  • Distance from Vertex: Distance from vertex to incircle center
  • Angle Bisectors: Incircle center lies on all angle bisectors
  • Tangent Lengths: Tangents from vertex equal: s-a, s-b, s-c

Worked Example 1: Area from Radius

A triangle has sides 7 cm, 8 cm, 9 cm and incircle radius 2 cm. Find the area.

Solution:

Semiperimeter s = (7 + 8 + 9)/2 = 12 cm

Area = r × s = 2 × 12 = 24 cm²

Worked Example 2: Radius from Area

A triangle has area 30 cm² and sides 5 cm, 6 cm, 7 cm. Find the incircle radius.

Solution:

Semiperimeter s = (5 + 6 + 7)/2 = 9 cm

Radius r = Area / s = 30 / 9 ≈ 3.33 cm

Worked Example 3: Complete Solution

Find the incircle radius of a triangle with sides 13 cm, 14 cm, 15 cm.

Solution:

First find the area using Heron's formula:

s = (13 + 14 + 15)/2 = 21 cm

Area = √[21(21-13)(21-14)(21-15)] = √[21×8×7×6] = √[7056] ≈ 84 cm²

Radius r = Area / s = 84 / 21 = 4 cm

Practice Problems

Problem 1: A triangle has sides 6 cm, 8 cm, 10 cm and incircle radius 2 cm. Find the area.
Solution:
s = (6 + 8 + 10)/2 = 12 cm
Area = r × s = 2 × 12 = 24 cm²
Problem 2: A triangle has area 48 cm² and sides 9 cm, 12 cm, 15 cm. Find the incircle radius.
Solution:
s = (9 + 12 + 15)/2 = 18 cm
r = Area / s = 48 / 18 ≈ 2.67 cm
Problem 3: Find the area of the incircle of a triangle with sides 11 cm, 13 cm, 20 cm.
Solution:
First find area: s = (11+13+20)/2 = 22 cm
Area = √[22(22-11)(22-13)(22-20)] = √[22×11×9×2] = √[4356] ≈ 66 cm²
r = Area / s = 66 / 22 = 3 cm
Incircle area = πr² ≈ 3.14 × 9 ≈ 28.26 cm²

Key Takeaways

  • Area Formula: Area = r × s (where s is semiperimeter)
  • Alternative: Area = (1/2) × r × (a + b + c)
  • Radius Formula: r = Area / s
  • Tangent Property: Tangents from vertex are equal: s-a, s-b, s-c
  • Incircle Center: Lies at intersection of angle bisectors
  • Touch Points: Incircle touches all three sides
  • Distance to Sides: Distance from vertex to incircle
  • Angle Bisectors: Incircle center on all angle bisectors
  • Maximum Circle: Largest circle that fits inside triangle
  • Applications: Geometry problems, engineering, design
