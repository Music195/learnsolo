Law of Sines

Mathematical Foundation

The Law of Sines is a fundamental relationship between the sides and angles of any triangle. It states that the ratio of the length of a side to the sine of its opposite angle is constant for all sides and angles in the same triangle. This law is particularly useful for solving triangles when you know some combination of sides and angles.

Law of Sines

$\dfrac{a}{\sin A} = \dfrac{b}{\sin B} = \dfrac{c}{\sin C} = 2R$

Where:

  • a, b, c are the lengths of the sides
  • A, B, C are the measures of the opposite angles
  • R is the radius of the circumscribed circle (circumradius)

Derivation: Circumcircle Method

Derivation Steps:

  1. Draw the circumcircle with center O and radius R
  2. From center O, draw radii to each vertex
  3. In △AOB: sin A = a/(2R) → a = 2R sin A
  4. In △AOC: sin C = c/(2R) → c = 2R sin C
  5. In △BOC: sin B = b/(2R) → b = 2R sin B
  6. Therefore: a/(sin A) = b/(sin B) = c/(sin C) = 2R

Applications of the Law of Sines

  • Solving Triangles: Find unknown sides/angles when given some information
  • SSA Case: Two sides and non-included angle (may have ambiguous solutions)
  • AAS Case: Two angles and non-included side
  • Navigation: Distance and bearing calculations
  • Surveying: Height and distance measurements
  • Physics: Vector resolution and force analysis

The Ambiguous Case (SSA)

When given two sides and a non-included angle, there may be zero, one, or two possible triangles. This occurs when:

  • The given angle is acute
  • The side opposite the given angle is shorter than the other given side

Conditions:

  • No solution: If the side opposite the angle is too short
  • One solution: Right triangle or obtuse triangle case
  • Two solutions: Both triangles satisfy the conditions

Worked Example 1: AAS Case

In △ABC, ∠A = 35°, ∠B = 65°, and side c = 12 cm. Find the lengths of sides a and b.

Solution:

First, find ∠C: 180° - 35° - 65° = 80°

Using Law of Sines: a/sin A = c/sin C

a/sin 35° = 12/sin 80°

a = 12 × sin 35° / sin 80° ≈ 12 × 0.5736 / 0.9848 ≈ 6.98 cm

b/sin B = c/sin C

b = 12 × sin 65° / sin 80° ≈ 12 × 0.9063 / 0.9848 ≈ 11.04 cm

Worked Example 2: SSA Case (One Solution)

In △ABC, ∠A = 30°, side b = 10, side c = 15. Find the remaining sides and angle.

Solution:

Using Law of Sines: b/sin B = c/sin C

But we don't know B or C. Use: a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C

sin B = b × sin A / a

This requires iteration or using the formula for angle B.

Actually: sin B = (b × sin A) / a

But we need to find a first. Wait, let's use the ratio:

a/sin 30° = b/sin B = c/sin C

Let k = a/sin 30° = a/0.5 = 2a

Then: b = k × sin B, c = k × sin C

So: sin B = b/k = 10/k, sin C = 15/k

Also: B + C = 150°

This requires solving: sin B + sin C = sin(150° - B) + sin B = 2 sin 75° cos(75° - B)

Easier approach: Use calculator to find angles.

Worked Example 3: Area of Triangle

Find the area of △ABC where sides a = 8, b = 6, and ∠C = 40°.

Solution:

Area = (1/2) × a × b × sin C = (1/2) × 8 × 6 × sin 40°

= 24 × 0.6428 ≈ 15.43 square units

Note: This uses the Law of Sines indirectly through the area formula.

Practice Problems

Problem 1: In △ABC, ∠A = 50°, ∠B = 70°, side a = 10 cm. Find sides b and c.
Solution:
∠C = 180° - 50° - 70° = 60°
b/sin 70° = 10/sin 50°
b = 10 × sin 70° / sin 50° ≈ 10 × 0.9397 / 0.7648 ≈ 12.29 cm
c/sin 60° = 10/sin 50°
c = 10 × sin 60° / sin 50° ≈ 10 × 0.8660 / 0.7648 ≈ 11.32 cm
Problem 2: In △ABC, ∠C = 35°, side a = 12, side b = 8. Find ∠A and ∠B.
Solution:
sin A / 12 = sin B / 8 = sin 35° / c
First find c: c² = a² + b² - 2ab cos C = 144 + 64 - 2×12×8×cos 35°
cos 35° ≈ 0.8192
c² = 208 - 196.6 ≈ 11.4, c ≈ 3.38
Now: sin A / 12 = sin 35° / 3.38 ≈ 0.5736 / 3.38 ≈ 0.1697
sin A ≈ 12 × 0.1697 ≈ 2.036
∠A ≈ 117.3°
∠B = 180° - 35° - 117.3° = 27.7°
Problem 3: Find the area of △ABC where ∠A = 60°, ∠B = 80°, side c = 15.
Solution:
∠C = 40°
Area = (1/2) × a × b × sin C
First find a and b using Law of Sines
a/sin 60° = 15/sin 40°
a = 15 × sin 60° / sin 40° ≈ 15 × 0.8660 / 0.6428 ≈ 20.21
b = 15 × sin 80° / sin 40° ≈ 15 × 0.9848 / 0.6428 ≈ 22.98
Area = (1/2) × 20.21 × 22.98 × sin 40° ≈ (1/2) × 464.8 × 0.6428 ≈ 149.4

Key Takeaways

  • Formula: a/sin A = b/sin B = c/sin C = 2R
  • Derivation: Based on circumcircle and angle relationships
  • Applications: Solving triangles with various angle/side combinations
  • Ambiguous Case: SSA can have 0, 1, or 2 solutions
  • Triangle Inequality: Must be satisfied for valid triangles
  • Area Formula: Area = (1/2)ab sin C
  • Right Triangles: Reduces to a = c sin A, etc.
  • Units: Angles in degrees, sides in consistent units
  • Domain: Angles between 0° and 180°, sides positive
  • Verification: Sum of angles = 180°, triangle inequalities
