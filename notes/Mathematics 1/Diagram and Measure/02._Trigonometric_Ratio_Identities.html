Trigonometric Ratio Identities

Mathematical Foundation

Trigonometric identities are equations involving trigonometric functions that are true for all values of the variables involved. These identities are derived from the definitions of trigonometric ratios and the Pythagorean theorem. They form the foundation for solving trigonometric equations and simplifying complex trigonometric expressions.

1. Tangent Identity

tan θ = sin θ / cos θ
Derivation: tan θ = opposite/adjacent, sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse, cos θ = adjacent/hypotenuse
Therefore: tan θ = (opposite/hypotenuse) ÷ (adjacent/hypotenuse) = opposite/adjacent

2. Pythagorean Identity

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
Derivation: From Pythagoras: (adjacent)² + (opposite)² = (hypotenuse)²
Divide both sides by (hypotenuse)²: (adjacent/hypotenuse)² + (opposite/hypotenuse)² = 1
Therefore: cos²θ + sin²θ = 1

3. Secant Identity

1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
Derivation: Divide Pythagorean identity by cos²θ:
sin²θ/cos²θ + cos²θ/cos²θ = 1/cos²θ
tan²θ + 1 = sec²θ

Visual Proof: Pythagorean Identity

Consider a right triangle with angle θ:

Pythagoras: a² + b² = c²

Divide by c²: (a/c)² + (b/c)² = 1

Therefore: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

Additional Trigonometric Identities

Cosecant:
csc θ = 1/sin θ
Secant:
sec θ = 1/cos θ
Cotangent:
cot θ = 1/tan θ = cos θ/sin θ
Cosecant Identity:
1 + cot²θ = csc²θ

Worked Example 1: Using Pythagorean Identity

If sin θ = 3/5 and θ is in Quadrant I, find cos θ.

Solution:

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

(3/5)² + cos²θ = 1

9/25 + cos²θ = 1

cos²θ = 1 - 9/25 = 16/25

cos θ = 4/5 (since θ is in Quadrant I)

Worked Example 2: Using Tangent Identity

If cos θ = 12/13 and θ is in Quadrant I, find tan θ.

Solution:

tan θ = sin θ / cos θ

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

sin²θ + (12/13)² = 1

sin²θ = 1 - 144/169 = 25/169

sin θ = 5/13

tan θ = (5/13) / (12/13) = 5/12

Practice Problems

Problem 1: If sin θ = 4/5 and θ is acute, find cos θ and tan θ.
Solution:
sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
(4/5)² + cos²θ = 1
16/25 + cos²θ = 1
cos²θ = 9/25
cos θ = 3/5
tan θ = sin θ / cos θ = (4/5) / (3/5) = 4/3
Problem 2: Verify that 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ for θ = 45°.
Solution:
For θ = 45°: sin θ = cos θ = √2/2
tan θ = 1, sec θ = √2
Left side: 1 + tan²θ = 1 + 1² = 2
Right side: sec²θ = (√2)² = 2
✓ Verified: 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
Problem 3: If cos θ = 7/25 and θ is in Quadrant IV, find sin θ.
Solution:
sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
sin²θ + (7/25)² = 1
sin²θ = 1 - 49/625 = 576/625
sin θ = -24/25 (since θ is in Quadrant IV, sin is negative)
Problem 4: Simplify: (sin θ + cos θ)² + (sin θ - cos θ)²
Solution:
(sin θ + cos θ)² = sin²θ + 2 sin θ cos θ + cos²θ
(sin θ - cos θ)² = sin²θ - 2 sin θ cos θ + cos²θ
Sum = 2 sin²θ + 2 cos²θ = 2(sin²θ + cos²θ) = 2(1) = 2
Problem 5: Prove: (1 - sin θ)(1 + sin θ) = cos²θ
Solution:
(1 - sin θ)(1 + sin θ) = 1 - sin²θ = cos²θ
(Using Pythagorean identity: 1 - sin²θ = cos²θ)

Key Takeaways

  • Pythagorean Identity: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 (most fundamental)
  • Tangent Identity: tan θ = sin θ / cos θ
  • Secant Identity: 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
  • Cosecant Identity: 1 + cot²θ = csc²θ
  • Reciprocal Identities: csc θ = 1/sin θ, sec θ = 1/cos θ, cot θ = 1/tan θ
  • Derivation: All identities derived from Pythagoras and definitions
  • Verification: Identities hold for all angles where functions are defined
  • Simplification: Used to simplify complex trigonometric expressions
  • Solving Equations: Essential for solving trigonometric equations
  • Proofs: Foundation for proving other trigonometric theorems
