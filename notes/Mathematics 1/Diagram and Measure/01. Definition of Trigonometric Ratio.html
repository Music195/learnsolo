Definition of Trigonometric Ratios

Mathematical Foundation

Trigonometric ratios are defined as ratios of the sides of a right-angled triangle. These ratios relate the angles of a triangle to the lengths of its sides. The three primary trigonometric ratios are sine (sin), cosine (cos), and tangent (tan), each defined using the relationships between the hypotenuse and the other two sides relative to a given angle.

Memory Aid: SOHCAHTOA

SOHCAHTOA
Sine = Opposite ÷ Hypotenuse
Cosine = Adjacent ÷ Hypotenuse
Tangent = Opposite ÷ Adjacent

Sine (sin θ)

sin θ = opposite ÷ hypotenuse
Ratio of opposite side to hypotenuse

Cosine (cos θ)

cos θ = adjacent ÷ hypotenuse
Ratio of adjacent side to hypotenuse

Tangent (tan θ)

tan θ = opposite ÷ adjacent
Ratio of opposite side to adjacent side
Trigonometric Ratios:
\( \sin\theta = \dfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}} = \dfrac{y}{r} \)

\( \cos\theta = \dfrac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}} = \dfrac{x}{r} \)

\( \tan\theta = \dfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} = \dfrac{y}{x} = \dfrac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta} \)

Extension to Unit Circle

While trigonometric ratios are defined for right triangles, they can be extended to any angle using the unit circle. In the unit circle, the hypotenuse is always 1, so:

  • sin θ = y-coordinate on unit circle
  • cos θ = x-coordinate on unit circle
  • tan θ = sin θ / cos θ

Worked Example

In a right triangle with opposite side = 5 and hypotenuse = 13, find sin θ, cos θ, and tan θ.

Solution:

Using Pythagoras: adjacent = √(13² - 5²) = √(169 - 25) = √144 = 12

sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse = 5/13 = 0.3846

cos θ = adjacent/hypotenuse = 12/13 = 0.9231

tan θ = opposite/adjacent = 5/12 = 0.4167

Angle θ = tan⁻¹(5/12) ≈ 22.62°

Practice Problems

Problem 1: In a right triangle, opposite = 7, adjacent = 24. Find the trigonometric ratios.
Solution:
Hypotenuse = √(7² + 24²) = √(49 + 576) = √625 = 25
sin θ = 7/25 = 0.28
cos θ = 24/25 = 0.96
tan θ = 7/24 = 0.2917
Problem 2: If sin θ = 0.6 and θ is acute, find cos θ and tan θ.
Solution:
cos θ = √(1 - sin²θ) = √(1 - 0.36) = √0.64 = 0.8
tan θ = sin θ / cos θ = 0.6 / 0.8 = 0.75
Problem 3: Find the angle θ where tan θ = 1.
Solution:
θ = tan⁻¹(1) = 45°
Problem 4: In a 3-4-5 triangle, what are sin θ, cos θ, tan θ for the angle opposite the side of length 3?
Solution:
Opposite = 3, Adjacent = 4, Hypotenuse = 5
sin θ = 3/5 = 0.6
cos θ = 4/5 = 0.8
tan θ = 3/4 = 0.75
Problem 5: Why can't trigonometric ratios be greater than 1 in a right triangle?
Solution:
In a right triangle, the hypotenuse is always the longest side. Therefore, any side divided by the hypotenuse will always be ≤ 1. Only tangent can be greater than 1 since it doesn't involve the hypotenuse.

Key Takeaways

  • SOHCAHTOA: Memory aid for trigonometric ratios
  • Sine: Opposite over Hypotenuse (≤ 1)
  • Cosine: Adjacent over Hypotenuse (≤ 1)
  • Tangent: Opposite over Adjacent (can be > 1)
  • Right Triangle: All ratios defined for acute angles
  • Pythagorean Identity: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  • Tangent Identity: tan θ = sin θ / cos θ
  • Unit Circle: Extension beyond right triangles
  • Angle Measurement: Ratios determine angle size
  • Applications: Used in physics, engineering, navigation
