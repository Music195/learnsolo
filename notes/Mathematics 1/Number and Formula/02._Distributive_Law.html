Distributive Law

Mathematical Foundation

The distributive law is one of the fundamental properties of arithmetic and algebra. It describes how multiplication interacts with addition (and subtraction). This property allows us to "distribute" a factor across terms being added or subtracted, which is essential for expanding expressions, factoring, and solving equations. The distributive law is the basis for many algebraic techniques and is used extensively in mathematics.

The Distributive Laws

a(b + c) = ab + ac
Multiply a factor by a sum: distribute the factor to each term in the sum.
(a + b)c = ac + bc
Multiply a sum by a factor: distribute the factor to each term in the sum.
a(b - c) = ab - ac
The distributive law also works with subtraction.
(a - b)c = ac - bc
Subtracting terms can also be distributed.

Visual Representation

Consider a rectangle with width (a+b) and height c. The total area is c(a+b).

Geometrically, this area can be divided into two parts: a rectangle of area ac and another of area bc.

This visual approach shows why c(a+b) = ac + bc.

Worked Example 1: Basic Distribution

Expand: 2(3 + 4)

Solution:

2(3 + 4) = 2×3 + 2×4 = 6 + 8 = 14

Worked Example 2: Variable Distribution

Expand: 3x(x + 2)

Solution:

3x(x + 2) = 3x×x + 3x×2 = 3x² + 6x

Worked Example 3: With Subtraction

Expand: 5(2x - 3)

Solution:

5(2x - 3) = 5×2x - 5×3 = 10x - 15

Practice Problems

Problem 1: Expand 4(2 + 3)
Solution:
4(2 + 3) = 4×2 + 4×3 = 8 + 12 = 20
Problem 2: Expand 2x(x + 5)
Solution:
2x(x + 5) = 2x×x + 2x×5 = 2x² + 10x
Problem 3: Expand 3(4x - 2)
Solution:
3(4x - 2) = 3×4x - 3×2 = 12x - 6

Key Takeaways

  • Basic Law: a(b + c) = ab + ac - distribute multiplication over addition
  • Reverse: (a + b)c = ac + bc - same law, different arrangement
  • Subtraction: a(b - c) = ab - ac - works with subtraction too
  • Multiple Terms: a(b + c + d) = ab + ac + ad - extends to multiple terms
  • Variables: Applies to variables, numbers, and expressions
  • Factoring: Reverse process: ab + ac = a(b + c)
  • Geometric: Visual representation using area of rectangles
  • Foundation: Basis for expanding brackets and factoring
  • Operations: Essential for algebra, calculus, and equation solving
  • Commutative: Order doesn't matter: a(b+c) = (b+c)a
