Among infinite decimals, those in which the same sequence of digits repeats after a certain place are called repeating decimals .

Mathematical Foundation

Repeating decimals are decimal representations of rational numbers where a sequence of digits repeats infinitely. Every rational number can be expressed as either a terminating decimal or a repeating decimal. Understanding repeating decimals is crucial for converting between fractions and decimals, and for recognizing patterns in decimal representations of rational numbers.

Notation for Repeating Decimals

A bar over digits indicates which digits repeat:

  • 0.666... = 0.6Ì„ (single digit repeats)
  • 0.318181... = 0.3Ì„18Ì„ (two digits repeat)
  • 1.234234... = 1.2Ì„34Ì„ (three digits repeat)
  • 0.142857142857... = 0.142857Ì„ (six digits repeat)

The bar notation shows exactly which part repeats infinitely.

Types of Repeating Decimals

Pure Repeating Decimals
Repeating starts immediately after decimal point. Example: 0.333... = 0.3Ì„
Mixed Repeating Decimals
Some non-repeating digits followed by repeating digits. Example: 0.1666... = 0.16Ì„
Terminating Decimals
Finite decimal representation. Example: 0.25 = 1/4 (can be written as repeating with zeros)

Converting Repeating Decimals to Fractions

The key method is to multiply by a power of 10 to shift the repeating part:

  • Let x = repeating decimal
  • Multiply both sides by 10^k where k is the length of repeating sequence
  • Subtract the original equation to eliminate the repeating part
  • Solve for x as a fraction

Step-by-Step Conversion Method

For pure repeating decimals (0.abc...):
  1. Let x = 0.abc...
  2. Multiply by 10^k where k = number of repeating digits
  3. Subtract: (10^k Ã— x) - x = integer part
  4. Solve for x
For mixed repeating decimals (0.abcd...):
  1. Let x = 0.abcd...
  2. Multiply by 10^m where m = total digits before repeating
  3. Let y = resulting decimal
  4. Multiply y by 10^k where k = repeating digits
  5. Subtract equations to eliminate repeating part

Worked Example 1: Pure Repeating Decimal

Convert 0.3Ì„ to a fraction.

Solution:

Let x = 0.333...

Key Takeaways

  • Notation: 0.333... = 0.3Ì„, 0.142857... = 0.142857Ì„
  • Pure Repeating: Repeats from decimal point (0.abc...)
  • Mixed Repeating: Non-repeating digits then repeating (0.abcd...)
  • Conversion Method: Multiply by 10^k, subtract original equation
  • Power of 10: k equals length of repeating sequence
  • Rational Numbers: All repeating decimals are rational
  • Terminating: Can be written as repeating with trailing zeros
  • Patterns: 1/7 = 0.142857Ì„, 1/11 = 0.09Ì„, 1/3 = 0.3Ì„
  • Verification: Convert back to decimal to check accuracy
  • Simplification: Always simplify resulting fractions
