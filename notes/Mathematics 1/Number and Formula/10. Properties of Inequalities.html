Properties of Inequalities

Mathematical Foundations

Inequalities are mathematical statements that compare two expressions using symbols like < (less than), > (greater than), â‰¤ (less than or equal to), and â‰¥ (greater than or equal to). The properties of inequalities allow us to manipulate these statements while preserving their truth.

Basic Properties:

  1. Addition Property: Adding the same number to both sides preserves the inequality direction
  2. Subtraction Property: Subtracting the same number from both sides preserves the inequality direction
  3. Multiplication Property: Multiplying both sides by a positive number preserves the inequality direction
  4. Division Property: Dividing both sides by a positive number preserves the inequality direction
  5. Reversal Property: Multiplying or dividing by a negative number reverses the inequality direction

Worked Examples

Example 1: Addition Property

Given: $x + 2 < 7$

Subtract 2 from both sides: $x + 2 - 2 < 7 - 2$

Result: $x < 5$

Example 2: Multiplication by Negative Number

Given: $3x > 12$

Divide both sides by 3: $\dfrac{3x}{3} > \dfrac{12}{3}$

Result: $x > 4$

Example 3: Reversal with Negative Multiplication

Given: $-2x < 8$

Divide both sides by -2 (reverse inequality): $\dfrac{-2x}{-2} > \dfrac{8}{-2}$

Result: $x > -4$

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Solve: $x - 3 \leq 5$

Add 3 to both sides: $x - 3 + 3 \leq 5 + 3$

$x \leq 8$

Problem 2

Solve: $-4x > 20$

Divide both sides by -4 (reverse inequality): $\dfrac{-4x}{-4} < \dfrac{20}{-4}$

$x < -5$

Problem 3

Solve: $\dfrac{x}{2} \geq -3$

Multiply both sides by 2: $\dfrac{x}{2} \times 2 \geq -3 \times 2$

$x \geq -6$

Key Takeaways

  • When adding or subtracting the same number from both sides of an inequality, the inequality direction remains the same.
  • When multiplying or dividing both sides by a positive number, the inequality direction remains the same.
  • When multiplying or dividing both sides by a negative number, the inequality direction reverses.
  • Always be careful with the inequality direction when performing operations, especially with negative numbers.
  • These properties are fundamental for solving linear inequalities and systems of inequalities.
