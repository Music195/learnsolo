Calculation with Radicals
When $a > 0$ , $b > 0$ and $k > 0$ ,
1. $ \sqrt{a} \sqrt{b} $ = $ \sqrt{ab} $
2. $ \dfrac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}} $ = $ \sqrt{\dfrac{a}{b}} $
3. $ \sqrt{k^2 a} $ = $ k\sqrt{a} $

Mathematical Foundation

Radical expressions involve square roots and other root operations. The properties of radicals allow us to simplify, multiply, divide, and rationalize expressions containing radicals. These operations are fundamental in algebra and are essential for solving equations and simplifying complex expressions.

Key Properties of Radicals

Product Property
$\sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b} = \sqrt{a \cdot b}$ for a > 0, b > 0. The square root of a product equals the product of the square roots.
Quotient Property
$\frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}} = \sqrt{\frac{a}{b}}$ for a > 0, b > 0. The square root of a quotient equals the quotient of the square roots.
Simplification Property
$\sqrt{k^2 a} = k\sqrt{a}$ for k > 0, a > 0. Perfect squares can be factored out of the radical.
Rationalization
Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate to eliminate radicals from the denominator.

Simplifying Radicals

To simplify $\sqrt{n}$, find the largest perfect square that divides n:

  • Factor n into perfect square × remaining factor
  • Take square root of perfect square
  • Leave remaining factor under the radical
  • Example: $\sqrt{18} = \sqrt{9 \times 2} = \sqrt{9} \times \sqrt{2} = 3\sqrt{2}$

Worked Example 1: Simplifying Radicals

Simplify $\sqrt{18}$ and $\sqrt{50}$.

Solution:

$\sqrt{18} = \sqrt{9 \times 2} = \sqrt{9} \times \sqrt{2} = 3\sqrt{2}$

$\sqrt{50} = \sqrt{25 \times 2} = \sqrt{25} \times \sqrt{2} = 5\sqrt{2}$

Worked Example 2: Multiplying Radicals

Calculate $\sqrt{3} \times \sqrt{5}$ and $2\sqrt{6} \times 3\sqrt{2}$.

Solution:

$\sqrt{3} \times \sqrt{5} = \sqrt{3 \times 5} = \sqrt{15}$

$2\sqrt{6} \times 3\sqrt{2} = (2 \times 3) \times \sqrt{6 \times 2} = 6\sqrt{12} = 6\sqrt{4 \times 3} = 6 \times 2\sqrt{3} = 12\sqrt{3}$

Worked Example 3: Dividing Radicals

Calculate $\frac{\sqrt{20}}{\sqrt{5}}$ and $\frac{4\sqrt{18}}{2\sqrt{3}}$.

Solution:

$\frac{\sqrt{20}}{\sqrt{5}} = \sqrt{\frac{20}{5}} = \sqrt{4} = 2$

$\frac{4\sqrt{18}}{2\sqrt{3}} = \frac{4}{2} \times \sqrt{\frac{18}{3}} = 2 \times \sqrt{6} = 2\sqrt{6}$

Practice Problems

Problem 1: Simplify $\sqrt{8}$ and $\sqrt{32}$.
Solution:
$\sqrt{8} = \sqrt{4 \times 2} = 2\sqrt{2}$
$\sqrt{32} = \sqrt{16 \times 2} = 4\sqrt{2}$
Problem 2: Calculate $\sqrt{2} \times \sqrt{8}$ and $\sqrt{12} \times \sqrt{3}$.
Solution:
$\sqrt{2} \times \sqrt{8} = \sqrt{2 \times 8} = \sqrt{16} = 4$
$\sqrt{12} \times \sqrt{3} = \sqrt{12 \times 3} = \sqrt{36} = 6$
Problem 3: Calculate $\frac{\sqrt{18}}{\sqrt{2}}$ and $\frac{\sqrt{50}}{\sqrt{25}}$.
Solution:
$\frac{\sqrt{18}}{\sqrt{2}} = \sqrt{\frac{18}{2}} = \sqrt{9} = 3$
$\frac{\sqrt{50}}{\sqrt{25}} = \sqrt{\frac{50}{25}} = \sqrt{2}$
Problem 4: Rationalize $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$ and $\frac{3}{\sqrt{5}}$.
Solution:
$\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \times \frac{\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$
$\frac{3}{\sqrt{5}} \times \frac{\sqrt{5}}{\sqrt{5}} = \frac{3\sqrt{5}}{5}$
Problem 5: Simplify $\sqrt{4 \times 18}$ using radical properties.
Solution:
$\sqrt{4 \times 18} = \sqrt{4} \times \sqrt{18} = 2 \times \sqrt{9 \times 2} = 2 \times 3\sqrt{2} = 6\sqrt{2}$
Or: $\sqrt{72} = \sqrt{36 \times 2} = 6\sqrt{2}$

Key Takeaways

  • Product Rule: $\sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b} = \sqrt{ab}$
  • Quotient Rule: $\frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}} = \sqrt{\frac{a}{b}}$
  • Simplification: $\sqrt{k^2 a} = k\sqrt{a}$
  • Perfect Squares: Factor out largest perfect square
  • Rationalization: Multiply by conjugate to eliminate denominator radicals
  • Coefficients: Multiply coefficients separately from radicals
  • Domain: All numbers must be positive for these properties
  • Verification: Square the result to check correctness
  • Combining: Like radicals can be added/subtracted
  • Complex Cases: May need multiple simplification steps
