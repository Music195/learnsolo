Quadratic Equation: Discriminant and Real Solutions

Mathematical Foundations

The discriminant is a crucial concept in quadratic equations. It determines the nature and number of solutions without actually solving the equation. The discriminant appears under the square root in the quadratic formula and reveals important information about the equation's solutions.

Discriminant Formula:

For $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$: $D = b^2 - 4ac$

The discriminant tells us about the roots of the quadratic equation.

$D > 0$: Two Distinct Real Solutions

The parabola crosses the x-axis at two different points.

Example: $x^2 - 3x - 4 = 0$

$D = 9 - 4(1)(-4) = 25 > 0$

Solutions: $x = 4$ and $x = -1$

$D = 0$: One Repeated Real Solution

The parabola touches the x-axis at exactly one point.

Example: $x^2 - 6x + 9 = 0$

$D = 36 - 36 = 0$

Solution: $x = 3$ (multiplicity 2)

$D < 0$: No Real Solutions

The parabola never crosses the x-axis.

Example: $x^2 + 2x + 5 = 0$

$D = 4 - 20 = -16 < 0$

Solutions: $-1 ± 2i$ (complex)

Discriminant: $D = (-3)^2 - 4(1)(-4) = 9 + 16 = 25$

Since $D = 25 > 0$, there are two distinct real solutions.

Solutions: $x = \dfrac{3 \pm \sqrt{25}}{2} = \dfrac{3 \pm 5}{2}$

$x = 4$ or $x = -1$

Worked Examples

Example 1: Two Real Solutions

Analyze: $2x^2 - 5x + 2 = 0$

$D = (-5)^2 - 4(2)(2) = 25 - 16 = 9 > 0$

Two distinct real solutions exist.

Solutions: $x = \dfrac{5 \pm \sqrt{9}}{4} = \dfrac{5 \pm 3}{4}$

$x = 2$ or $x = 0.5$

Example 2: Repeated Solution

Analyze: $x^2 + 4x + 4 = 0$

$D = 4^2 - 4(1)(4) = 16 - 16 = 0$

One repeated real solution.

Solution: $x = \dfrac{-4 \pm \sqrt{0}}{2} = \dfrac{-4}{2} = -2$

Example 3: No Real Solutions

Analyze: $x^2 - 2x + 3 = 0$

$D = (-2)^2 - 4(1)(3) = 4 - 12 = -8 < 0$

No real solutions (complex solutions exist).

Solutions: $x = \dfrac{2 \pm \sqrt{-8}}{2} = \dfrac{2 \pm 2\sqrt{2}i}{2} = 1 \pm \sqrt{2}i$

Practice Problems

Problem 1

Determine the number of real solutions for $x^2 - 7x + 12 = 0$.

$D = 49 - 48 = 1 > 0$ → Two distinct real solutions

Problem 2

Determine the number of real solutions for $4x^2 - 4x + 1 = 0$.

$D = 16 - 16 = 0$ → One repeated real solution

Problem 3

Determine the number of real solutions for $x^2 + x + 1 = 0$.

$D = 1 - 4 = -3 < 0$ → No real solutions

Key Takeaways

  • The discriminant $D = b^2 - 4ac$ determines the nature of quadratic equation solutions
  • $D > 0$: Two distinct real solutions
  • $D = 0$: One repeated real solution (double root)
  • $D < 0$: No real solutions (two complex conjugate solutions)
  • The discriminant is always a real number
  • $D ≥ 0$ means at least one real solution exists
  • The sign of the discriminant reveals whether the parabola crosses, touches, or misses the x-axis
For the quadratic equation $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$, the discriminant is defined as:
$D = b^2 - 4ac$
  • $D > 0$ ↔   There are two distinct real solutions.
  • $D = 0$ ↔   There is exactly one real solution (a repeated root).
  • $D < 0$ ↔   There are no real solutions.
In particular, $D \geq 0$ ↔ "There is at least one real solution."
