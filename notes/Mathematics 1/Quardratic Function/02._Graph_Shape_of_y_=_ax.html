Graph Shape of $y = ax^2$

Mathematical Foundations

The simplest quadratic function is $y = ax^2$, where the coefficient $a$ determines the shape and orientation of the parabola. The parabola is the fundamental shape of quadratic functions and appears in many real-world applications.

When $a > 0$

  • Parabola opens upward (∪ shape)
  • Vertex is the minimum point
  • Function decreases then increases
  • Range: $[0, +\infty)$

When $a < 0$

  • Parabola opens downward (∩ shape)
  • Vertex is the maximum point
  • Function increases then decreases
  • Range: $(-\infty, 0]$
Key Properties:
  • Vertex: Always at the origin (0, 0)
  • Axis of Symmetry: The y-axis (x = 0)
  • Domain: All real numbers $(-\infty, +\infty)$
  • Width: Determined by |a| - larger |a| means narrower parabola

Worked Examples

Example 1: $y = 2x^2$

Since $a = 2 > 0$, the parabola opens upward.

The coefficient 2 makes the parabola narrower than $y = x^2$.

Vertex: (0, 0), Axis of symmetry: x = 0

Example 2: $y = -x^2$

Since $a = -1 < 0$, the parabola opens downward.

The vertex (0, 0) is now the maximum point.

As x moves away from 0, y decreases.

Example 3: $y = 0.5x^2$

Since $a = 0.5 > 0$, the parabola opens upward.

The coefficient 0.5 makes the parabola wider than $y = x^2$.

This represents slower growth compared to $y = x^2$.

Practice Problems

The parabola opens upward (since a = 3 > 0) and is narrower than $y = x^2$.

Problem 3

How does the graph of $y = 0.25x^2$ compare to $y = x^2$?

The graph of $y = 0.25x^2$ is wider than $y = x^2$ because |a| = 0.25 < 1.

Key Takeaways

  • The sign of coefficient $a$ determines the direction the parabola opens
  • $a > 0$: opens upward, vertex is minimum point
  • $a < 0$: opens downward, vertex is maximum point
  • The absolute value |a| determines the width - larger |a| means narrower parabola
  • All graphs of $y = ax^2$ pass through the origin (0, 0)
  • The axis of symmetry is always the y-axis (x = 0)
